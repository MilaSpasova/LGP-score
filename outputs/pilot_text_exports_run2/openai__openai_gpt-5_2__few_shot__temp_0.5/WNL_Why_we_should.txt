James Hamblin is a senior editor at the American magazine The Atlantic. In his series “If Our Bodies Could Talk,” he tried a challenge: he did not shower for a while. He took fewer showers, and when he did shower, he did not use shampoo or soap. He wanted to see what happens when people clean their bodies too much.

He found that many other people have learned the same thing. When we use a lot of soap and body wash, our skin tries harder to get back to normal. Showering can remove the skin’s natural oil and bacteria. Hot water and cleaning products can make this effect stronger.

After a shower, you may feel tight and dry. This is because much of your skin’s natural moisture is gone. Our skin also has millions of helpful bacteria, like our gut does. Frequent washing and scrubbing can kill these bacteria. When the bacteria come back, the ones that cause bad smell may grow more. So showering too often may make you smell more. If you stop showering and using soap, your skin may have a first adjustment period. After that, the skin often returns to balance, makes less oil, and healthy bacteria grow again.

After the challenge, Hamblin felt the human body can be nice when it works on its own. Dermatologists also say using less soap can help acne and eczema. Taking fewer showers and using fewer products can also help the environment. The average shower is seven minutes and uses 65 litres of water. That is 65 litres of clean drinking water that goes down the drain with soap every day, sometimes more than once.

Clean water is becoming more important, as California faces another summer of drought. It is clear that clean water is very valuable and may soon be limited. Also, many body wash bottles affect the environment. These are good reasons to let your body be more natural.

If this idea makes you feel uncomfortable, relax. Many people who use less cleaning still use deodorant when they need it. Everyone’s natural smell is different, and it can change with diet, hydration, and exercise. Also, washing your hands with soap is still important to help stop infectious diseases from spreading.

You do not need to stop all at once like Hamblin did. But between cleaning many times a day and never showering, there is a simple idea: reduce. Skip a few showers, use less soap, and let the helpful bacteria grow a little.
