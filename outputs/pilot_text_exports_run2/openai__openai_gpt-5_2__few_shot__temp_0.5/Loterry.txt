The lottery balls fall, and all six numbers match. Then it is time to buy an Audi, book a holiday in the US, and call an estate agent. An analysis says many lottery millionaires do this.

The UK National Lottery started in 1994. Since then, it has made 3,000 millionaires. Together they have won more than £8.5bn. The average win was £2.8m each. Oxford Economics says their money also helped create 3,780 more millionaires among their children, family, and friends.

Most winners (59%) stop working straight away. But 19% keep their job, and 31% do unpaid volunteer work. 98% of winners’ spending stayed in the UK. The study estimates that each winner’s spending supports six full-time jobs for one year.

Winners added almost £750m to GDP and produced more than £500m in tax for the Exchequer. Most money was spent on property. Winners spent £2.72bn on their main homes, and £170m on paying off debt and mortgages.

Keeping an income was important, so £2.125bn went into investments. Winners gave £1.17bn as gifts to family and friends. They spent £680m on cars and holidays.

Camelot, which runs the UK National Lottery, paid for the study to mark 3,000 winners. The study used research from 100 winners who won £1m or more. It found that the 3,000 winners bought 7,958 houses or flats in the UK, about 2.7 each, and spent £3.3bn. Most winners (82%) changed their main home, spending an average of £900,000.

Many also bought extra things for the home. Almost a third (29%) wanted a hot tub. 28% wanted a walk-in wardrobe. 24% chose a home behind electric gates. 22% had a games room, and 7% installed a snooker table.

Bigger homes need help. 30% hired a cleaner, and 24% hired a gardener. 5% hired a beautician.

Audi was the most popular car brand for 16% of winners. Range Rover and BMW were also popular (11% each), and Mercedes (10%) and Land Rover (5%) too. Winners spent £463m on 17,190 cars. The average price of their favourite car was £46,116.

Holidays were important too. Most winners (68%) chose five-star hotels overseas. The US was the top place to visit for 27%, then the Caribbean (9%). In the UK, caravan sales also increased. Over the past 18 years, 10% of millionaires bought a caravan, creating about £7.4m in sales.

Some winners (15%) started their own business. 9% helped other people start a business, and 6% invested in or bought other people’s businesses. Businesses started or supported by winners employ 3,195 people.

Andy Logan, the report writer, said a win affects many people. It changes the lives of friends and family. It also affects the UK economy because much of the money is spent in the UK. He said each win creates a ripple effect for this generation and often the next.
