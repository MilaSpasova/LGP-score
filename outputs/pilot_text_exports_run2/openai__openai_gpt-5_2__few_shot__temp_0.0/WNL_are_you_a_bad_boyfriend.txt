Dan Sullivan was late to breakfast with the Guardian. Police stopped him while he was riding his motorcycle.

Sullivan says he works too much. He misses dinner dates. He forgets to buy presents.

So the 27-year-old venture capitalist made a start-up to help: BetterBoyfriend.me. It sends a present to girlfriends and wives every month for about $70.

Sullivan works in finance. He has been testing the service quietly. About 350 boyfriends have signed up. They bring in about $17,000 each month. He says most are his Harvard and investor friends: venture capitalists, founders, and workers at companies like Apple, Google, and Facebook. Their girlfriends have been getting gifts from him for the last year.

Each month, members pick one gift from a list of seven. Gifts include chocolate, tea sets, and manicures. The package is sent to the boyfriend with no brand name and no label.

Sullivan tests ideas by buying Facebook ads to see how many people respond. BetterBoyfriend.me became popular. Sullivan said it solved a problem many people have.

BetterBoyfriend.me was not his first idea. For a few days, he thought about sharing dogs. He said he saw rich guys looking unhappy while walking dogs. He said a dog is only valuable 5% of the time. He thought about paying someone to watch the dogs so other people could rent them.

Another start-up is called InvisibleBoyfriend. It lets users create a fake social partner who is always available to talk. The result is a “boyfriend” who can text all day.

Founder Kyle Tabor started it to help users stop unwanted advances. But he found that some people got attached to their computer partners. He said many users want companionship through conversation.

For Sullivan and Tabor, a surprise was the real relationships they have formed with customers. Sullivan says he now feels like a kind of relationship helper for the boyfriends.

Sullivan says he made mistakes. At first, the packages had receipts with his name, Dan Sullivan. One boyfriend wrote to him and said, “Listen, dude, she’s not mad but Cynthia found out.”

About 50% of the women who get packages know Sullivan is involved. Sullivan says it is connected to age. He thinks that after you have been married for a while, you do not keep many secrets.

Over the year, Sullivan says he got to know the boyfriends well. They even sent a gift to a hospital delivery room. He said the important thing is to remember that his relationship is with the boyfriend.

When he first started the company, he put tags that said BetterBoyfriend.me on flower bouquets. He went to Dolores Park, a popular place in the Mission District.

He looked for couples and gave the flowers to the woman. The boyfriend did not like it. So Sullivan changed his plan and started giving the flowers to the boyfriends.
