From glow-in-the-dark trees to bike sheds under the ground and solar-powered bins, people are trying unusual ideas to make cities nicer places to live.

Living in a city can be good, but it is harder to live in a way that is good for the environment. People deal with pollution, traffic, and fewer green spaces every day. Inventors are trying new ideas to help.

Pop-up parks
Some cities seem built more for cars than for people. A pop-up park is a simple idea. You take an empty parking space, a little money, and a plant or two, and you make a small park. The PARK(ing) project started as an art experiment in San Francisco and then spread around the world. People have also made temporary city farms and ecology demonstrations. The movement has a day of action every September.

Subterranean storage
Not everyone in cities uses a car. Bikes are more popular now. But where can you keep a bike safe? A Tokyo engineering company called Giken made an idea for an “eco-cycle, anti-seismic underground bicycle park.” It is a cylinder about seven metres wide and it goes deep into the ground. It can hold 204 bikes. Bike owners can get their bike back by pressing a button, and the system brings it up in about 13 seconds.

Glow-in-the-dark trees
Many people think of Christmas decorations when they think of things that glow. But Daan Roosegaarde, a Dutch designer-artist, created a “bioluminescent” plant. This experimental technology mixes DNA from glowing marine bacteria with part of a plant to make a glow like a jellyfish. Trials are happening to make a larger, industrial version. Roosegaarde hopes it could one day replace normal street lights.

Footfall harvesting
In east London, in West Ham, many commuters and shoppers walk on a raised walkway near the underground station every day. Most people do not notice that the ground feels a little springy. The rubber surface bends about five millimetres. Under the floor are smart tiles that capture energy from people’s footsteps and turn it into electricity. This can power the streetlights above. Pavegen, the UK company behind this idea, also installed a similar system at London’s Heathrow Airport and in other places.

Supertrees
Singapore’s Gardens by the Bay has man-made trees called “supertrees.” They are up to 50 metres high and have a steel frame. Flowers and ferns grow on them. Their metal tops help take in and spread out heat. They can collect rainwater and help move air for two “climate-controlled biomes” (large conservatories) below. Eleven of the 18 trees also have solar panels on their “branches.”
