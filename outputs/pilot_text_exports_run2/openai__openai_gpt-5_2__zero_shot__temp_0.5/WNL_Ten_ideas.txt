From glow-in-the-dark trees to bike storage under the ground and solar-powered bins, people are trying new ideas to make cities nicer places to live.

Living in a city can be good, but it is often not good for the environment. People face pollution, traffic, and fewer green spaces every day. Inventors have come up with some unusual ideas to help.

Pop-up parks
Some cities seem built more for cars than for people. A pop-up park is a simple idea. You take an empty parking space, a little money, and one or two plants, and you turn it into a small park. The PARK(ing) project started as an art experiment in San Francisco and then spread around the world. People have also tried temporary city farms and ecology demonstrations. The movement has a special day of action every September.

Subterranean storage
Not everyone in the city uses a car. Many people ride bikes. But where can you keep a bike safe? A Tokyo engineering company called Giken made an underground bicycle park called an “eco-cycle, anti-seismic underground bicycle park.” It is a cylinder that is only seven metres wide. It goes deep into the ground and can hold 204 bikes. Owners can get their bike back by pushing a button. The system brings the bike above ground in about 13 seconds.

Glow-in-the-dark trees
When people think of things that glow on trees, they often think of Christmas decorations. But Dutch designer-artist Daan Roosegaarde made a “bioluminescent” plant. This experimental technology mixes DNA from glowing marine bacteria with parts of a plant to make it glow like a jellyfish. Trials are happening to make a large-scale version. Roosegaarde hopes it could one day replace normal street lights.

Footfall harvesting
In West Ham in east London, many commuters and shoppers walk on a raised walkway near the underground station every day. Most people do not notice that the ground feels a little springy. The rubber surface bends about five millimetres. The walkway has smart tiles that capture the energy from people’s footsteps and turn it into electricity. This electricity powers the streetlights above. The UK company Pavegen made this system. It has also put a similar system in London’s Heathrow Airport and other places around the world.

Supertrees
Singapore’s Gardens by the Bay has man-made trees called “supertrees.” They are steel-framed and can be up to 50 metres high. Flowers and ferns grow on them. Their metal tops help take in and spread out heat. They also collect rainwater and help provide air ventilation for two “climate-controlled biomes” below (large conservatories). Eleven of the 18 trees also have solar panels on their “branches.”
