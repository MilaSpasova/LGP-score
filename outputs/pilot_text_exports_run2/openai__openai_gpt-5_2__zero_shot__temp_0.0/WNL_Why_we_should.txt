James Hamblin is a senior editor at The Atlantic. He did a challenge where he did not shower much. This was for his series, “If Our Bodies Could Talk.” He wanted to see what happens when people clean their bodies too much. He took fewer showers and stopped using shampoo and soap.

He learned something many others have learned. When we use lots of soap and body wash, our skin tries harder to balance itself again. Showering can wash away the skin’s natural oil and bacteria. Hot water and cleaning products can wash away too much.

After a shower, your skin may feel very tight. This is because a lot of your skin’s moisture is gone. Skin also has millions of helpful bacteria, like the gut does. Frequent scrubbing and soap can kill these bacteria. When new bacteria grow back, they may cause more smell. So showering too often can sometimes make you smell worse. When you stop showering and using soap, there can be an early period that feels unpleasant. After that, the skin often balances again, makes less oil, and healthy bacteria grow.

After his challenge, Hamblin felt the human body can be nice on its own. Dermatologists also say using less soap can help acne and eczema. Showering less can also help the environment. The average shower lasts seven minutes and uses 65 litres of water. That is 65 litres of clean, drinkable water mixed with soap and sent down the drain every day, and sometimes more than once a day.

Clean water is very important, and it is becoming harder to ignore this, as California has another summer of drought. Clean water is one of the world’s most valuable resources, and it may soon be in short supply. There is also waste from body wash bottles. These are reasons to let your body be more natural.

If this idea makes you feel nervous, relax. Many people who shower less still use deodorant when needed. Everyone’s natural smell is different, and it can change with diet, hydration, and exercise. Also, washing hands with soap is still recommended to help stop infectious diseases from spreading.

You do not have to stop showering all at once like Hamblin. But you can try a simple idea: reduce. Skip a few showers, use less soap, and let the helpful bacteria grow a little.
