JMW Turner, one of Britain’s greatest painters, will be on the new £20 note after a nationwide vote.

This is the first time an artist has been on a British banknote. The governor of the Bank of England, Mark Carney, asked people to name a dead cultural figure who should get this honour.

Turner is famous for dramatic paintings of the sea. He beat 590 other people who were suggested, including painters, sculptors, fashion designers, photographers, film-makers, and actors. About 30,000 members of the public sent in names.

The long list included Alfred Hitchcock, Alexander McQueen, Derek Jarman, Laura Ashley, William Morris, and Vanessa Bell. A group of artists, critics, and historians cut the list down to five.

The final five were Barbara Hepworth, Charlie Chaplin, Josiah Wedgwood, William Hogarth, and Turner. They were chosen because they made a clear contribution to visual arts and British society, and because their influence has lasted.

The new banknote was announced at the Turner Contemporary gallery in Margate. The gallery is on the place where Mrs Booth’s lodging house used to be, where Turner always stayed when he visited. Carney and the artist Tracey Emin, who grew up in Margate, made the announcement together.

Carney said it was important to have a proper process that involved the public. He said banknotes are not only practical, and that if done well, they can be “a piece of art in everyone’s pocket”. He also said, “Money is memory for a country and its people,” and that banknotes celebrate the UK’s heritage and culture.

Emin said that having Turner on the £20 note shows that British people value creativity and the arts.

The note will show Turner’s self-portrait from 1799, which is in Tate Britain. It will also show one of his most famous paintings, The Fighting Temeraire. This painting is about a ship that played an important part in Nelson’s victory at the Battle of Trafalgar in 1805.

The note will also have a quote from Turner, “light is therefore colour,” and his signature. The signature is taken from his will, where he left many of his works to the nation.

The new £20 note will replace the one with Adam Smith. It will start being used by 2020. This is the first time the public has been able to help choose whose face is on a British banknote.

Carney changed the process in 2013. This happened after a well-known campaign to keep women, apart from the Queen, on banknotes, after the Bank decided to stop using the Elizabeth Fry £5 note.

Historical people first appeared on banknotes in 1970. Turner will join Winston Churchill and Jane Austen on new polymer notes, made from a plastic-type material. Churchill is on the £5 note and Austen is on the £10 note. Austen, the Pride and Prejudice author, is only the third woman to be on British money, after Florence Nightingale and Elizabeth Fry.

Turner was born in 1775 in London. His father was a barber. Turner started at the Royal Academy Schools when he was 14. In 1786 he was sent to Margate, and he began to love painting and drawing the north-east Kent coast. He went back there many times and painted some of his most dramatic oil paintings and watercolours. He called its skies “the loveliest in all Europe”.

Turner made a lot of art in his life: more than 550 oil paintings and 2,000 watercolours. A film about his life was made in 2014 by Mike Leigh, with Timothy Spall playing Turner.

Victoria Pomery, the director of Turner Contemporary, said the choice to honour Turner is extraordinary. She said the public vote shows Turner is the nation’s favourite artist, and it is an official sign of the great historical value of visual arts in the UK. She also said it reminds the UK to value creative people in the future and to invest to support the next great artists and innovators.
