This invention could be very useful, like Trevor Baylis’s wind-up radio in the 1990s before the internet. It is a cheap light that uses free power from gravity. It could help stop people in Africa and India from using dangerous kerosene lamps.

British designer Patrick Hunt tried to get money from banks and venture capital to start selling his invention. But it was hard. He said getting funding was slow and complicated, and people did not want to take a risk.

So he tried crowdfunding on a US website called Indiegogo, which had just opened in the UK. In five days, he reached his goal and raised £36,200. By the end of 2012, after 40 days, he had raised £400,000.

The LED light works with a dynamo. A 10kg bag of rocks is attached to the light. The bag is lifted to about 2m high. As it slowly falls to the ground, it makes enough power for 30 minutes of light.

Hunt is getting ready to make the light in China. He will test it again by sending 1,000 lights to Africa before making many more. He hopes to make millions.

Hunt is part of a new group of entrepreneurs who use crowdfunding to get money. Another new website is InvestingZone. It matches rich people with start-up entrepreneurs.

Indiegogo does not give shares. Instead, people who donate can get “perks” at different donation levels. People who helped fund Hunt’s light could feel good about helping poorer people, and they also got their own light.

Danae Ringelmann, a co-founder of Indiegogo, says the “gravity light” shows how crowdfunding can work well. She says it is fast and fair because it is not about knowing people at a bank or a venture capital company. It is about showing customers and fans that your idea is good, and getting them to support it.

She says it is still useful to test ideas even if they do not get enough funding. Then you find out sooner that the idea was not good.

Ringelmann lives in the US. She first worked as a Wall Street analyst. In 2008, she quit and used her skills to help friends in the arts raise money.

At first, the site focused on films. It launched at the Sundance Film Festival that year.

Five years later, the site was raising about $2m each week for new businesses that are starting or growing. In December 2012, it started services in euros and pounds to grow in Europe. It says Britain is its third biggest market. International activity has gone up 41% since December.

There are many competing sites, like Kickstarter, Seedrs, and Funding Circle. But Ringelmann says Indiegogo is different because anyone can start a campaign. No project is too strange.

Indiegogo charges a 4% fee for successful campaigns. If a campaign does not reach its target, users can choose to refund all money with no charge, or keep the money raised but pay a 9% fee.

A British woman, Lauren Pears, raised £100,000 on the site to open a “cat café” in London. It is called Lady Dinah’s Cat Emporium. It has not opened yet, but it is described as a place where people can sit in a comfortable wingback chair, drink hot tea, read a book, and spend time with a cat.

Ringelmann says some campaigns are rejected by venture capitalists. The investors say they do not know if people really want the product. But an Indiegogo campaign can show proof that there is a market. Even the campaign itself can be enough proof.

She says crowdfunding helps you test your market, price, and features, and get important feedback.

Ringelmann says ideas are common. What matters is doing the work to make them real. She says if you are afraid someone will steal your idea and do it better and faster, then you may not be the right person to do it. She says you need confidence to move fast and learn.

She says expanding into Europe and working with a web transaction provider is part of a dream to make finance more open to everyone. This will allow payments through local card services like Maestro in the UK and Carte Bleue in France, as well as PayPal. More than 7,000 finance campaigns are live on the site.

Crowdfunding has grown as a choice instead of banks, but it does not attract many big investors. Big investors usually want a stake in a business. This may start to change in the UK with the launch of InvestingZone.
