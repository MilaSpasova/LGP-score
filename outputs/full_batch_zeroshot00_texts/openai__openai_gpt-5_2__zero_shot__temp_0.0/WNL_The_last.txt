What is it like to see the very last of something? Sudan is the last male northern white rhino on Earth. He lives at Ol Pejeta Conservancy in Kenya with two female northern white rhinos. If Sudan does not have a baby with one of them soon, then no more northern white rhinos will ever be born. Sudan is 42 years old, and attempts to help him breed have not worked. Besides these three, there are only two other northern white rhinos in the world. They are both females and live in zoos.

Sudan is guarded by armed men who stay close to him. This may look like kindness, but it is needed because of violence. Poachers still threaten him. Poachers kill rhinos and cut off their horns to sell them in the Asian medicine market, even though Sudan’s horn has been cut off to try to stop them.

Sudan does not know how rare he is. He walks around the reserve with his guards. His big head shows great strength, but he is still in danger. He seems like an old fighter who is tired and worn down.

Sudan’s body is huge, and his legs look short and strong under him. People have been amazed by rhinos for a very long time. Sudan looks a lot like a rhinoceros that Albrecht Dürer drew in 1515. In 1515, a live Indian rhinoceros was sent by the ruler of Gujarat in India to the king of Portugal. The king then sent it to the Pope, but it died in a shipwreck on the way.

Humans often kill the animals they admire. During the Ice Age, people painted pictures of woolly rhinos in caves in France up to 30,000 years ago. These animals were strong and powerful, but they also seemed gentle. A woolly rhino in Chauvet Cave looks young and full of life. But the same people who painted these rhinos also helped to wipe them out. When the climate changed at the end of the last Ice Age, human spears probably helped finish them.

Today many people care about rhinos, but rhinos are still being killed in large numbers. The northern white rhino is the rarest African rhino. There are many more southern white rhinos and black rhinos. But in some countries, people want rhino horn as a traditional medicine. They believe it can cure many illnesses, from flu to cancer. This belief is causing more poaching. In South Africa, only 13 rhinos were killed by poachers in 2007. In 2014, 1,215 rhinos were killed for their horns. 2015 looks likely to be even worse.

The northern white rhino has been hunted almost to extinction, even with guards and guns. Other African rhinos are also under heavy attack from poachers. The Javan rhinoceros is close to extinction too. India has protected the Indian rhinoceros after it was almost wiped out by British hunters in colonial times, but poaching is still a danger. Rhinos are majestic animals, but humans keep destroying them. Have we learned nothing since the Ice Age?
