Vienna is the best city in the world to live in. Baghdad is the worst. London, Paris, and New York are not in the top 35. This is from an international study about quality of life.

In the 18th Mercer Quality of Life study, many German-speaking cities are near the top. Vienna is number one. Zurich, Munich, Dusseldorf, and Frankfurt are also in the top seven.

Paris fell ten places to 37th place. London is 39th. Mercer says this is mostly because Paris is more at risk of terrorist attacks.

The study looked at many things, like social and economic conditions, health, education, housing, and the environment. Big companies use this study to help decide where to work and how much to pay workers.

Helena Hartlauer, 32, was born in Vienna. She said she is not surprised Vienna is number one. She said Vienna has a long tradition of building good social housing. This makes Vienna cheaper to live in than many big cities.

Hartlauer said she lives in a 100 square metre old apartment in a good area, about 20 minutes’ walk from the city centre. Her rent is 800 euros (£625) a month. She said a similar apartment in London would cost more than £2,000. It would cost even more in New York, which is 44th.

Cities in the US did not do very well in the study. Mercer says this is mostly because of personal safety and crime. The highest US city is San Francisco at 28th. Boston is 34th. Canadian cities did much better, led by Vancouver.

Hartlauer said Vienna feels very safe. She also said Vienna has very good public transport. The underground runs 24 hours on weekends. One trip costs 1 euro.

Vienna did very well after the Berlin Wall fell. It became an important place for travel and business with Eastern European countries.

Martin Eichtinger, the Austrian ambassador to London, lived in Vienna for 20 years. He said Vienna’s biggest strength is its location. He said the fall of the Berlin Wall helped make Vienna a center for companies that want to do business in Central Europe.

The World Bank says Austria has one of the highest GDP per head in the world. It is just behind the US and ahead of Germany and Britain. It is still below Switzerland.

Mercer says Zurich has the second highest quality of life. But people from Vienna say their city is more fun. Hartlauer said Vienna has more students than any other German-speaking city. She said it is growing fast and is young and lively. She also said she works for Vienna’s tourist board.

Many British tourists have not visited Vienna much. They often go to Barcelona or Berlin, and they often think of Austria as a place for skiing, lakes, and mountains.

But Vienna is becoming more popular with British visitors. There are more budget flights from cities like Manchester and Edinburgh. In 2015, 588,000 British people visited Vienna. This was 18% more than the year before. Eichtinger said London is now the number one city destination for Austrian visitors.

Mercer said Vienna has been number one in the last seven published rankings. Mercer said Vienna scores high because it is safe and stable, has good public services and transport, and has good places for free time.

Eichtinger said the European migrant crisis has had little effect on Vienna, a city of nearly 1.8 million people. He said Austria has taken in 90,000 refugees, but the numbers have slowed in recent months.

Mercer says London has never been in the top ten. London scores poorly for air pollution, traffic congestion, and climate. After London, Edinburgh is the next British city on the list, in 46th place.

Paris had the biggest fall in the newest rankings. Mercer said Paris had stayed stable for several years, but this year it dropped ten places. Mercer said the main reason was the terrorist attacks in 2015. Mercer also said safety is very important in the scoring, so even small changes can change the ranking a lot.

Auckland in New Zealand was the highest English-speaking city, in third place. Vancouver was fifth. Australian cities also did very well: Sydney was 10th and Melbourne was 15th. The Economist has often ranked Melbourne as the most liveable city, but some people say its survey focuses too much on English-speaking places.

War and political unrest are the main reasons for the worst-ranked cities. Damascus was only the seventh worst. It ranked better than Baghdad and also better than Bangui in Central African Republic, Sana’a in Yemen, Port-au-Prince in Haiti, Khartoum in Sudan, and N’Djamena in Chad.
