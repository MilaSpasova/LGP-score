The Duchess of Cambridge had a baby boy on Monday, 22 July. The baby is third in line to the throne. He is expected to be the 43rd monarch since William the Conqueror took the English crown in 1066.

Kensington Palace announced at 8.30pm that the baby was born at 4.24pm in the Lindo Wing at St Mary’s Hospital in Paddington, West London. The Duke of Cambridge said, “We could not be happier.”

Kensington Palace said the duchess gave birth safely at 4.24pm. The baby weighs 8lbs 6oz. The Duke of Cambridge was there for the birth. The palace said the labour lasted at least ten and a half hours and “progressed as normal.”

The Queen, the Duke of Edinburgh, the Prince of Wales, the Duchess of Cornwall, Prince Harry, and other family members were told. They were happy to hear that the duchess and the baby were doing well.

The couple waited before they made the news public so they could have some private time with the new baby. William called his family, including the Queen, his father Charles, and Prince Harry.

Because of this birth, the monarchy has three generations of heirs to the throne for the first time since 1894.

The baby is the first Prince of Cambridge born in more than 190 years, since Prince George of Cambridge, a grandson of George III and the only son of Prince Adolphus Frederick, the 1st Duke of Cambridge.

Prince Charles said he and his wife were very happy about their first grandchild. He said it was a special moment for William and Catherine, and they looked forward to seeing the baby soon.

The new royal will be called HRH Prince George of Cambridge. When Kate was three months pregnant, the Queen signed a decree that said the children of the eldest son of the Prince of Wales should have the title Royal Highness, and be called Prince or Princess.

A formal notice was put on an ornate rococo-style easel in front of Buckingham Palace shortly before 9pm. It was the same easel used to announce Prince William’s birth in 1982. Soon, the crowd outside grew from hundreds to thousands of local people and tourists.

A New Yorker, Sharon Surloff, was happy with the photo she took of the notice. She said the police told people they had 20 seconds each. She said she arrived at nine in the morning and was glad to be there.

The palace also announced the birth in a press release. Soon after, the medical notice was taken from the hospital to a car by Ed Perkins, Prince William’s press secretary. He gave it to a soldier, who took it to Buckingham Palace with police escort. The notice was signed by Marcus Setchell, the Queen’s gynaecologist.

Prime minister David Cameron congratulated the couple outside 10 Downing Street. He said people in the country and the Commonwealth would celebrate and wish them well. He said it was an important moment for the nation, and also a happy moment for the couple.

Barack Obama and his wife also sent congratulations from the White House. Obama said he and Michelle were happy for the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge and wished them happiness.

The Archbishop of Canterbury, Justin Welby, wrote that he was delighted for them and wished them love, health, and happiness.

Labour leader Ed Miliband also congratulated them and wished them and their son happiness and good health.

The campaign group Republic said the royal birth raised questions about Britain and democratic values. Chief Executive Graham Smith said the baby should be able to grow up without “constant interference and intrusion”. He said the baby’s life, including career, religion, and personal relationships, was already planned, and he said people should think about whether this is the best way to run things.
