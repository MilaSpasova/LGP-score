David Bowie died from cancer. Until the end, he could still surprise people. His last album, Blackstar, came out on his 69th birthday, 8 January 2016. It showed he could still make strong messages and unusual, sometimes upsetting music.

In the 1970s, Bowie led new music and fashion trends. In the late 1960s he did mime and cabaret shows. Then he became a singer-songwriter. He helped start glam-rock. Later he made a style he called “plastic soul”. After that he moved to Berlin and made new electronic music. He often changed his sound and his look, and he was very curious and thoughtful.

Bowie was born David Robert Jones in south London. In 1953, his family moved to Kent. David was good at singing and playing the recorder. Later he studied art, music, and design. In 1961, his mother bought him a plastic saxophone. This instrument appeared many times in his music.

When he was 15, David formed his first band, the Kon-rads. Soon it was clear he should work alone. He chose the name Bowie so people would not confuse him with Davy Jones of the Monkees.

His first album came out in June 1967 and was called David Bowie. In July 1969 he released the song Space Oddity. This was his first big success. It came out at the time of the Apollo 11 moon landing and reached the top five in the UK.

In March 1970, Bowie married an art student, Angela Barnett. His music career kept growing. The Man Who Sold the World came out in the US in late 1970 and in the UK the next year. It had bold songs and a dark hard-rock sound. It talked about immortality, insanity, murder, and mysticism, which were not usual pop topics.

In 1972 he released Hunky Dory, with detailed and wordy songs. It was very good but only had moderate success. Later that year, The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars changed everything.

With this album, Bowie became a science-fiction character: a glam-rock star from space visiting a doomed Earth. The song Starman made the album a big hit. His stage clothes and daring shows made fans very excited, like the excitement seen during Beatlemania.

Bowie kept having success. His album Aladdin Sane (1973) was his first UK number 1 album. It included the hit songs The Jean Genie and Drive-in Saturday. But he was already planning new changes.

His interest in funk and soul was clear on Young Americans (1975). It gave him a US number 1 song, Fame, with John Lennon as a guest singer.

Station to Station (1976) introduced a new character, the Thin White Duke. Bowie also used this character in Nicolas Roeg’s film The Man Who Fell to Earth, where he played a sad space traveller.

Bowie’s marriage was falling apart, and he and Angela divorced in 1980. That year he had more success with the album Scary Monsters (and Super Creeps) and the number 1 song Ashes to Ashes. He also performed on Broadway as John Merrick in The Elephant Man.

In 1981 he worked with Queen on Under Pressure, which reached number 1. He also worked more across music and film. He appeared in the German movie Christiane F (1981) and wrote music for the soundtrack. He had another hit with Cat People (Putting Out Fire) from Paul Schrader’s movie Cat People (1982).

Bowie acted in more films, including The Hunger and the war drama Merry Christmas, Mr Lawrence, both in 1983. In music, 1983 was the year of his biggest push for mainstream success with the album Let’s Dance and concerts. Let’s Dance made him a worldwide rock star. The album and its songs Let’s Dance, China Girl, and Modern Love became huge international hits.

MTV was very popular then, and Bowie’s eye-catching videos helped. His six-month Serious Moonlight tour drew very large crowds. This was the most commercially successful time in his career.

In 1985 his fame grew again when he performed at the Live Aid concert at Wembley Stadium. He was one of the best performers there. He also recorded Dancing in the Street with Mick Jagger, and it quickly reached number 1.

In April 1992, a few days after he appeared at the Freddie Mercury tribute concert at Wembley Stadium, Bowie married the Somalian model Iman. They bought a home in New York. This new start in his private life came with a search for new musical ideas.

On Black Tie White Noise (1993), he mixed in soul, electronica, and hip hop. The album reached number 1 in the UK and had a top 10 single, Jump They Say.

New media and technology also affected his music. His 1999 album Hours… used music he wrote for the computer game Omikron. In the game, Bowie and Iman appeared as characters. In August 2000, Bowie and Iman’s daughter, Alexandria, was born.

Living in New York, Bowie opened the Concert for New York City in October 2001. He performed with Paul McCartney, Jon Bon Jovi, Billy Joel, the Who, and Elton John. This benefit show happened six weeks after the 9/11 attacks.

Bowie made the album Reality the next year. During the Reality tour in 2004, he had chest pains while performing in Germany. He had emergency surgery in Hamburg to clear a blocked artery.
