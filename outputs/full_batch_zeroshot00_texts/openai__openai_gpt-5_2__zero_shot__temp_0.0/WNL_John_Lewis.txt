John Lewis made a Christmas advert in 2015 about a lonely old man who lives in a crater on the moon. The store also wanted the advert to help a charity.

Many people get excited about the John Lewis Christmas advert. For some people, it feels like the start of Christmas shopping. John Lewis said it would try to raise hundreds of thousands of pounds for Age UK. It also asked staff and customers to join their local Age UK branch to help older people who might be alone during the holiday.

John Lewis spent £7m on the campaign. It included the TV advert, a smartphone game, and items to buy, like glow-in-the-dark pyjamas. In 11 stores, there were areas decorated to look like the moon.

In the past two years, the adverts had cute animals: a bear and hare, and then a penguin. This time, the story is about a young girl named Lily. She sees an old man living in a small shack on the moon through her telescope. Lily tries to contact him by sending a letter and by shooting a note with a bow and arrow. Then she sends him a present: a telescope tied to balloons. At last, they can make contact.

The advert’s line is: “Show someone they’re loved this Christmas”. This is similar to Age UK’s message: “No one should have no one at Christmas”. Profits from three products, a mug, a gift tag, and a card, will go to the charity.

Rachel Swift, head of marketing at John Lewis, said the charity connects with people at this time of year. She said the advert can make people think about someone on their street who might not see anybody.

The campaign uses the Oasis song Half the World Away, sung in a new way by Norwegian artist Aurora.

The advert cost £1m to make. The moon scenes were filmed at Warner Bros Studios, where the Harry Potter films were made. The set was built by someone from the team behind the Star Wars film The Force Awakens.

John Lewis also shared teasers on TV and social media using the hashtag #OnTheMoon. There was a full moon on Christmas Day 2015, and Swift said this was just a coincidence.

In 2014, John Lewis also spent £7m on a campaign. It had a realistic animated penguin and a young boy, with the song Real Love by John Lennon, sung by Tom Odell. By the first week of January, it had 22m views on YouTube. That was more than Sainsbury’s advert from 2014, which had 16.6m views and showed First World War soldiers sharing a bar of chocolate.

Swift said John Lewis has used the same plan for the last five years. She said it is about giving thoughtful gifts and doing extra for someone you love at Christmas. She said they do not start by thinking the advert will be huge. They try to make something right for the brand and something customers will love.

Sarah Vizard, news editor at Marketing Week, said John Lewis seemed quieter in stores this time, even though more companies are competing. She said many brands tried to copy John Lewis by making emotional and creative adverts, but then went back to showing what you can buy in the store. She said John Lewis still does the emotional story best, and she thought people would find the advert really cute.

Other Christmas adverts also tried to get attention. Waitrose, which is connected to John Lewis, put its advert online before it appeared on TV during The X Factor. It used the same agency that has made John Lewis Christmas adverts for years.

Burberry released its second Christmas film, with Romeo Beckham and Elton John. Currys used Jeff Goldblum for its first special Christmas advert. Asda, Lidl, and Morrisons also started their campaigns at the beginning of November.

Marc Bolland, chief executive of Marks & Spencer, said that in the first week of September, the most searched word on its website was Christmas.

Boots also started its Christmas campaign in early November. It went back to a more traditional style after its praised 2014 advert about a family doing a lot to be together at Christmas.
