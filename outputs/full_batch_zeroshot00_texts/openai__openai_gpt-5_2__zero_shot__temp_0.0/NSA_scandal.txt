A top-secret document says the National Security Agency (NSA) got direct access to the computer systems of Google, Facebook, Apple, and other big US internet companies. The document says this is part of a secret program called PRISM. PRISM lets officials collect things like search history, email content, file transfers, and live chats.

The Guardian says it checked and confirmed the document is real. The document is a 41-slide PowerPoint and was marked “top secret.” It was not meant to be shared with foreign allies. It seems it was used to train intelligence workers. The document says the NSA can collect information “directly from the servers” of major US service providers. The presentation suggests the companies help with the program, but the companies that answered questions said they did not know about any such program.

Google said it cares about keeping users’ data safe. Google said it shares user data with the government only when the law requires it, and it carefully reviews requests. Google also said it does not have a government “back door” to private user data.

Several senior tech leaders said they did not know about PRISM or anything like it. They said they would not take part in such a program. One said that if the NSA is doing this, it is doing it without their knowledge. An Apple spokesman said he had “never heard” of PRISM.

The document says the NSA access was made possible by changes to US surveillance law. These changes started under President Bush and were renewed under Obama in December 2012. The program allows deep surveillance of live communications and stored information. The law allows targeting of customers of the companies who live outside the US. It also allows targeting of Americans when their communications include people outside the US. It could also allow communications made only inside the US to be collected without warrants.

The PRISM news came after another leak to the Guardian. That earlier leak was a top-secret court order that required the phone company Verizon to give phone records of millions of US customers. PRISM adds to the debate about how much surveillance intelligence services do. Unlike collecting call records, this kind of surveillance can include the content of messages, not only the metadata.

The document claims some of the biggest internet brands have been part of PRISM since 2007. It says Microsoft was first, starting in December 2007. Then Yahoo joined in 2008. Google, Facebook, and PalTalk joined in 2009. YouTube joined in 2010. Skype and AOL joined in 2011. Apple joined in 2012. The document says the program is still growing and more providers may join. Together, these companies cover most online email, search, video, and communication services. The document says the amount and type of data collected from each company is different.

Under US law, companies must follow legal requests for users’ communications. But the PRISM program, the document says, lets intelligence services get direct access to the companies’ servers. The NSA document says the operations have the “assistance of communications providers in the US.”

The news also supports worries raised by several US senators when the FISA Amendments Act (FAA) was renewed in December 2012. They warned the law could allow very large-scale surveillance and that the protections might not be strong enough. When the FAA first became law, people who supported it said a key protection was that the NSA could not get electronic communications without the agreement of the phone and internet companies that control the data. But PRISM, the document says, makes that agreement unnecessary, because it lets the NSA take communications directly from the companies’ servers.

A chart in the document lists many types of data the NSA can get: email, video and voice chat, videos, photos, voice-over-IP chats (like Skype), file transfers, social networking details, and more. The document is dated April 2013. Leaks like this are very rare for the NSA, which tries to keep things secret.

The document says PRISM lets the NSA get targeted communications without asking the service providers for them and without getting separate court orders for each person. It says the NSA can reach into the companies’ servers to get stored messages and also collect information in real time about targeted users.

A senior administration official said the Guardian and Washington Post articles describe collection under Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA). The official said the law does not allow targeting any US citizen or anyone inside the United States. The official said the program is overseen by the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court, the Executive Branch, and Congress. The official said there are court-approved rules to make sure only non-US people outside the US are targeted, and to reduce how much information about US people is collected, kept, and shared.

The official also said Congress recently approved the program again after hearings and debate. The official said information collected under this program is very important and is used to protect the nation from many kinds of threats.
