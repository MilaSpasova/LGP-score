The last series of Downton Abbey starts with a sad feeling and a sense that times are changing. It is 1925. People can already feel that hard economic times are coming later in the decade. Neighbours are selling their big house. Lord Grantham wants fewer servants because he says under-butlers cost too much.

But at the real “Downton Abbey”, Highclere Castle, the money situation is much better. Highclere is owned by George “Geordie” Herbert, the 8th Earl of Carnarvon. Lady Fiona Carnarvon says the world-wide success of Downton Abbey has paid for many building repairs, to protect Highclere for the next generation.

Lady Carnarvon said it has been like a “magic carpet ride”. She said the show gave them great marketing and made them well known around the world. She said she is very grateful. She said she and her husband love the house and the people there, and now millions of other people love it too.

Right now, only the ground floor and first floor of Highclere, near Hampshire, are used. A project has started to fix old tower rooms. Later, visitors will be able to climb up to an exhibition about Sir Charles Barry. He was the architect of the Houses of Parliament, and he rebuilt the house between 1839 and 1842.

When the Downton Abbey producers first contacted Highclere in 2009, the family had a repair bill close to £12m. Urgent work cost £1.8m. By 2012, the “Downton effect” was helping. Lord Carnarvon said it was just after the banking crisis and things felt gloomy. They had done corporate events, but there were fewer after that. Then Downton came, and Highclere became a major tourist attraction.

The number of visitors doubled to 1,200 a day. Downton Abbey, written by Julian Fellowes, became a hit in the UK in 2010 and then in the US. Now it is shown in 250 countries.

Highclere’s ticket system used to be simple. Now it is a computer system where people book in advance, which helps make sure foreign visitors can get in. Accounts from Highclere Enterprises for 2014-15 show that current assets have almost tripled since 2012, to about £1m. Gareth Neame, the executive producer, said Downton Abbey helped secure Highclere’s future.

Peter Fincham, ITV’s director of television, remembers when they booked Highclere. At first he thought it did not matter because he had never heard of Highclere Castle. He thought one stately home looked like another. But he was wrong. He said the castle became a huge “character” in the show too.

Downton tourists are part of a growing trend. VisitBritain says nearly 30% of foreign visitors, almost nine million people, include castles and historic houses in their plans. Almost half of people who may visit Britain now say they want to do “set jetting”, which means visiting places they saw in films or on TV.

Each year, more than a million people take tours of historic buildings and spend more than £1bn. From new growing tourist markets, 51% of Brazilians, 42% of Russians and Chinese, and 35% of Indian visitors are likely to visit an interesting site.

VisitBritain’s director, Patricia Yates, said the connection between tourism and films and TV is very strong. She said period dramas have also made places outside London more popular.

Neame is now an ambassador for the GREAT Britain campaign. It is supported by government departments and the British Council, and it promotes the UK around the world. Some events are special Downton-themed receptions at British embassies.

Neame said they asked him because the show reaches so many people. He said some people in the UK think it is just “soapy” entertainment. But in other countries, people greatly respect British actors, writing, and production skills. He said he believes in “soft power”, and he thinks the UK should be prouder of what it does well.

He said Downton Abbey is famous for showing “Britishness”. He said it is really a fantasy world in a certain time in history. He said it is the first TV period drama that has jumped off the screen and become part of popular culture.

Lady Carnarvon still says Highclere’s long-term future is not fully safe. She said there is not much extra money. She said the show has let them spend faster on the buildings and restore the follies.

They are planning a Tutankhamun centenary event in 2022. It is 100 years after the 5th Earl of Carnarvon and Howard Carter discovered the tomb, which changed what people knew about Egyptology. Another chance to keep Highclere famous is the 300th anniversary of the birth of Lancelot “Capability” Brown, who designed the grounds.

Lady Carnarvon said you should never relax and think the work is finished. She said you must not take anything for granted. She said great houses need constant investment. She said there has been a deficit since the 1930s. In the past, the estate and house supported the family’s lifestyle. Today it is the opposite: she and Geordie must find ways to support and take care of Highclere.

She said she tries to show people that visiting can be fun, with special events, not only walking around an old dusty house. She said they also have to compete with attractions like the London Dungeon.
