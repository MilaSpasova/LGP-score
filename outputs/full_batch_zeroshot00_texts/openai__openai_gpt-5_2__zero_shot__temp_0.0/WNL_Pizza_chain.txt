Angry waiters want the public to help them keep their tips. Protesters plan to target PizzaExpress restaurants. They want PizzaExpress to stop taking part of the tips that customers pay by credit or debit card. Campaigners also started an online petition, hoping customers will support them.

Some workers are upset because PizzaExpress keeps 8p out of every £1 of card tips as an “admin fee.” Unite, a union, says the company makes about £1m a year this way. PizzaExpress has 430 branches in the UK and is popular with families.

Chantal Chegrinec from Unite said the 8% fee is unfair. She said staff should get all the tips customers give. She also said PizzaExpress is not the only company doing this, and the union may look at other companies later.

Local Unite groups are organizing the protests. The first protest will be at the PizzaExpress at the British Museum in London. The union also wrote to PizzaExpress CEO Richard Hodgson. Unite started the campaign after a survey of PizzaExpress union members. The chain was sold to a Chinese private equity firm, Hony Capital, in 2014. One big issue was the 8% taken from tips.

One PizzaExpress employee, who did not want her name used, said the fee costs her £3 each night. She said she has worked there for 15 years but is still paid the national minimum wage of £6.50 an hour. She said she and her co-workers need tips because their wages are low. She said it upsets her when PizzaExpress takes part of tips paid by card.

PizzaExpress is not the only chain that takes 8% from card tips. Ask and Zizzi do the same. Some chains take even more: Cafe Rouge, Bella Italia, and Belgo take 10%. Strada and Giraffe also take 10%, and Giraffe is owned by Tesco.

A PizzaExpress spokesperson said the admin charge pays for a “tronc.” This is a system used to share tips among staff. She said it is run by employees, with a troncmaster and a committee of waiters and pizzaiolos. They decide how card tips are shared between front-of-house and back-of-house teams.

PizzaExpress sells 29m pizzas a year in the UK. The company said it does not profit from the admin fee. Some other restaurant groups do not take any fee from tips. Wagamama, Pizza Hut, and TGI Friday take nothing. The Restaurant Group, which owns Frankie & Benny’s, Chiquitos, and Garfunkels, used to take 10% but stopped several years ago.

Unite recently went to ten PizzaExpress restaurants in south London and gave leaflets to customers. Unite said customers were “shocked and disgusted.” PizzaExpress said the charge is written in small print at the bottom of menus. But the employee said customers were always surprised when she told them. Many then paid the tip in cash. Almost 6,000 people have signed the online petition.

A waiter who does not work for PizzaExpress, but has worked for another chain for 11 years, said tips are at least a third of his income. He did not want his name used because he is afraid of punishment. He said he works in a busy London branch. On an average night, he serves 150 people and takes home £40 to £50 in tips. He said this money is very important because his basic pay is only £6.50 an hour.

Conservative MP Andrew Percy said he wants the law to change so restaurant staff have more control over tips. He said he plans to bring the issue up in parliament after the summer recess.
