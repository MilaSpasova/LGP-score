A few months before he died, Carl Sagan recorded a hopeful message for people who might go to Mars. He told them: “Whatever the reason you’re on Mars is, I’m glad you’re there. And I wish I was with you.”

Seventeen years after Sagan shared his ideas in 1996, a company from the Netherlands said it wants to help make a Mars trip real. The company is called Mars One. It plans to send four astronauts to Mars in 2023 to start a human colony. But there are serious problems.

First, on Mars the astronauts would live with gravity that is only 38% of Earth’s. People think this could change their bodies so much—bones, muscles, and blood flow—that they might not be able to live on Earth again. Second, they would have to leave their family and friends, because the plan does not include a ticket back.

The Mars One website says a return “cannot be anticipated nor expected.” To come back, they would need a complete rocket with fuel to leave Mars, life support for a trip of up to seven months, and a way to connect to a space station near Earth or land safely back on Earth.

The website says none of this is easy, and it would take a lot of technology, weight, and money.

Still, the company’s Medical Director, Norbert Kraft, said 10,000 people had applied. He told The Guardian that the applicants were from age 18 to at least 62. Some were women, but most were men.

Kraft said people had many reasons for wanting to go. A 32-year-old American woman named Cynthia told the company she had dreamed of going to Mars since she was a child. She remembered a trip her mother took her on in the early 1990s to a talk at the University of Wisconsin.

Cynthia said the speaker was Carl Sagan. She asked him if humans would land on Mars during her lifetime. She said he asked her if she would want to be stuck in a “tin can spacecraft” for the two years it would take to get there. She said yes. He smiled and told her he truly believed humans would reach Mars in her lifetime.

Cynthia told the project that when she first heard about Mars One, she thought it was her chance. Her childhood dream could come true. She could be a pioneer, help build the first settlement on Mars, and show people back home that humans can still reach new places.

Kraft said Mars One is looking for people who are strong in hard times, able to adjust to change, curious, able to trust others, and good at finding solutions. They must also be over 18.

Mars One was founded in 2010 by Bas Lansdorp, an engineer. The company says it has a realistic plan and a way to pay for it using existing technology, and that the mission can be done. The website says Mars already has basic things needed for life. For example, water can be taken from ice in the soil. Mars also has nitrogen, which is the main part of the air we breathe. The company says the colony would use specially adapted solar panels for power.

Mars One said it signed a contract with the American company Paragon Space Development Corporation to begin work on life support systems and spacesuits for the mission.

The project is reported to cost $6bn. Lansdorp said he hopes to pay for part of it by selling broadcasting rights. He said the money from broadcasting the London Olympics was almost enough to pay for a Mars mission.

Another ambassador for the project is Paul Römer, who helped create Big Brother, an early and very successful reality TV show.

On the website, Römer said this Mars mission could be the biggest media event in the world. He said it would be like a reality show and a talent show with no ending, with the whole world watching.

The company’s goal is to build a permanent human colony. The first team would land on Mars in 2023 to start building it. After that, a team of four astronauts would arrive every two years.

Not everyone believes the plan will work. Some people worry about how astronauts would get to Mars and build a colony with all the life support and other things they would need.

Professor Gerard ‘t Hooft, who won the Nobel Prize for theoretical physics in 1999, is also an ambassador for the project. He says there are unknown health risks. He said the radiation is very different from anything tested on Earth.

The mission also hopes to inspire people for many years to believe that anything can be achieved, like the Apollo Moon landings.

Mars One says it believes it is not only possible, but necessary, to build a permanent settlement on Mars. It says this would help humans learn more about how the solar system formed, how life began, and our place in the universe.
