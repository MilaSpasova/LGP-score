Apple’s brand and logo have been named the most valuable in the world. It is worth nearly $119bn. That is more than the whole gross domestic product of Morocco, Ecuador, or Oman.

Apple is already the world’s biggest company. Its stock market value is $591bn. Interbrand’s Best Global Brands report says Apple’s brand value grew by 21% in 12 months.

Apple is known around the world for its simple logo: an apple with a bite missing. In the 2014 report, many technology companies moved up. Older famous brands like Coca-Cola, McDonald’s, and Gillette moved down.

Google’s brand value rose by 15% to $107bn, taking second place. Coca-Cola was next, up 3% to $81.5bn. IBM was worth $72.2bn, and Microsoft was worth $45.5bn.

Facebook had the biggest rise. Its brand value grew by 86% to $14.3bn. It reached 29th place, ahead of Volkswagen, Kellogg’s, and Ford.

Jez Frampton, the chief executive of Interbrand (part of Omnicom), said digital and mobile technology helped brands like Apple grow stronger. He said Google, Amazon, and Facebook have changed what customers expect and have raised the standard for brand experiences.

Apple was founded by former Chief Executive Steve Jobs in his Los Altos garage in 1976. Apple only entered the top ten of Interbrand’s yearly study in 2011.

The Apple logo was created by advertising executive Rob Janoff in 1977. It was designed with a bite taken out so it would not be confused with a cherry. Janoff also described the logo as a symbol with ideas like lust, knowledge, hope, and anarchy.

Graham Hayles, Interbrand’s chief marketing officer, said it makes sense that Apple’s brand could be about one fifth of the company’s total market value. He said Apple makes a lot of money because it has a very strong brand, and that branding and profit are closely linked.

Hayles said Interbrand has done this yearly study since 2000. It calculates brand value by looking at company financial results, how loyal customers are to the brand, and an analysis of brand strength.

Some technology companies fell a lot. Nokia dropped 41 places to 98th, with a value of $4.1bn. Nintendo was 100th, down 33 places. Hayles said this shows innovation is important, because falling behind can hurt a company.

A Chinese company entered the top 100 for the first time. Huawei, a mobile-phone and broadband company, came in at 94th with a brand value of $4.3bn. Huawei has been partly banned by the US and Australian governments because of fears its equipment could be used for cyber-espionage by the Chinese.

Most brands in the top 100 are owned by US companies. The highest non-US brands were South Korea’s Samsung (6th), Japan’s Toyota (8th), and Germany’s Mercedes-Benz (10th). The highest British brands were HSBC (33rd), Shell (65th), and Burberry (73rd).

Other fashion brands in the top 100 include Boss, Prada, and Ralph Lauren. Louis Vuitton was the highest fashion brand, at 19th, worth $23bn. It was just ahead of H&M at 21st, worth $21bn.

Nike was 22nd, worth nearly $20bn. It was far ahead of Adidas, which was 59th, worth $7bn.

Frampton said social media like Twitter lets people talk to brands and criticize them. He said companies must respond faster to protect and improve their reputations. He said customers now have more control, and brand-building is now about two-way conversations, support from customers, influence, and engagement. He also said customers expect smooth service, quick replies, 24/7 access, choices for customization, and personal service. He said customers increasingly expect brands to know them.
