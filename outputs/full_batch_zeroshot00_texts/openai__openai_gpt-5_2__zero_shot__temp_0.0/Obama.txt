Barack Obama flew back to Washington on Wednesday. He went back to work in the Oval Office. Earlier that day, he gave a victory speech in Chicago after winning the election. He asked the country to unite.

Obama said, “You voted for action, not politics as usual.” But it did not look like things would get easier. The House of Representatives was led by Republicans, and Obama would have to work with them for at least two more years. There was also a coming fight about government spending and debt, called the “fiscal cliff.”

After his 2008 election, Obama got a short “honeymoon” time. This time, he likely would not.

House Speaker John Boehner and Senate Democratic Leader Harry Reid both said they needed to work together. Still, the fight between the White House and Congress could become very big.

Obama beat his Republican opponent, Mitt Romney. He won many swing states. But the election also showed that America is still very divided.

Obama’s inauguration would not be until January, but he began his second term in practice on Wednesday. Many supporters felt disappointed by his first term. Now he wanted to build a strong legacy.

Obama hoped the economy would keep getting better. He also hoped to work on immigration reform, education, climate change, and foreign policy issues like Iran and Israel-Palestine.

Obama won more than the 270 electoral college votes he needed. He also won a higher share of the popular vote.

Boehner spoke in a friendly way. He said both parties should find common ground to help the economy grow, create jobs, and solve the debt problem. Reports said Obama called Boehner to start talks.

Reid also spoke in a friendly way. He said he wanted to work with Republicans in the Senate and the House to solve the issues.

During the campaign, Obama said he was hopeful about a “grand bargain” with Republicans. He could not reach that kind of deal in 2011. Problems would come when they talked about details. Republicans wanted to protect military spending, while Democrats wanted cuts. Obama called for tax increases on households earning more than $250,000. Boehner rejected any tax increases.

Stock prices fell because people expected continued gridlock. By lunchtime, major US markets were down over 300 points.

The new House in January would look similar to the current one, with a large Republican majority. The Senate also stayed similar. Democrats kept their slim majority, gaining three seats and losing one.

In the presidential race, Romney won only one swing state: North Carolina. Obama won New Hampshire, Virginia, Ohio, Wisconsin, Nevada, Iowa, and Colorado.

In his Chicago victory speech, Obama talked about long lines of people waiting to vote. He said the country needed electoral reform.

He used the kind of inspiring language he was known for in 2008. He used less of that in 2012 because many people were unhappy with his presidency and the economy was tough. But after winning, he returned to famous lines and his 2008 slogan about “hope.”

As Stevie Wonder’s “Signed, Sealed, Delivered, I’m Yours” played, Obama spoke to cheering supporters. He said the road had been hard and the journey long, but Americans had fought their way back, and that the best was still to come. His speech lasted more than 25 minutes. He thanked his wife, Michelle, his daughters, Malia and Sasha, and his Vice-President, Joe Biden.

Obama said, “We are not as divided as our politics suggests.” He said Americans were more than red states and blue states. He said they were the United States of America.

Obama said he had plans for his second term. He mentioned changes in the tax code, immigration reform, and an America “that isn’t threatened by the destructive power of a warming planet.”

Before that, Romney called Obama to admit he lost. In a concession speech in Boston, Romney said the nation was at a critical point. He said leaders should stop partisan fighting and work together. He also said he prayed Obama would be successful in guiding the nation.

For much of the campaign, the election was a referendum on Obama. Many people were unhappy about the slow economic recovery and high unemployment. But Americans chose to keep him as president.

For African Americans and many white liberals, it would have been a major disappointment if the first black presidency had ended in failure and stopped early.
