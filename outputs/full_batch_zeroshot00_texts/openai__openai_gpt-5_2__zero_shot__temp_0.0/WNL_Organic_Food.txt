A big scientific study says organic food has more antioxidant compounds than regular food. It also has lower levels of toxic metals and pesticides. The researchers say that eating organic fruit and vegetables could give a similar benefit to adding one or two extra portions in the daily “five a day” advice.

The team was led by Professor Carlo Leifert at Newcastle University. They said the differences were “statistically significant” and “meaningful.” They found many antioxidants were higher in organic food, by about 19% to 69%. They said this is the first study to show clear differences across many kinds of fruits, vegetables, and cereals.

The researchers said the extra antioxidants could be like eating one or two of the five daily portions of fruit and vegetables. They said this would matter for nutrition if it is confirmed that these compounds really cause the health benefits linked to eating more fruit, vegetables, and wholegrains.

Not everyone agrees. Tom Sanders, a nutrition professor at King’s College London, said the study does show some differences. But he questioned if the differences are just normal natural changes, and if they really matter for health. He also said Leifert’s work has caused arguments before. Sanders added that the research showed organic cereals have less protein than conventional cereals.

The results came from looking at 343 peer-reviewed studies from around the world. Helen Browning, the chief executive of the Soil Association, said the study shows that the way we farm can affect the quality of food.

Plants can make antioxidants to protect themselves from pests. So organic crops may have more antioxidants because they are not protected by chemical sprays. The scientists also said other reasons might matter, like organic plants being bred to be tough and not being given too much artificial fertilizer.

Leifert and his team said many antioxidants have been linked before to a lower risk of long-term diseases, including cardiovascular diseases, neurodegenerative diseases, and some cancers. But they also said there have not been long-term studies that show health benefits from eating a wide organic diet. They found much higher levels of cadmium, a toxic metal, in conventional crops. They also found pesticide residues on conventional crops four times more often than on organic crops. The EU and an organic farming charity paid for the research.

The study will likely be criticized. Using so many studies could let some poor-quality studies affect the results. The team did extra checks and said removing weaker studies did not change the results much. Also, the cadmium and pesticide levels in conventional food were still below legal limits. But the researchers said cadmium builds up in the body over time, and some people may want to avoid it. They also said pesticide limits are set for each chemical one by one, not for mixtures of chemicals.

Another criticism is that the differences might come from different climates, soil, and crop types, not from organic farming. The researchers said using many studies should reduce these effects. The biggest argument is about possible health benefits. A major study in 2012 that used 223 studies found little evidence. It said there was not strong evidence that organic food is much more nutritious than conventional food.

Smaller studies in 2009, including one by the UK Food Standards Agency, also said about the same thing. The 2012 study did say organic food might help people avoid pesticide residues. Sanders said he was not convinced by the new study. He said people will not be better nourished just because food is organic. He said what matters most is eating fruit and vegetables, whether they are organic or not. He said some people also buy organic because they want food without chemicals and not from big business.

Opinion polls show people buy organic food mainly because they want healthy eating (55%) and to avoid chemical residues (53%). Other reasons include caring for the environment (44%), animal welfare (31%), and taste (35%). Browning said this research supports what many people already believe about organic food. She said that in other countries there has long been more support for the benefits of organic food and farming, and she hopes the UK will become more like the rest of Europe.
