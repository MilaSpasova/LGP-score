When we talk about climate change, we often focus only on the problem and forget that there are many solutions. Solutions can help us recycle faster, reduce emissions, and find greener choices instead of plastic, air conditioning, smartphones, and fast fashion.

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change met in Copenhagen to share its newest report. Climate change is now measured on all continents. We must work harder to lower emissions so the problem does not get out of control. The meeting also talked about solutions, not only risks.

Sustainia Award, led by Arnold Schwarzenegger, honored ten strong sustainability solutions used in 84 countries. The solutions covered many areas, like food, fashion, energy, transportation, education, and health. The awards showed that a better future is possible and helped innovators, investors, consumers, and policy makers.

From California, people showed a way to make plastic from greenhouse gases. This plastic can match oil-based plastic in price and quality. From Switzerland, people showed how to recycle and reuse old clothes and shoes better, using a system that is in over 60 countries. From Canada, people showed how smartphones can make bike sharing easier.

All ten projects had different ideas, but the Nigerian project Wecyclers won the Sustainia Award 2014.

Wecyclers helps low-income communities earn money from waste in their streets. In Lagos, it uses cargo bicycles to collect and recycle waste. Families can trade their garbage for consumer goods using an SMS point system.

Recycling companies buy Wecyclers’ sorted waste and turn it into new products, like mattresses, pillows, and trash bags. Wecyclers answers a local problem, because only about 40% of the city’s rubbish is collected. The World Bank says only 46% of municipal solid waste in Africa is collected. More than 5,000 households have joined so far, and there are plans to expand to other cities in Nigeria.

People often think climate change solutions must be high-tech and focused only on cutting emissions, building infrastructure, or improving efficiency. But we need many kinds of solutions. Sustainability is not only about lowering emissions. It is also about using natural resources wisely and creating healthier lives. Some ideas may be low-tech but can still make a big difference for whole communities.

Because there are many challenges and many solutions, we should focus more on the part of the story that gives hope, builds energy, and leads to action for needed change.
