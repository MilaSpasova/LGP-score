Independent data suggests Facebook is losing millions of users each month in its biggest markets. Other social networks are getting attention from people who want something new.

Facebook is about to tell investors how it did in the first three months of the year. Analysts expect revenue to be 36% higher than last year. But studies say Facebook’s growth in the US, UK, and other big European countries may have reached its top point.

In the last month, Facebook lost 6m visitors in the US, a 4% drop, according to Socialbakers. In the UK, 1.4m fewer users visited in March, a 4.5% drop. These drops have continued. Over the last six months, Facebook has lost nearly 9m monthly visitors in the US and 2m in the UK.

Users are also leaving in Canada, Spain, France, Germany, and Japan. These are countries where Facebook has many users. A Facebook spokeswoman did not comment.

Ian Maude, a new media specialist at Enders Analysis, said, “In the US and UK, most people who want Facebook have already signed up.” He also said some people get bored and want to try something new. He asked if Facebook could end up like MySpace. He said the risk is small, but it still exists.

Other social networks are growing. Instagram, a photo-sharing app, gained 30m users in 18 months before Facebook bought it. It has become more popular with younger people.

Path is a social network for mobile phones. It was started by Dave Morin, a former Facebook employee. It limits users to 150 friends. Path is gaining 1m users each week. It recently passed 9m users. In one weekend, 500,000 people in Venezuela downloaded the app.

Facebook is still growing fast in South America. In Brazil, monthly visitors rose 6% in the last month to 70m, according to Socialbakers. In India, monthly visitors rose 4% to 64m. That is still only a small part of India’s population, so there is room to grow.

But in developed markets, other trackers also report declines. Analysts at Jefferies bank made an algorithm that connects directly to Facebook software. It suggests user levels in the first quarter may have dropped from their peak.

Jefferies saw global monthly numbers peak at 1.05bn in January, then fall by 20m in February. Numbers rose again in April. In the UK, Facebook has lost nearly 2m visitors since December, according to Nielsen. Nielsen says Facebook has about 27m total there, about the same as a year earlier.

Americans also seem to be spending less time on Facebook. comScore says people spent 121 billion minutes on Facebook in December 2012, but only 115 billion minutes in February.

Facebook has warned that time spent on Facebook pages from personal computers is dropping fast. This is because people are using smartphones and tablets more.

Smartphone minutes have doubled in a year to 69 a month. But this growth may not fully make up for people using desktop computers less.

Facebook is the main source for its own user numbers, and it will update investors on the quarter. Wall Street expects revenue of about $1.44bn, up from $1.06bn in 2012.

Shareholders especially want to know how fast Facebook’s mobile user base is growing. They also want to know if advertising revenue is growing just as fast.

At the end of 2012, mobile use made up nearly a quarter of Facebook’s advertising income. In December, Facebook had 680m mobile users each month.

In recent stockmarket filings, the company warned it might be losing “younger users” to “other products and services similar to, or as a substitute for, Facebook”.

Mark Zuckerberg, Facebook’s founder, is worried about competition from services made for mobile phones instead of PCs. He has pushed new projects to attract smartphone users. The biggest is Facebook Home. It is software that some Android phone owners can download. It puts news, photos from friends, and advertising onto the phone’s locked home screen.
