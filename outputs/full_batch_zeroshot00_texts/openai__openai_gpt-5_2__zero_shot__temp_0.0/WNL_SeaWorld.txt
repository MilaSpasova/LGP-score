SeaWorld’s profits fell by 84% because many customers stopped going to the park. People left after claims that SeaWorld mistreated orca whales.

SeaWorld trains dolphins and killer whales to do tricks for large crowds. The company said attendance, sales, and profits went down because of ongoing problems with its brand.

SeaWorld has had a lot of news coverage since the 2013 documentary Blackfish. The film said SeaWorld’s treatment of orcas led to violent behavior and was linked to the deaths of three people. After the film came out, attendance dropped a lot. SeaWorld lost more than half of its market value on Wall Street, and its CEO left.

SeaWorld started a marketing campaign across the country to fight claims from animal rights activists. One claim is that orcas in captivity die younger than wild orcas.

SeaWorld lowered ticket prices and spent $10m on the campaign. The ads show SeaWorld veterinarians caring for whales. But CEO Joel Manby said the company is still having trouble convincing the public that it treats whales well.

Manby said the company needs time and investment to raise attendance, increase revenue, and improve results. He said early feedback on the campaign was positive, but fixing the brand problems in California will take continued effort to correct misinformation.

He also said, “We will continue to fight with the facts because the facts are on our side.”

Manby became CEO in 2015 to help SeaWorld rebuild its image. He said he would share his future plans at a special event on 6 November.

SeaWorld has plans for a new shark exhibit in Orlando. It also plans an attraction in San Antonio where customers can swim with dolphins in a “naturalistic” setting.

A financial report released on 6 August showed SeaWorld’s net income in the second quarter fell from $37.4m in 2014 to $5.8m in 2015. Revenue fell from $405m to $392m. Attendance dropped by more than 100,000, from 6.58 million to 6.48 million.

Analysts will watch sales and attendance in the third quarter. This is usually SeaWorld’s most profitable time and includes the summer holiday season. Attendance may drop more because of a new scandal from July 2015. In that case, a SeaWorld employee was accused of secretly joining animal rights protest groups.

Jared Goodman from PETA said SeaWorld is in a spying scandal, animals are dying in its tanks, and many people opposed its plan to build a new place to keep orcas. He said families do not want to buy tickets to see orcas suffering in small tanks. He said SeaWorld’s profits will not recover until SeaWorld empties its tanks and builds coastal sanctuaries.

SeaWorld’s shares were $39 in 2013, but were just under $18 in August 2015.
