Music subscription services, like Spotify and Deezer, have made over $1 billion in sales worldwide. More and more fans are choosing to pay for music online.

In 2013, money from streaming and subscriptions rose by more than 50% and reached $1.1 billion. Figures published in March 2014 said this helped recorded music sales in Europe grow for the first time in 12 years.

There are about 450 music-rental services around the world. Many people still listen for free, but more people are paying because they want more choice. In three years, the number of paying subscribers grew from 8 million to 28 million. This information came from the 2014 digital music report from the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI).

People can use these services easily on smartphones and tablets. They are popular because people can try new music without buying a download or a CD. Consumers say they like that it is cheap, easy to use, and legal instead of pirated downloads. IFPI Chief Executive Frances Moore said music streaming and subscription is now a main way of doing business.

The IFPI also said One Direction were the biggest-selling artists of 2013. They had 4 million physical and digital sales for their album Midnight Memories. Katy Perry’s Prism was the best-selling album by a female artist. It was in sixth place, behind Eminem, Justin Timberlake, Bruno Mars, and Daft Punk.

Technology companies are also joining music streaming. Apple launched iTunes Radio, and Google promoted its Play Store. Smaller companies like Beats Music, made by the team behind the Beats headphones brand, also joined.

In Britain and America, streaming may soon bring in more money for the music industry than downloads from online stores like Apple’s iTunes. Subscription services are now one third of all digital sales worldwide. Downloads make up the rest, but they are growing at different speeds.

In the US, the percent of people who said they use subscription and streaming went from 19% in 2012 to 23%. The percent of people downloading went from 28% to 27%. In Britain, the number of downloaders stayed the same at exactly one third, but subscribers grew from 19% to 22%. In Sweden, France, and Italy, streaming is already more popular than downloading.

Digital formats now make up 39% of all music sales, or nearly £5.9 billion out of £15 billion. CD and vinyl sales fell a lot in 2013, but they still bring in just over half of the industry’s income. Music sales in Japan dropped. Japan makes up one fifth of music industry sales, and physical formats stayed popular there longer than in other places. Because of this, total global sales across all formats fell 3.9%.

However, vinyl is coming back in some markets. Vinyl sales rose by 32% in America and by 101% in the UK in 2013.
