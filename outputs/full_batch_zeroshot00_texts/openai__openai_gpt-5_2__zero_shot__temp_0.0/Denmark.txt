Emmanuel Limal is a man who dated many people. He was tired of meeting women who did not want to start a family, or did not say they wanted one. Limal is a 43-year-old actor from France. He lived in Copenhagen for 20 years and looked for love because he wanted more children. He tried online dating, but he did not like what he saw.

He said many people tried to look very busy. They wrote about travelling a lot, going to the gym, and having many hobbies, but they did not write about children. Some websites had a box to click that said, “I’d like kids someday.” But when he read the profile, he thought the person would never have time for a family. He said that if someone goes to the gym eight times a week and travels every month, they are not putting a family first.

Limal has a six-year-old daughter from an earlier relationship. His father is one of 11 children, so Limal comes from a big family. He has always wanted more children. He said it was hard to know when to tell someone new that he wanted kids. He called it a big dating taboo. One day he read a profile from a 38-year-old woman. She said she knew it was “really bad to admit,” but she wanted children. Limal thought she should not feel ashamed.

Limal remortgaged his apartment to pay for a new dating website called Babyklar.nu. In English, the name means “baby-ready now.” It works like a normal dating site, but it asks people to be honest about wanting to start a family soon. The site asks if people are OK with dating someone who already has children and also wants another baby. But it does not ask people to say how many children they want. Limal said that would feel too much like shopping online.

He said many people joined the site. When it started in June, it got 50 sign-ups an hour. He said some couples have already met through the site and are now together. He expects the first Babyklar.nu baby by next summer. More men joined than women: 53% men and 47% women. Some people wrote messages like, “It’s so lovely to be able to say this out loud,” and “I finally dare to be honest about what I want.”

The site came at a good time for Denmark, a country of five million people. A report from the Copenhagen hospital Rigshospitalet says Danes are not having enough babies. The rate is now 1.7 children per family, and that is not enough to keep Denmark’s population the same size. Some people blame women for waiting “too late.” Others blame couples who live together and wait to start families.

Limal hopes the site will help men and women who want a family but have not met the right person. He says it is not only about making babies. He wants it to be about children and love. His goal is to match people who want a family and a partner, and who will stay together. He says he is a romantic.

There are plans to bring the site to France and the UK later in 2013. But for now, it is helping people in Denmark. Limal said Danes do not mind having children before marriage, so things can move fast. He also said Denmark is small, so a person from Jutland can date someone from Copenhagen without too much travel. Limal also said he has finally found love. He met a nice woman, and she wants a baby too, so he said they will see.
