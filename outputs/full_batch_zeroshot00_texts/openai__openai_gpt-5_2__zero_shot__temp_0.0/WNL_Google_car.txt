A car that can only go 25 mph, has two seats, and has no pedals or steering wheel might not seem exciting at a car show. But Google in the US surprised the car and taxi industries when it showed a new driverless car.

The electric car is being tested near Google’s headquarters in Mountain View, California. It has no normal controls, not even foot pedals. Instead, people use a smartphone app to call the car and choose where to go. There is one STOP button between the two front seats if the people inside need to stop the computer.

The car does all the driving jobs by itself. It handles navigation, steering, speeding up, and braking.

Google is building about 100 test cars for a two-year test. Google co-founder Sergey Brin said the car is still a prototype. He said the project is meant to help people who do not have good transportation today.

Brin said sitting in the car was relaxing because there was no steering wheel and no pedals. He said that after about ten seconds, he forgot about it. He said it felt like riding a chairlift alone and enjoying the quiet.

Google says the main goal is to make travel safer. The car has foam on the front to absorb impacts and a plastic windscreen. Google says this should make it much safer for pedestrians than other cars.

The cars were specially built by a company in Detroit, but its name has not been shared yet. Google will use the cars in a pilot program to learn how to make driverless cars work better. The cars are not for sale yet.

One problem is that the cars need very detailed maps of roads and nearby areas before they can drive there. They cannot collect and understand enough information fast enough while driving.

Right now, there are detailed maps of about 2,000 miles of roads in California. But California has more than 170,000 miles of public roads.

Google says it may license the technology to regular car makers after it improves it enough. Some team members worked on this idea for more than ten years, even before joining Google.

Some people worry that driverless cars could replace taxi drivers. Dennis Conyon, southeast director of the UK National Taxi Association, said that if you remove the driver, the driver will not have a job. He said it would strongly affect workers.

London has about 22,000 licensed black cabs. Conyon thinks about 100,000 people drive taxis for hire in the UK.

Steve McNamara, general secretary of the London Taxi Drivers’ Association with 10,500 members, said he does not expect driverless cars in London for 20 or 25 years. He also said they might need a charge point, because he said there is not a single one in London now.

Other car makers, like Volvo, Ford, and Mercedes, are working on cars that assist drivers. Unlike Google’s car, these still have driver controls.

Chris Urmson, director of Google’s self-driving car project, said the prototypes have no steering wheel and no brakes because a person inside might not be able to take over during an emergency. He said it is simpler to have an emergency stop button.

Urmson said the vehicles will be very basic so the team can learn quickly and improve them. He said they will take you where you want to go with the push of a button. He said this is an important step toward safer roads and better travel for millions of people.

So far, Google’s self-driving cars have driven 700,000 miles with no accident caused by the computer. Google says thousands of people die on roads each year, and about 80% of crashes are caused by human mistakes.

But Conyon says he has been driving a taxi for 50 years and has never had an accident. He is 79 years old.
