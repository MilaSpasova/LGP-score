Scarlett Johansson is suing a French novelist. She wants Û50,000 in damages. She says his fiction book makes false claims about her personal life.

The book is called La Premiere chose qu’on regarde (The First Thing We Look At). It was written by Grégoire Delacourt. It is about a French model who looks very much like Scarlett Johansson. A man in the story thinks the model is Johansson. Because she looks like her, men treat her like a sex object and women feel jealous. The model pretends to be Johansson and has many adventures. Later, people learn the truth. At the end, the model dies in a car crash.

Johansson does not like the book. Her lawyer, Vincent Toledano, says the novel illegally uses her name, reputation, and image. He also says the book has defamatory claims about her private life. They went to court to try to stop the book from being translated or made into a movie. The court case began in Paris on Wednesday afternoon. Neither Johansson nor Delacourt was there.

Toledano said freedom of expression is not the issue. He said the book was made for money, not creativity.

Delacourt said he mentioned Johansson because she is “the archetype of beauty today.” He said, “I wrote a work of fiction. My character is not Scarlett Johansson.”

On French radio, Delacourt said the lawsuit was “rather sad.” He said it scares him that judges could get involved in what happens to a character in a novel.

Delacourt is a well-known author in France. His earlier novel, My List of Desires, was translated into 47 languages and is being made into a film. He said he was “speechless” when he learned Johansson was suing him.

He said he thought she would contact him to meet for coffee. He said he did not write a novel about a celebrity. He said he wrote a love story and a tribute to women’s beauty, especially inner beauty.

He said if authors cannot mention things around them, like a beer brand, a monument, or an actor, it will be hard to write fiction. He also said it is surprising because he thinks Johansson has not read the novel, since it has not been translated yet.

Emmanuelle Allibert, a spokeswoman for the publisher JC Lattès, said the lawsuit is “crazy.” She said the novel is not about Scarlett Johansson. She said it is about a woman who is Johansson’s double.

The text says the case might be easier for Delacourt if the book had been published in the United States instead of France. Lloyd Jassin, a New York intellectual property lawyer, told Time that a U.S. court would probably not take the case because the book would be protected by the First Amendment. He said the First Amendment usually protects books if the use of a person’s name has literary meaning and merit. In France, he said, personality rights are taken more seriously.

Delacourt said he thought Johansson might send him flowers because he saw the book as a declaration of love for her, but she did not understand. He called it a strange paradox.
