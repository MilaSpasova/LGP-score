About 20 years ago, Larry Pizzi worked in the bicycle business. When he first heard about electric bikes, he asked, “Why would anyone ruin a bike by adding a motor and batteries?”

Many people who love regular bikes still wonder the same thing. Bicycle shops in the US were slow to sell e-bikes, even though e-bikes have existed since the late 1990s. Sales in the US have been small.

Now Pizzi is the CEO of Currie Technologies, the top seller of e-bikes in the US. He thinks this is going to change soon, and other people in the industry agree. Brands like Trek, Raleigh, and Specialized now sell electric bikes too. They think the market will grow fast.

Pizzi said e-bikes are close to becoming common. More companies are joining the e-bike business all the time.

The US is different from many other places. In 2014, almost 32m e-bikes were sold, mostly in China, where people use them to get around. E-bikes are also popular in much of Europe. They are common in the Netherlands and Switzerland. German postal workers use them, and BMW sells one for about $3,000.

E-bikes are not the same as motorcycles or mopeds. A motorcycle or moped depends on the motor. An e-bike is still a bicycle you can pedal, with or without help from an electric motor. Riding one feels like riding a regular bike with a strong wind pushing you from behind. The motor helps you go faster or climb hills, but it usually is not the main power. Unlike mopeds, e-bikes are usually allowed on bike paths, and they cannot go faster than 20mph.

No one knows the exact number of e-bikes sold in the US. There is no official count. Estimates say about 50,000 to 175,000 are sold each year. This is similar to electric car sales in the US, which were 118,000 in 2014. But while many people know cars like the Nissan Leaf, Chevy Volt, and Tesla, few know e-bike companies like Currie, Pedego, or ElectroBike.

To do well in the US, e-bike companies must deal with legal, cultural, and money problems. Some states ban e-bikes, including New York, even though the law is not enforced strongly. Many bike shop owners do not like adding motors to bicycles. One reason is weight. Some e-bikes are close to 30kg.

E-bikes can also cost a lot. Some low-end models cost as little as $700. But Court Rye, who runs ElectricBikeReview.com, says a good e-bike with a good battery usually costs at least $1,500. The most expensive models cost more than twice that.

E-bike companies say they can solve these problems. The technology is getting better, especially the batteries. Don DiCostanza, the founder and CEO of Pedego, says batteries are getting smaller, lighter, more reliable, and they last longer.

Big companies like Bosch and Shimano are entering the e-bike business. This may help bike shops feel better about selling e-bikes. Pizzi says bicycle dealers are paying more attention. Pedego and the startup ElectroBike are not waiting for bike shops. They are opening their own stores. At the same time, people are working to change laws so e-bikes are allowed everywhere.

Another big reason e-bikes may grow is that more cities are building bike lanes and other cycling paths. More people are biking to work. The US Census Bureau said that bike commuters grew from about 488,000 in 2000 to 786,000 in 2012. The report said this was a bigger percentage increase than any other way of getting to work. E-bikes can make commuting easier and more fun, because they help with hills, wind, tiredness, and sweating.

DiCostanza says many Pedego customers are “ageing baby boomers” who want to feel like they did when they were kids. He says many stopped riding because of hills. Pedego has opened nearly 60 stores in the US. It has also sold bikes to tour companies in San Francisco and Washington, DC.

ElectroBike runs 30 stores in Mexico. It opened its first US store in Venice Beach, California in the autumn of 2014. It hopes to grow to 25 US stores in a year. CEO Craig Anderson says the company wants to help reduce traffic, reduce the carbon footprint, and support a healthy lifestyle. He tells customers, “Ride this once and try not to smile.”

Startups like Pedego and ElectroBike must compete with big companies like Trek and Currie. In 2012, Currie was bought by the Accell Group, a public company in the Netherlands. Accell is Europe’s market leader in e-bikes. Accell owns the Raleigh brand and also Haibike, a German electric bike that has won awards.

Pizzi says Accell expects e-bikes to grow in North America. He says baby boomers are still important customers, but more younger people are also using e-bikes for transportation instead of cars.

Accell’s Yuba brand even sells a cargo bike with a stronger motor and a rear rack. Pizzi says you can carry two children or 45kg of shopping. He says it is an alternative to a minivan.
