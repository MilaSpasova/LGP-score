Prince Harry has left Afghanistan after a four-month tour. During this time, he said he had killed insurgents while flying an Apache helicopter. He also talked openly about how hard it is to be a royal who wants a more private life.

He said he does not like or trust some parts of the media. He also said his father often told him to act more like a member of the royal family.

Prince Harry was a commander of an attack helicopter. He said he fired at the Taliban during missions to help troops on the ground and to rescue injured Afghan and NATO people. He said he was doing his job. He also said the helicopter often worked as a warning to stop attacks.

While he was based at Camp Bastion in Helmand Province, he gave interviews about having different roles in life. In the army he was known as Captain Wales. He said he had “three mes”: one in the army, one in his private social life, and one with his family. He said he can switch between them when needed.

He said he sometimes disappointed himself with “laddish” behaviour. He said this was “probably being too much army and not enough prince.” But he also said he should have privacy.

He strongly criticized some newspapers, especially the Sun, the Daily Mail, and the Daily Telegraph. He said he was annoyed by stories that compared his job as an Apache co-pilot gunner to Spitfire crews waiting to fly in the second world war. He said it was not like that.

He said he became suspicious of the media because of how his family was treated when he was very small. He said he still reads stories about himself, but they upset and anger him. He said his father tells him not to read them, and others say the stories are “always rubbish.”

Prince Harry was sent to Afghanistan last September to command a £45m Apache helicopter. During his tour, Apaches supported NATO troops fighting the Taliban. They also flew with British Chinook and US Black Hawk medical helicopters during casualty evacuations.

Four years ago, he had to leave Afghanistan during his first tour after a media embargo was broken by mistake by an Australian magazine. This time, the Ministry of Defence told the public about his deployment, with the understanding that the media would not report on his daily life there. Two-man crews from the BBC, Sky, and ITN each visited once. A photographer and a reporter from the Press Association were embedded on all three visits.

When asked if he felt more comfortable as Captain Wales than Prince Harry, he said yes. He said his father often reminds him who he is, but in the army it is easy to forget because everyone wears the same uniform and does the same work. He said he gets on well with the other soldiers and enjoys his job.

Before he went to Afghanistan, there was another media storm after pictures appeared of him naked at a private party in Las Vegas. Harry said he let himself, his family, and others down. But he said he was in a private area and should have expected some privacy. He said it was another example of being “too much army, and not enough prince.” He also said he did not think the way the papers treated him was acceptable, because they already knew he was going to Afghanistan. He said some people remind him to “Remember who you are” and not to drop his guard.

When asked why he and his brother like helicopters, he said it might be because only a few people can fit in one, so no one can follow them “like you guys.”
