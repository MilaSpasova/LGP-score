Top water scientists gave a very serious warning about the world’s food supply. They say that by 2050, people may need to eat almost completely vegetarian food to stop very large food shortages.

Today, humans get about 20% of their protein from animal products. The scientists say this may need to drop to only 5% to feed the extra two billion people expected by 2050.

A report by Malik Falkenmark and coworkers at the Stockholm International Water Institute (SIWI) says there will not be enough water on today’s croplands to feed about nine billion people in 2050 if diets keep moving toward western-style eating. The report says there will be just enough water if animal-based foods are limited to 5% of total calories and if food trade can reliably move food from places with enough water to places with water shortages.

These warnings come as Oxfam and the UN prepare for a possible second global food crisis in five years. Prices for basic foods like corn and wheat have risen almost 50% on world markets since June. This was caused by severe droughts in the US and Russia, and weak monsoon rains in Asia. More than 18 million people already face serious food shortages in the Sahel.

Oxfam says the higher prices will badly hurt developing countries that depend a lot on imported food. This includes parts of Latin America, North Africa, and the Middle East. Food shortages in 2008 led to civil unrest in 28 countries.

The scientists say eating vegetarian is one way to use water better in a world with more unpredictable climate. Foods high in animal protein can use five to ten times more water than a vegetarian diet. One third of the world’s farm land is used to grow crops to feed animals. Other ways to feed people include cutting food waste and increasing trade between countries with extra food and countries that do not have enough.

The scientists also say that 900 million people already go hungry, and two billion people are malnourished, even though food production per person keeps rising. They say 70% of all available water is used in agriculture. Growing enough food for two billion more people by 2050 will put more pressure on water and land.

The report is being released at the start of the yearly world water conference in Stockholm, Sweden. At the meeting, 2,500 politicians, UN groups, non-governmental groups, and researchers from 120 countries come together to talk about world water supply problems.

The scientists say competition for water between growing food and other uses will increase. The report says the UN predicts we must increase food production by 70% by mid-century. This will add pressure to already stressed water resources. At the same time, the world also needs more water for energy needs. Global energy demand is expected to rise by 60% over the next 30 years. The report also says we need to produce electricity for the 1.3 billion people who do not have it now.

The report says overeating, poor nutrition, and waste are all increasing, and water shortages may limit future food production.

“We will need a new recipe to feed the world in the future,” said the report’s editor, Anders Jägerskog.

A different report from the International Water Management Institute (IWMI) says the best way to protect millions of farmers in sub-Saharan Africa and south Asia from food insecurity is to help them buy small pumps and simple technology, instead of building expensive, large irrigation projects.

Dr Colin Chartres, the Director General, said the world’s poor often depend on farming for their income and already suffer from water shortages. He said farmers in developing countries are using small, local water solutions more and more. He said these methods could raise crop yields up to 300% and add tens of billions of US dollars to household incomes in sub-Saharan Africa and south Asia.
