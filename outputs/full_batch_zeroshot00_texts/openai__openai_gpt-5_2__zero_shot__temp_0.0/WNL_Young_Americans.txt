McDonald’s is the biggest burger chain in the world. It is also a symbol of American business. But now the company is having trouble. After many years of growing, fewer people are buying its food. McDonald’s said worldwide sales fell by 3.3% in 2013, and the results were called very bad.

McDonald’s has problems in many places. In China, sales fell by 23% after a food scare. News reports said workers at a local supplier talked about using old beef and chicken for food going to McDonald’s and KFC. In Europe, sales fell by 4%, mostly because of trouble in Ukraine and angry feelings in Russia. In Russia, health inspectors are checking about 200 of McDonald’s 450 restaurants. Ten restaurants have been closed.

The biggest problem is in the US, where McDonald’s has about 40% of its restaurants. Almost 60 years after Ray Kroc opened his first restaurant in Des Plaines, Illinois, Americans are not as interested in Big Macs and fries.

In the US, sales have gone down for 12 months in a row. Sales were down 4.1% in the latest quarter. Many younger people are choosing other places, like Chipotle Mexican Grill. Technomic said visits by 19-to-21-year-olds who go once a month fell by 13% since 2011. Visits by 22-to-37-year-olds have not grown.

McDonald’s also got bad news in a poll of more than 32,000 American diners. People voted McDonald’s hamburgers as the worst in America. They said they would rather eat burgers at Five Guys, Smashburger, or Fuddruckers. Chick-fil-A was seen as best for chicken. Many people also think McDonald’s is less healthy than rivals, especially Chipotle. Chipotle talks about antibiotic-free meat and “locally sourced, seasonal produce,” though “local” for Chipotle can mean 350 miles away.

In October, McDonald’s started a website in the US called Our Food Your Questions. It asked people to send questions. One question was, “Have you ever used pink slime in your burgers?” This meant a beef filler made from gristle and fat. It was sprayed with ammonia to make it safe to eat. This product has been banned in Europe since the BSE crisis. McDonald’s stopped using it in 2012.

Even so, many people still worry. Someone asked, “Does McDonald’s beef contain worms?” McDonald’s answered, “No. Gross. End of story.” Others asked if it uses real eggs, or if the McRib is made with the same plastic used in yoga mats. Chief Executive Don Thompson said the company must fix wrong ideas about how fresh and good its ingredients are.

At the same time, McDonald’s is losing another strength: fast service at low prices. After it added $2 items to the dollar menu, some people started to think it costs more than other fast-food places. Many people also say service is slower.

Mary Chapman at Technomic said the “expensive” idea is not true. Prices at McDonald’s have gone up, but not as fast as at many other fast-service chains. Technomic said McDonald’s prices rose 4.8% since 2009. The “quick service” sector average rose 19.4%. “Fast casual” eating, including Chipotle, rose 16.9%. US consumer prices rose 11% in the same time.

But waiting in line is a real problem. McDonald’s has a large menu with more complex items. A chicken McWrap takes 60 seconds to make. Chapman said it might be worth waiting, but someone who wants a double cheeseburger for a dollar may not want to wait.

In the UK, McDonald’s has done better. Britain is a bright spot for the company. Peter Martin of CGA Peach said a strong breakfast menu, better coffee, and free wi-fi helped. His numbers show that 56% of British adults visited McDonald’s at least once in the last six months.

In the US, executives say they will fight wrong ideas about the food. Thompson promised more organic food and custom-made burgers. But he also wants simpler menus to make lines shorter. Some analysts wonder how McDonald’s can have simpler menus and also more choices.

Mark Kalinowski at Janney Capital Markets said this plan sounds hard. He said only four of McDonald’s 14,000 US restaurants had tested “build your own burger.” He questioned how it could be used everywhere. He said he is not sure yet and wants more details.

Even with falling sales, McDonald’s is still growing around the world. By the end of 2014, it expects to open 1,400 new restaurants. Kalinowski thinks McDonald’s share of the market will keep shrinking, but he warned people not to give up on the company. He said McDonald’s will likely stay number one in total sales for many years, even decades.
