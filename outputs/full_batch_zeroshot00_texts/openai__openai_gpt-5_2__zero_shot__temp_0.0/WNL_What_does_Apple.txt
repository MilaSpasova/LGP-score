Is streaming music becoming popular with everyone?
The International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI) said that in 2014 there were only 41m people in the world paying for music streaming. Streaming is growing fast, but it is still used by a small group. Also, many people get streaming because it comes in a phone deal, so it is not clear how many are really active users.
Apple often takes something that already exists, changes it, and makes many people want it. This happened with downloads (iTunes), digital music players (the iPod), and smartphones (the iPhone). Leaked information said Apple wants 100m subscribers. If each person pays $120 per year, that would be $12bn each year. In 2014, the total value of recorded music worldwide was just under $15bn. Apple is good at making products popular, but reaching that goal would still be very hard.

Is downloading ending?
The iTunes Store started in 2003 (2004 in Europe). At that time, MP3 piracy seemed impossible to stop. Apple convinced people to pay for downloads and built a very big business, with an estimated 70% market share. Downloads were still the biggest part of digital music money in 2014. IFPI said downloads were 52% of total digital income in 2014. Apple has most of this market, and it is the biggest music retailer in the world.
But download money is falling. In the UK, download revenue peaked in 2013 at £283m and fell to £249m in 2014. The same decline reached the US in 2013. So Apple bought Beats in 2014 for $3bn. It wanted premium headphones, and it also wanted to move from music ownership (downloads) to music access (subscription streaming).
Apple and the record industry cannot end downloads yet. So streaming and downloading will both stay under the Apple brand. Many people like music, but do not want to pay $120 a year. They prefer to buy a download sometimes. Research said the average music buyer in the UK spent £39.52 in 2014. Asking most people to triple what they spend each year would be very difficult, even for Apple.

Has Apple Connect made Apple the most artist-friendly service?
Apple tried an artist-friendly platform on iTunes before. It was called Ping, and it failed. Apple Connect is different. It is like a mix of YouTube, Facebook, and SoundCloud. Artists can post music, videos, photos, and more on their profile pages.
Because of its size, and because it treats music as “art” and not just “content,” Apple has often had good relationships with the music industry and with artists. Many artists see Apple in a positive way. Spotify has been criticized by artists, from Radiohead’s Thom Yorke to Taylor Swift. Apple seems friendlier in comparison. Apple wants to be on the right side as the industry changes.

Where are the artist exclusives?
This will matter when the service starts. Exclusive deals for big albums may become very important for streaming. Spotify paid a lot of money to get Led Zeppelin and Metallica exclusively. Apple has watched this.
Apple already has AC/DC and the Beatles’ catalogues exclusively for download on iTunes. It is not clear if it can convince them to join streaming too. Apple also got the surprise Beyonce album in 2013 before anyone else, and it will want more deals like that. People also speculate that Apple wants exclusive rights to the new Adele album.
Giving iTunes a download exclusive was easier because Apple controls so much of the download market. Doing this in streaming is harder. Also, streaming now counts toward the album chart in places like the UK and US. Many artists care about chart success and will not want to limit their audience by being on only one service.

Is this going to kill Spotify?
Some people say Apple Music will destroy Spotify and other rivals. But it is not that simple. Apple is joining a market where other companies have worked for years and learned a lot. Apple has to catch up.
The winner may not be the company with the best service. It may be the company with the most money. Apple’s rivals started earlier, but they are losing a lot of money. Spotify, for example, lost Û93.1m in 2013. Apple started 2015 as the most profitable company in corporate history, with $178bn in the bank. If Apple Music loses too much money, Apple will not keep it for long. But Apple will keep investing for a while and will try to beat the competition.
