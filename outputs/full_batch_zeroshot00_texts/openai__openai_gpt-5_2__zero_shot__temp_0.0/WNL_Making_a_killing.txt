James Bond is the hero of one of the longest and most successful movie series in the world. There are 23 Bond movies, and they have made more than $6bn worldwide. The Bond movies are still very popular.

The movie Skyfall did extremely well. When it came out in 2012, it became the biggest film ever at the UK box office. It earned $1.1bn worldwide and is now ninth on the list of the biggest-earning films of all time.

Because of Skyfall, many people were excited to hear news about the 24th Bond film. Its title is Spectre. People ask: can Spectre be as successful as Skyfall? Will Skyfall stay the best, or can Spectre do even better?

Charles Gant, film editor for Heat magazine, says things look good. He says Skyfall was a smart movie because it felt modern and also old-fashioned. It pleased newer fans from the Daniel Craig era and also older fans who missed the classic Bond style. Gant thinks the title Spectre shows they want to keep older fans and also keep younger fans.

Eon Productions makes the “official” Bond films. The company was started in 1961 to make Dr No. Eon is trying hard to make Spectre another big success by keeping important people from Skyfall. Daniel Craig will play Bond at least until Bond 25, and the same writers wrote the script.

Many Bond fans are happy that the director Sam Mendes is back. Mendes is known for movies like American Beauty and Revolutionary Road, but he helped the Bond series reach a new high level. Gant says Mendes connected with both modern and traditional Bond fans. He also brought in famous actors like Ralph Fiennes. Big actors often want a director they respect.

Skyfall almost doubled the worldwide box office of the movie before it, Quantum of Solace. Quantum of Solace made $586m worldwide in 2008. No one expects Spectre to double Skyfall, but Eon still wants to do very well.

Before Mendes and Craig, the Bond series was not failing, but it started to feel a bit tired near the end of Pierce Brosnan’s time as Bond. The movies were no longer close to the original Ian Fleming books, and the series needed more energy.

The Bond series has always gone up and down. It often changes when a new actor plays Bond. The main Bond actors have been Sean Connery, George Lazenby, Roger Moore, Timothy Dalton, Pierce Brosnan, and now Daniel Craig. Some choices worked better than others.

Lazenby made only one Bond film. Dalton made two: The Living Daylights and Licence to Kill. These came out in the late 1980s, when other Hollywood action movies were very strong and exciting. Gant says audiences were not very excited by the last Roger Moore films and then Dalton’s films. Brosnan helped the series rise again in money made, and Craig has taken it to even higher levels. Gant also says the early Bond films were very successful, sexy, and exciting, and there were few movies like them at the time.

Even though Skyfall made more money than any other Bond movie, some older Bond films were almost as successful when you adjust for inflation. Thunderball (1965) is very close to Skyfall by that measure. Goldfinger and You Only Live Twice did better than the other Craig films. Two 1970s Bond films, The Spy Who Loved Me and Live and Let Die, also did better than the other Craig films. By this measure, Licence to Kill is the lowest-earning Bond film. Roger Moore’s last film, A View to a Kill, is second from the bottom.

Still, the Bond brand has stayed very strong for many years. Eon had to fight off other groups that wanted to use Bond’s popularity. Because of a strange situation with rights, other versions of Casino Royale (in 1967) and Thunderball (as Never Say Never Again, in 1983) were released to compete with Eon’s Bond films. After many legal fights over decades, Eon now fully controls both books.

The rights to Casino Royale were sold separately by Fleming in 1955. They finally went to Eon in 1999 because of an agreement between MGM (which supports Eon) and Sony. This allowed the 2006 version to be made. Thunderball had problems because writer-producer Kevin McClory helped Fleming plan the story and said he owned the novel. That led to McClory making Never Say Never Again. After McClory died in 2006, his family made a deal with MGM and Eon’s parent company in November 2013.

Bond movies were very popular in the 1960s and into the 1970s. But after Licence to Kill made little money, the series nearly stopped. Goldeneye, Brosnan’s first Bond film, brought the series back strongly. This was not only because of the new actor, but also because of the people making the movie.

After a six-year break, Eon chose director Martin Campbell. He was an experienced British director, and he created one of the most complex stunts in Bond history. In the famous opening of Goldeneye, Bond falls through the air into the cockpit of a small plane with no pilot. This helped make the series feel exciting and modern again. Also, the Goldeneye video game was very successful and reached a new generation. It helped people feel that Bond was not stale or old-fashioned anymore.
