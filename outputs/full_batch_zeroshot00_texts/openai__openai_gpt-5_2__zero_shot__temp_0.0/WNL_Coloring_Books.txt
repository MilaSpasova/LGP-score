The top-selling book on Amazon in the US is not Harper Lee’s second novel, Go Set a Watchman. It is not George RR Martin’s Song of Ice and Fire series. It is not Zoella’s Girl Online. Instead, a Scottish illustrator named Johanna Basford is number one with colouring books for adults. Her books are taking top places on Amazon.com’s bestseller lists.

Basford’s book Secret Garden has very detailed drawings of plants and animals. It has sold 1.4 million copies worldwide so far. Her new book, Enchanted Forest, has already sold just under 226,000 copies. Famous people also like the books. Zooey Deschanel shared a link about the book on Facebook. The South Korean pop star Kim Ki-Bum posted a picture on Instagram for his 1.6 million followers.

“It’s been crazy. The last few weeks since Enchanted Forest came out have been utter madness, but fantastic madness,” said Eleanor Blatherwick. She is head of sales and marketing at the publisher, a small British press called Laurence King. “We knew the books would be beautiful but we didn’t realize it would be such a phenomenal success.”

Basford is not the only person doing well from this. Many adults want something to colour. In the UK, Richard Merritt’s Art Therapy Colouring Book is fourth on Amazon’s bestseller lists. Millie Marotta’s Animal Kingdom is seventh. A mindfulness colouring book is ninth. Basford’s books are second and eighth. That means 5 of Amazon.co.uk’s top ten are colouring books for adults.

At the UK publisher Michael O’Mara, they have sold almost 340,000 adult colouring books so far. Ana McLaughlin, Head of Publicity, Marketing and Online, says the craze is happening because people now see these books as a way to relax. “The first one we did was in 2012, Creative Colouring for Grown-Ups. It sold strongly and reprinted but it was in 2014 that it all really mushroomed with Art Therapy. It really took off for us – selling the anti-stress angle gave people permission to enjoy something they might have felt was quite childish,” she said.

The Mindfulness Colouring Book pushes this idea strongly. Its publisher tells readers it is “filled with templates for exquisite scenes and intricate, sophisticated patterns,” and says it helps you meditate while you colour. It also tells people to “take a few minutes out of your day, wherever you are, and colour your way to peace and calm”.

“I think it is really relaxing to do something analogue, to unplug,” said Basford. “And it’s creative. For many people, a blank sheet is very daunting; with a colouring book, you just need to bring the colour. Also, there’s a bit of nostalgia there. So many people have said to me that they used to do secret colouring in when their kids were in bed. Now, it is socially acceptable; it’s a category of its own. These are books for adults. The art in my books is super intricate.”

Basford lives in Aberdeenshire. She has been surprised by the reaction since Secret Garden came out in 2013. “I had a kids’ book commissioned and I told them I would like to do one for grown-ups. It really wasn’t a trend then. I drew up the first story and they thought, ‘Let’s go for it’. I was thinking simply that people like me would like to do it. My intention was just to make a book I would like to have. So it’s been a real surprise to see the category bloom.”

Basford is now working on a third book. Michael O’Mara already has 17 adult colouring books for sale, and will increase this to 22 by May. New titles will include The Classic Comic Colouring Book and The Typography Colouring Book. “It’s just an enormous trend and shows no signs of slowing down,” said McLaughlin. She said buyers also like to show other people that they can colour neatly inside the lines.

“The pictures are all over Twitter and Instagram. People are really proud of them – they are so intricate,” she said. “You don’t have to have any artistic talent but what you create is unique. People send us pictures of them, framed and laminated. The appetite is simply enormous. I reckon people are taking their kids’ pictures off the fridge and replacing them with their own.”
