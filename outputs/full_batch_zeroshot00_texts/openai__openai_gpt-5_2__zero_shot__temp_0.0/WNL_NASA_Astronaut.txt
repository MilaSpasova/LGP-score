Astronaut Scott Kelly told reporters how he stayed calm during 340 days in space. He said he looked at Himalayan lakes, did spacewalks, and followed the US presidential primaries. After he landed back on Earth from a record-setting mission, he said, “It seemed like I lived there forever.” He said the biggest surprise was how long it felt. He also said that sometimes you “go bananas.”

Kelly and a Russian astronaut, Mikhail Kornienko, spent almost a year on the International Space Station (ISS). They did this to study how weightlessness, radiation, and small living spaces affect people. NASA says this research is needed for a future mission to Mars.

Kelly said the hardest part was the long time in space. He said he felt much more sore when he came back to Earth than after shorter trips. Kelly and his twin brother, Mark, who is a retired astronaut, have been doing physical and mental tests for the last year. The tests will continue so NASA can learn how the body handles the stress of space flight.

Kelly said he had aches and very sensitive skin. So far, he said his balance has been mostly okay. But he said the first thing he tried to throw on a table, he missed, because you can underestimate gravity.

He said coming back to gravity was uncomfortable. In space, he grew 1.5 inches taller, and then returned to his normal height on Earth. Still, he said he felt amazed after his capsule landed. When the Russian capsule opened in the cool air of Kazakhstan, he said he smelled a fragrance like a plant was blooming. He said this fresh air mixed with the burned, kind of sweet smell of the spacecraft after it came back through the atmosphere.

As he moved away from the capsule, he said he started to understand what the mission meant: 340 days on a 15-year-old space station. He said the ISS is a million pounds and the size of a football field. He said the inside space is, some say, like a six-bedroom house. He said the ISS uses power from the sun and was built with help from an international team and millions of taxpayers.

Kelly said people will discover new things from life and work on the space station that they do not even know yet. He compared the research from more than 450 missions to the work done by NASA computer scientists in the 1960s and 1970s. He also said, “The view is great, too.”

Kelly shared many photos online of Earth’s cities, land, oceans, and atmosphere. He said, “The Earth is a beautiful planet.” He described the bright colors near the Bahamas and the rainbow colors of lakes in the northern Himalayas. He said he would like to visit that area, but first he would need to learn what country owns the lakes. He said you mostly notice how thin and fragile the atmosphere looks. He said that, along with large areas of pollution, is alarming.

He said he could see pollution systems from space. He could see smoke clouds from wildfires over parts of the US. He could also see parts of Asia with pollution that was visible almost all year. He said the message “we need to save the planet” misses the point. He said the planet will get better, but humans might not be here because humans could destroy the environment. He said seeing the thin atmosphere can make you more of an environmentalist.

He said people need to take care of the air they breathe and the water they drink. He said he believes people affect this and can change it if they decide to.

Kelly said his social media work was done together with his girlfriend, and it gave him many followers. But he said he did not know much about his popularity. Instead, he watched the 2016 presidential election news. One of the first questions he asked the crew that helped him out of the capsule was, “How did Super Tuesday go?”

But he did not share his opinions about Donald Trump and Bernie Sanders. He said that as a government employee he must follow the Hatch Act, a law that stops some political activity by federal employees.

He said doing steady work helped him stay sane. He said he set small goals, like the next crew arriving, the next spacewalk, the next robotics work, and the next science experiment. He said that helped his sanity. He said being back on Earth with everyone had not fully felt real yet. He remembered being shocked to see a crowd after a past mission. He said he might feel culture shock soon.

A NASA scientist said Kelly showed the qualities needed for a mission to Mars. But Kelly said he probably will not fly again with NASA. Still, he said he would not say he is 100% done. He mentioned the success of private spaceflight companies like SpaceX. He said they might need someone like him someday. He also said that maybe in the next 20 years, people could buy a cheap ticket and go for a short visit to space.
