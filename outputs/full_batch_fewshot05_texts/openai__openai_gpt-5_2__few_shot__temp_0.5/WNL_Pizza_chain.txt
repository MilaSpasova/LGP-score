Angry waiters are asking the public to support them in a fight to keep their tips. Protesters plan to target PizzaExpress restaurants. They want the company to stop taking part of the tips when customers pay tips by credit or debit card. Campaigners have also started an online petition, hoping customers will support them.

Some workers are upset because PizzaExpress keeps 8p from every £1 of card tips as an “admin fee” (an 8% fee). PizzaExpress has 430 branches in the UK and is popular with families. The union Unite says the company makes about £1m a year from this.

Chantal Chegrinec from Unite said the 8% fee is unfair. She said that if the company values staff, it should give them all the tips customers leave. She also said PizzaExpress is not the only company doing this, and Unite will look at other companies later.

Local Unite branches are organizing the protests. The first protest will be at the PizzaExpress branch at the British Museum in London. Unite has also written to PizzaExpress CEO Richard Hodgson. Unite started the campaign after a survey of PizzaExpress union members, after the company was sold to a Chinese private equity firm, Hony Capital, in 2014. One of the main issues was the 8% taken from tips.

One unhappy PizzaExpress worker, who did not want to give her name, said the fee costs her £3 each night. She said she has worked at PizzaExpress for 15 years and is still paid the national minimum wage of £6.50 an hour. She said she and her co-workers depend on tips to add to their low wages. She said she works hard, so she gets upset when PizzaExpress takes a percentage of tips paid by card.

PizzaExpress is not the only restaurant chain that takes 8% from card tips. Ask and Zizzi do the same. Other chains take more. Cafe Rouge, Bella Italia, and Belgo take 10%. Strada and Giraffe also take 10%. Giraffe is owned by Tesco.

A PizzaExpress spokesperson said the admin charge covers the cost of running a “tronc.” This is a common system for sharing tips among staff. She said the tronc is led by a “troncmaster” and run by a group of waiters and pizzaiolos. They decide how card tips are shared between front-of-house and back-of-house teams. She said it is run by employees for employees.

PizzaExpress sells 29m pizzas a year in the UK. The company said it does not make profit from the admin fee. But some other restaurant groups do not take any admin fee from tips. Wagamama, Pizza Hut, and TGI Friday take nothing. The Restaurant Group, which owns Frankie & Benny’s, Chiquitos, and Garfunkels, used to take 10% but stopped several years ago.

Unite recently targeted ten PizzaExpress restaurants in south London and gave leaflets to customers. Customers were said to be “shocked and disgusted.” PizzaExpress says the charge is written in small print at the bottom of its menus. But the worker said customers were always surprised when she told them. Many then paid tips in cash. Nearly 6,000 people have signed Unite’s online petition.

One waiter, who does not work for PizzaExpress but has worked for another chain for 11 years, said tips are at least a third of his income. He did not want to be named because he feared punishment. He said he works in a busy London branch and on an average night serves 150 people and takes home £40 to £50 in tips. He said this money is very important because his basic pay is only £6.50 an hour.

Conservative MP Andrew Percy has called for a change in the law so restaurant staff have more control over tips. He said he plans to raise the issue in parliament after the summer recess.
