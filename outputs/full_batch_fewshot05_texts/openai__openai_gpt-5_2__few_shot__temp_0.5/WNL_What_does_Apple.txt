Is streaming becoming popular with most people?
The International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI) said that in 2014 only 41 million people worldwide paid for music streaming services. Streaming may be the fastest-growing way for record companies to make money, but it was still for a small group of users. Also, many people got streaming through mobile phone bundle deals, so it is unclear how many were really “active” users.
Apple often takes something that already exists, like downloads (iTunes), digital music players (the iPod), and smartphones (the iPhone), and changes it so that many people want it. Leaked information said Apple wanted 100 million subscribers. If each person pays $120 per year, that would be $12 billion each year. In 2014, the total global value of recorded music was just under $15 billion. Apple is good at making products popular, but it may not be able to reach that level.

Is downloading ending?
The iTunes Store started in 2003 (2004 in Europe), when MP3 piracy seemed impossible to stop. Apple convinced people to pay for downloads and built a very large business. It had about 70% market share. Downloads still brought in the biggest part of digital money for recorded music. IFPI said downloads were 52% of total digital income in 2014. Apple has the biggest share of downloads, so it is the biggest music retailer in the world.
But download money started to fall. In the UK it reached a high point in 2013 at £283m, then fell to £249m in 2014. The drop started in the US in 2013. So Apple bought Beats in 2014 for $3bn. This was to enter the premium headphone market, and also to move from owning music (downloads) to accessing music (subscription streaming).
Apple and the record industry cannot end downloads yet. Streaming and downloading will need to exist together under the Apple brand. Many people like music but do not want to pay $120 a year. For them, buying a download sometimes is enough. In the UK in 2014, the average music buyer spent £39.52. Expecting most people to spend three times more each year will be very hard, even for Apple.

Is Apple Connect the most friendly service for artists?
Apple tried an artist-friendly platform on iTunes before. It was called Ping, and it failed. Apple Connect is different. It is between YouTube, Facebook, and SoundCloud. Artists can post music, videos, photos, and more on their profile pages.
Apple has often had good relationships with the music industry and with artists. Partly this is because Apple is very large, and partly because it treats music as “art” rather than just “content.” Many artists have criticized Spotify, from Radiohead’s Thom Yorke to Taylor Swift. Compared to Spotify, Apple seems kinder. Apple wants to be on the right side if the industry changes in a big way.

Where are the exclusive deals for artists?
This will matter when the service begins. Getting exclusive big albums will become important for streaming. Spotify paid a lot of money to get Led Zeppelin and Metallica exclusively. Apple already has AC/DC and the Beatles catalogues only for download on iTunes. But it is not clear if it can convince them to also do streaming. Apple also got the surprise Beyonce album in 2013 before anyone else, and it will want more deals like that. Some people think Apple wants exclusive rights to the new Adele album.
Giving iTunes a download exclusive was easier because Apple controls so much of the download market. But doing the same thing for streaming is harder. Also, streaming now counts for album charts in places like the UK and US. Many artists still care about the charts, so they may not want to limit their audience by being on only one service.

Will this kill Spotify?
Some people say Apple Music will destroy competitors like Spotify. But it is not so simple. Apple is entering a market where other companies have worked for many years and learned a lot. Apple must catch up. In the end, the winner may not be the company with the best service, but the one with the most money.
Apple’s competitors started earlier, but they are losing a lot of money. Spotify lost Û93.1m in 2013. Apple became the most profitable company in corporate history at the start of 2015, and it had $178bn in the bank. If Apple Music loses Apple too much money, Apple will not keep it for long. But Apple will not stop investing without trying to beat the competition first.
