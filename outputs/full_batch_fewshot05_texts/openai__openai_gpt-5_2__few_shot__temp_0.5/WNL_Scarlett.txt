Scarlett Johansson is suing a French novelist. She wants 50,000 euros in damages. She says his fiction book makes false claims about her private life.

The book is called La Premiere chose qu’on regarde (The First Thing We Look At). It is by Gregoire Delacourt. It is about a French model who looks very much like Johansson. A man in the story thinks the model is really Johansson.

In the novel, men treat the model like a sex object, and women feel jealous. The model has many adventures while pretending to be Johansson. Later people learn the truth. At the end, she dies in a car crash.

Johansson is unhappy about the best-selling book. Her lawyer, Vincent Toledano, said the novel uses her name and image in an illegal way. He said the book also says bad things about her private life. They went to court to try to stop the book from being translated or made into a movie. The case began in Paris on Wednesday afternoon. Johansson and Delacourt were not there.

Toledano said freedom of expression is not the main issue. He said this is being done only for money, not creativity.

Delacourt said he chose Johansson because she is “the archetype of beauty today.” He said it is a work of fiction and the character is not Scarlett Johansson.

On French radio, Delacourt said the lawsuit is “rather sad.” He said it scares him that judges can get involved in a novel’s characters.

Delacourt is one of France’s best-loved authors. His earlier novel, My List of Desires, was translated into 47 languages and is being made into a film. He said he was “speechless” when he learned Johansson was suing him.

He said he thought Johansson might contact him for coffee. He said he did not write a novel about a celebrity. He said it is a love story and a tribute to feminine beauty, especially inner beauty.

He said if writers cannot mention things around us, like a beer brand, a monument, or an actor, writing fiction will be difficult. He also said it is shocking, because he is not sure Johansson has even read the novel, since it has not been translated yet.

Emmanuelle Allibert, a spokeswoman for the publisher JC Lattes, said the lawsuit is “crazy.” She said the novel is not about Scarlett Johansson. She said it is about a woman who looks like her.

Delacourt’s lawyer, Anne Veil, is also representing the publisher JC Lattes.

A New York lawyer, Lloyd Jassin, said the case would probably not happen in the United States. He said the book would be protected by the First Amendment. He said the First Amendment usually does not treat most books as commercial use. If the use of her name has literary value, the First Amendment protects it. But in France, the law is more complicated, and personality rights are taken more seriously.

Delacourt said he thought Johansson might send him flowers because he saw the book as a declaration of love for her. He said she did not understand. He called it a strange paradox, and said it is a very American one.
