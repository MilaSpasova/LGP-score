JMW Turner is one of Britain’s greatest painters. He will be on the new £20 note, after a nationwide vote.

This is the first time an artist will appear on a British banknote. The governor of the Bank of England, Mark Carney, asked the public to nominate a deceased cultural figure for this honour.

Turner is famous for dramatic sea pictures. He beat 590 other people, including painters, sculptors, fashion designers, photographers, film-makers, and actors. About 30,000 people sent in names.

The list included Alfred Hitchcock, Alexander McQueen, Derek Jarman, Laura Ashley, William Morris, and Vanessa Bell. A panel of artists, critics, and historians cut the list down to five.

The final five were Barbara Hepworth, Charlie Chaplin, Josiah Wedgwood, William Hogarth, and Turner. They were chosen because of their strong contribution to visual arts and British society, and their lasting influence.

The announcement was made at the Turner Contemporary gallery in Margate. It stands on the old site of Mrs Booth’s lodging house, where Turner always stayed when he visited. Mark Carney and the artist Tracey Emin, who grew up in the town, made the announcement together.

Carney said it was important to do this carefully and involve the public. He said banknotes are not only practical, and if done well they can be a piece of art that everyone carries.

Carney also said money is a country’s memory. Bank of England banknotes celebrate the UK’s heritage and culture, and its great achievements and people. He said money has cultural value as well as economic value.

Emin said that putting Turner on the £20 note shows that British people value creativity and the arts.

The note will show Turner’s 1799 self-portrait, which is in Tate Britain. It will also show his famous painting The Fighting Temeraire. This painting is about a ship that played an important role in Nelson’s victory at the Battle of Trafalgar in 1805.

The note will also have Turner’s quote, “light is therefore colour,” and his signature from his will. In his will, he left many of his works to the nation.

The new £20 note will replace the one with Adam Smith. It will be used by 2020. This is the first time the public has had a say about whose face is on a British banknote.

Carney changed the process in 2013. This happened after a campaign to keep women, besides the Queen, on banknotes. The Bank had decided to remove the Elizabeth Fry £5 note.

Historical figures first appeared on banknotes in 1970. Turner joins Winston Churchill and Jane Austen on the new polymer notes. Polymer is a plastic-type material used for the new notes. Churchill is on the £5 note and Austen is on the £10 note. Before Austen, only Florence Nightingale and Elizabeth Fry were women on British money, apart from the Queen. So Austen is the third.

Turner was born in 1775 in London. His father was a barber. Turner entered the Royal Academy Schools at age 14. In 1786 he was sent to Margate, and he began to love painting and drawing the north-east Kent coast. He returned there many times and painted some of his most dramatic oil paintings and watercolours. He called its skies “the loveliest in all Europe”.

Turner made a lot of art. He produced more than 550 oil paintings and 2,000 watercolours. His life was also shown in a 2014 film by Mike Leigh, with Timothy Spall playing Turner.

Victoria Pomery, the director of Turner Contemporary, said the choice to celebrate Turner is extraordinary. She said the public vote shows Turner is the nation’s favourite artist. She said it is an official recognition of the great historical value of visual arts to the UK.

She also said it reminds the country to value creativity in the future. She said the UK must invest to support the next great artists and innovators.
