Independent data suggests Facebook is losing millions of users each month in its biggest markets. Other social networks are attracting people who want something new.

Facebook will soon tell investors how it did in the first three months of the year. Analysts expect revenues to be 36% higher than last year. But studies suggest that Facebook’s growth in the US, UK, and other big European countries has reached its highest point.

In the last month, Facebook lost 6m visitors in the US, a 4% drop, says the analysis firm Socialbakers. In the UK, 1.4m fewer users used Facebook in March, a 4.5% drop. This drop has continued. In the last six months, Facebook lost almost 9m monthly visitors in the US and 2m in the UK.

Users are also leaving in Canada, Spain, France, Germany, and Japan. A Facebook spokeswoman did not comment.

Ian Maude from Enders Analysis said that in the US and UK, most people who want Facebook already have it. He said some people get bored and want to try something new. He asked if Facebook could end up like MySpace. He said the risk is quite small, but it still exists.

Other social networks are growing with younger people. One is Instagram, a photo-sharing site. It got 30m users in 18 months before Facebook bought it.

Path is a mobile phone social network started by former Facebook employee Dave Morin. It limits users to 150 friends. It is gaining 1m users each week. It recently reached 9m users. In one weekend, 500,000 Venezuelans downloaded the app.

Facebook is still growing fast in South America. In Brazil, monthly visitors rose 6% in the last month to 70m, says Socialbakers. In India, visitors rose 4% to 64m. This is still a small part of India’s population, so there is room to grow.

But in developed markets, other trackers also report declines. Analysts at Jefferies bank use an algorithm connected to Facebook software. It suggests that user levels in the first quarter may have fallen from their peak.

Jefferies said global numbers peaked at 1.05bn a month in January, then fell by 20m in February. Numbers rose again in April. Research firm Nielsen says Facebook has lost almost 2m visitors in the UK since December. It says the total is 27m, about the same as a year earlier.

Americans also seem to spend less time on Facebook. comScore says the total was 121 billion minutes in December 2012, but it fell to 115 billion minutes in February.

Facebook has warned that time on its pages from personal computers is falling quickly. People are using smartphones and tablets more.

Smartphone minutes have doubled in a year to 69 a month. But this growth may not fully make up for the drop in desktop use.

Facebook is the main source for its own user numbers, and it will update investors about the quarter. Wall Street expects revenues of about $1.44bn, up from $1.06bn in 2012.

Shareholders especially want to know how fast Facebook’s mobile user base is growing, and if advertising revenues are growing at the same speed.

At the end of 2012, mobile use made up almost a quarter of Facebook’s advertising income. In December, Facebook had 680m mobile users each month.

In recent stockmarket filings, the company warned it might be losing “younger users” to “other products and services similar to, or as a substitute for, Facebook”.

Founder Mark Zuckerberg is worried about competition from services made for mobile phones, not PCs. He has pushed new plans to attract smartphone users. The biggest one is Facebook Home. It is software that some Android phone users can download. It shows news and photos from friends, and also advertising, on the phone’s locked home screen.
