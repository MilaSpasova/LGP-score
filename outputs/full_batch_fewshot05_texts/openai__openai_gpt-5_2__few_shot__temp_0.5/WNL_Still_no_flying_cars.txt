We are not living in a time of flying cars, like the 1985 film Back to the Future II predicted. But smartphones and new technology are changing transportation in exciting ways. Experts say changes in the economy and population, new technology, and worries about the environment are changing how people travel. Robert Puentes from the Brookings Institution’s Metropolitan Policy Program said this is a very active time. He said transportation is closely tied to the economic health of cities and affects people.

In the US, the average trip to work is getting longer, and old roads and bridges are in bad condition. Because of this, many US cities must rethink what transportation means. Planners and scientists say old car-focused ideas that only try to reduce traffic jams are not enough. Cities also need to deal with population growth and carbon emissions. Transportation is now a key part of a city’s plans for sustainability.

Big cities like Los Angeles, Seattle, and Chicago are trying to use streets better. They are adding more bus lanes, improving sidewalks for people walking, and expanding rail options. They are also working with private companies on technology that lets a vehicle drive itself and communicate with other vehicles and the environment. Puentes said the most sustainable places to live have many ways to travel. He also said a city cannot compete globally without a strong transportation network.

Experts say three key trends will shape transportation in the next few years.

Connectivity Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft let people book rides with a smartphone. Apps like Waze use real-time traffic data to find the fastest routes. These tools are changing how people move around and how traffic flows in a city. Allan Clelland, a transportation technology expert, says communication between riders and drivers, between vehicles, and between cars and infrastructure is creating a new era.

A UCLA Institute of Transportation Studies study says millennials (born roughly from the early 1980s to the early 2000s) travel less by car than earlier generations. People born in the 1990s make 4% fewer car trips and travel 18% fewer miles per year, on average, than earlier generations did at the same age.

People who still drive may have less traffic because of Waze. Experts say Waze has reduced traffic jams on motorways and shortened travel times. But it has also caused more cars to go through residential neighbourhoods. Some residents are angry because more traffic on quiet roads lowers their quality of life.

This trend may continue as vehicles share more data with each other and with traffic systems. Today, a traffic light can detect that a car is coming, but not much more. Companies are developing technology so a vehicle can tell traffic control systems where it is going and how fast it is travelling.

Automation Driverless cars have been widely discussed since Google began testing them on roads in 2012. But no one knows when they will be common. Still, cars are already becoming partly automated. Alexandre Bayen from the University of California says automation may happen in steps. First, there may be automated buses in special lanes. Later, there may be lorries in ports or mining towns. These vehicles would be electronically connected and travel in a single line.

A fully automated system could improve safety by reducing human error. It could also help car owners use travel time for other tasks. It may reduce carbon emissions and traffic jams, and give more people access to cars. But even if the technology were ready now, it would take a long time to become fully common because the average car on the road is 11.5 years old.

Environmental concern Many places are trying to avoid catastrophic climate change and meet strong emissions goals. These policies could strongly affect transportation. They could encourage zero- and low-emission vehicles and apps that push people to walk, bike, and carpool more.

Sharon Feigon, executive director of the Shared Use Mobility Center, imagines a future with one transit pass for many types of travel in one day. If a train is delayed, a person could use the same pass to take a city bike, a shuttle, or a car from a car-share programme. She said these systems will be put together so it is easy to connect them and move smoothly between them.

When thinking about the future of transportation, it is also important to remember why people travel. They travel to work, to meet friends or family, or to shop. Technology that reduces the need for trips, like virtual meetings or telecommuting, could also change transportation a lot.

Flying cars once seemed like the best symbol of innovation. But today’s new technologies may be even more advanced and more useful for dealing with social and environmental problems in the coming decades.
