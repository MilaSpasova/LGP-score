About 20 years ago, Larry Pizzi, a long-time bicycle business leader, first heard about electric bikes. He asked, “Why would anyone ruin a bike by adding a motor and batteries?”

Many traditional bike riders still wonder about this. Bike shops in the US have been slow to sell e-bikes, even though e-bikes have existed since the late 1990s. US sales have been small.

Now Pizzi is the CEO of Currie Technologies, the top seller of e-bikes in the US. He thinks this will change soon, and other people in the industry agree. Big brands like Trek, Raleigh, and Specialized also sell electric bikes. They believe the market will grow fast.

Pizzi says e-bikes are close to becoming normal for many people. More companies are joining the e-bike business each month.

The US is different from many other places. In 2014, about 32m e-bikes were sold, mostly in China. In China, people mainly use them to travel. E-bikes are also popular in much of Europe. They are common in the Netherlands and Switzerland. German postal workers use them, and BMW sells one for about $3,000.

Electric bikes are not the same as motorcycles or mopeds. E-bikes are bicycles that you can pedal, and the electric motor can help you. Riding one feels like riding a normal bike with a strong wind pushing you from behind. The motor helps you go faster or climb hills, but it is usually not the main power. E-bikes are usually allowed on bike paths, and they cannot go faster than 20mph.

Nobody knows the exact number of e-bikes sold in the US. There is no official count. Estimates say about 50,000 to 175,000 are sold each year. That is similar to electric car sales in the US: 118,000 in 2014. Many people know cars like the Nissan Leaf, Chevy Volt, and Tesla. But few people know e-bike companies like Currie, Pedego, or ElectroBike.

To do well in the US, e-bike companies must deal with legal, cultural, and money problems. Some states ban e-bikes, including New York, but the law is not strictly enforced. Many bike shop owners do not like motors on bicycles. They also say e-bikes are heavier. Some weigh close to 30kg.

E-bikes can also be expensive. Cheap models can cost $700. But Court Rye, the founder and editor of ElectricBikeReview.com, says people should expect to pay at least $1,500 for a good e-bike with a good battery. The best models cost more than twice that.

E-bike companies say they can solve these problems. The technology is getting better, especially the batteries. Don DiCostanza, the founder and CEO of Pedego, says batteries are getting smaller, lighter, more reliable, and they last longer.

More big companies are joining the business, like Bosch and Shimano. This may help bike shops accept e-bikes. Pizzi says this has caught the interest of bicycle dealers. Pedego and the startup ElectroBike are also opening their own stores instead of waiting for bike shops. At the same time, people are working to change laws so e-bikes are allowed everywhere.

Another important change is that more cities are building places for cycling, like bike lanes. More people now ride bikes to work. The US Census Bureau said in 2014 that bike commuters grew from about 488,000 in 2000 to 786,000 in 2012. The report says this was a bigger percentage increase than any other way of commuting. E-bikes make commuting easier and more fun by helping with hills, headwinds, tiredness, and sweat.

DiCostanza says most Pedego customers are ageing baby boomers who want to feel like they did when they were kids. He says many stopped riding because of hills. Pedego has opened nearly 60 stores in the US. It has also sold bikes to tour companies in San Francisco and Washington, DC.

ElectroBike runs 30 stores in Mexico. It opened its first US store in Venice Beach, California, in autumn 2014. It hopes to grow to 25 US stores in one year. CEO Craig Anderson says they want to reduce traffic, reduce their carbon footprint, and support a healthy lifestyle. He tells customers, “Ride this once and try not to smile.”

Startups like Pedego and ElectroBike must compete with big companies like Trek and Currie. In 2012, Accell Group bought Currie. Accell is a public company in the Netherlands, and it is Europe’s market leader in e-bikes. Accell owns the Raleigh brand and Haibike, an award-winning German electric bike.

Pizzi says Accell expects strong e-bike growth in North America. He says baby boomers are still important customers. But he also says many younger people use e-bikes for transportation instead of cars.

Accell’s Yuba brand sells a cargo bike with a stronger motor and a rear rack. Pizzi says you can carry two children, or carry 45kg of shopping. He calls it an alternative to a minivan.
