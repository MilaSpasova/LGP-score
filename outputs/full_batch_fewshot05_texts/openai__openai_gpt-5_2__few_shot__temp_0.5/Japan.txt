Takahito Iguchi says the problem with Google Glass is that it is not cool. Many people think the glasses look silly or smug. Google gave the first 10,000 pairs to software developers, not to famous models or Scarlett Johansson. If you search Google Images, you can even find a picture of a big naked man wearing them in the shower. Iguchi hopes this bad image will hurt Google. He is making a new product that looks more stylish, more like Blade Runner, and more Japanese.

Iguchi is a Japanese entrepreneur. He is making augmented reality glasses called Telepathy One. They are not really glasses. They are one piece of metal with a camera and a small projector. He showed them at the South by Southwest festival in Austin, Texas, and he got $5m of venture capital. Like Google Glass, Telepathy One is planned to launch in 2014.

Telepathy One is a simpler version of Google Glass. Iguchi says Google Glass is “an egotistical device” with many uses. You can use the internet, read emails, take photos, and use apps. Telepathy will be more for communication. It connects to your phone with Bluetooth. It will focus on sharing video and sound in real time. You can post photos and videos from what you see on Facebook, or send them by email. You can also see and talk to a floating video image of a friend.

Iguchi says the device will help you feel closer to friends and family. He has worked in the Japanese tech industry for 20 years. Most recently, he worked on a location-based phone app called Sekai Camera.

I say that not everyone wants to feel closer to a man wearing a futuristic headset. Iguchi says he is a visionary. He says he dreams that people will understand each other. When he goes to London, he feels like a stranger and sometimes feels fear. But he believes everyone wants to be understood and to understand others. He says that with this device, you can learn more information about people before you even speak to them.

Compared to Google, Telepathy is very small. But Iguchi is not worried. In his shared office in San Francisco, he quotes Sun Tzu’s The Art of War. He says small armies can sometimes beat big forces. When he was young, Japanese technology was very strong. The Sony Walkman was like the iPhone of its time. Now, to compete, he left Tokyo for Silicon Valley.

He says Tokyo has fashion and culture, but it is still like an island, and it is hard to expand. In Silicon Valley, people come from everywhere, and you can connect with the world. The hardware will be made in Japan. He is building a team of software engineers in the US to develop the apps. When I meet him, a news crew from Japan is following him. They are interested in Japanese entrepreneurs who leave Japan.

The correspondent, Takashi Yanagisawa, says Japan is losing confidence and needs a way to regain power. He says Iguchi is like a new frontier and might be part of a solution. Iguchi says building the Telepathy One prototype was easy because Tokyo has all kinds of technology. The hard part is showing it to the world. He says big manufacturers want to work with him because they have the technology but have trouble selling it. He says a product needs a story, like Apple did with the iPod: “1,000 songs in your pocket.” He also mentions how Apple positioned itself against Microsoft and IBM, like David and Goliath. He says Steve Jobs was inspired by Akio Morita, the co-founder of Sony, and Jobs inspired him.

Iguchi has a lot of confidence, but he has a thick Japanese accent and sometimes people do not understand him. This may have helped create the idea for Telepathy One. He says that when he went to London to present the headset at the Founders Forum, he stayed in an Airbnb. He talked with the house owner for three hours, and then they became friends. He says that is how long it can take to understand each other and share feelings, background, and career. He thinks Telepathy could make that time shorter. He says that if you get information from the cloud and social networks, it will be easier. He also says this house owner was involved in getting investment from the UK to Africa. The man was excited about Telepathy because it could educate people about Africa and show other people’s point of view.

Iguchi’s biggest hope is that seeing another person’s real point of view will help you understand their deeper point of view too. He says that when he was a student, he studied philosophy in the day and taught himself coding at night. One day, he says, he realized that everything is code. He calls this his enlightenment. He says everything is coded and can be shared between humans. He says everything can be encoded and decoded. And he believes that if code can be exchanged between humans, it will end all war against each other.
