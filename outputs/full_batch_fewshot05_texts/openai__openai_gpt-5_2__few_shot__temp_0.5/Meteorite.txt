Galina Zaglumyonova was asleep in her flat in central Chelyabinsk. She woke up because of a huge explosion. The explosion blew in the balcony windows and broke clay pots with her few houseplants. She jumped out of bed. She saw a big white trail in the morning sky. She also heard many car alarms outside.

“I didn’t understand what was happening,” Zaglumyonova said. “There was one big explosion and then many smaller ones. At first I thought a plane had crashed.”

But it was a meteorite. The ten-tonne meteorite fell toward Earth just after sunrise. It broke apart in the air and made fireballs.

Officials said almost 1,200 people were injured. More than 40 people went to hospital. Most injuries were from broken glass. The glass shattered because of a sonic boom made by the meteorite. No deaths were reported.

The Russian Academy of Sciences said the meteorite entered the atmosphere at least 33,000mph. It broke into pieces 18 to 32 miles above the ground.

People in Chelyabinsk panicked. The city has more than one million people and is south of Russia’s Ural mountains. Mobile phone networks quickly became too busy because so many people called. Videos from the area showed pieces of the meteorite glowing brighter as they fell. The trail in the sky could be seen for hundreds of miles, including in Kazakhstan.

Tatyana Bets was working at a hospital clinic reception in the city centre. She said they first felt wind. Then the room filled with a very bright light. They saw a cloud of unknown smoke in the sky. A few minutes later, the explosions came.

Later, officials found at least three craters. The Ministry of the Interior said the military was watching them. One crater was more than six metres wide. Another piece was reported to have hit a nearby lake and broken through thick ice. Local military officials said radiation levels were normal.

In Chelyabinsk, schools and universities closed. Many other workers were told to go home early. About 200 injured people were children.

Many lightly injured people came to Bets’s clinic. Most had cuts from flying glass. She said a nearby student dormitory was badly damaged. Many students were very frightened. “There were a lot of girls in shock. Some were very pale and many fainted,” she said.

Early estimates said more than 100,000 square metres of glass was broken. About 3,000 buildings were hit. The total damage in the city was estimated at more than one billion roubles (£20m).

The meteorite arrived less than a day before asteroid 2012 DA14 was expected to pass close to Earth. It was expected to pass about 17,510 miles away. Experts said the meteorite and the asteroid were only a coincidence and were not connected.

In the first hours, rumours spread. Some reports on Russian state TV and local media suggested air defence units shot at the meteorite and broke it apart above 15 miles.

Vladimir Zhirinovsky, the ultranationalist leader of Russia’s Liberal Democrat party, said it was not a meteorite. He said it was military action by the United States. He called it a test of new American weapons.

Some people tried to make money during the confusion. Within a few hours, people were offering pieces of meteorite for sale on internet sites.

President Vladimir Putin and Prime Minister Dmitry Medvedev were told about the event. Putin held a meeting with the head of the Emergency Situations Ministry. Medvedev said the event showed that not only economies are vulnerable, but the whole planet.

Dmitry Rogozin, Russia’s Deputy Prime Minister and former Ambassador to NATO, wrote on Twitter. He called for countries to work together to create a warning system for all “objects of an alien origin”. He added that neither the US nor Russia could bring down such objects.
