The roof is plastic and a bent tree trunk holds it up. The desks are made from old chairs. But the students at the Chemin des Dunes school study very hard, like students at Oxford University. They hope this can help them start a new life in France.

Kamal is a refugee from Sudan’s Darfur area, which has had war. He says, “French is very difficult, but we try hard. If we come every day, maybe we can reach our dreams.” He comes to class for three or four hours every day. He says it is good to keep your brain active.

Kamal is 29 and he is an electrical engineer. He is one of many refugees living in the “jungle” camp outside Calais. They have applied for asylum in France and they want to learn French for their new home.

Kamal is upset because the media often talks only about people who try to sneak onto lorries or trains at night to cross the Channel. He says many people want to stay in France. He says people in the UK think everyone wants to go to the UK, but that is not true.

The United Nations says France has more than a quarter of a million refugees. France has taken in more than twice as many as the UK, even though the two countries have similar populations.

In France, 56,000 asylum seekers are waiting for their cases to be processed. This is the second highest number in Europe. In the UK, there are 36,000.

While people wait for an answer in France, they do not get money from the government and they cannot work. The process is slow and can take many months. The jungle camp gives one free meal a day and a plastic roof. Many people stay in these basic conditions for a few more months, because they do not want to hurt their asylum case by working illegally.

Some refugees first talked about starting the school at the beginning of the summer. They were bored from waiting, and they were worried about starting life in France without being able to communicate. The school became real within weeks and opened on 11 July.

Zimarco Jones is the Nigerian founder of the school. He arrived in Calais in 2013 and is still waiting for his asylum case. He says they started the school to bring the “brothers” together and help them learn French. He says they now need to build another school.

When it is busiest, the small classroom has 30 students. They sit in five rows. There is a big green chalkboard and pictures of cartoon animals for each letter of the French alphabet. There are also classes in English, art, and t’ai chi. But the most popular classes are French lessons. Volunteers from Calais and other places teach them.

George is another refugee from Darfur. He says French is not as easy as English. Two weeks ago, he decided he could not get to the UK. He wanted to cross the Channel because he speaks fluent English. But with language classes, he says he is happy to live in France. He says, “Anywhere there is peace, I can stay, no problem.” He often waits at the classroom more than half an hour before the teachers arrive.

George says he does not know much about France, but the classes are slowly helping him understand the country and the language.

Many volunteers at the school are local teachers. They are giving up their summer holidays.

Jenny Flahaut is 33 and works at a children’s home. She decided to volunteer after seeing an ad on Facebook. She says she saw these people in Calais every day and wanted to do something.

She is angry about how the media shows migrants, and about politicians who have not visited the camp. She mentions David Cameron, the British prime minister. In a speech that many people criticized, he talked about “swarms” of people trying to reach the UK.

Flahaut says people do not know the migrants and have a bad idea about them. She says most of them are very good people. She says they are welcoming and friendly. They want to improve their life, and learning is part of that.

The teachers and Zimarco now want to set up a separate classroom for about 200 women and two dozen children. In the Calais camp, men outnumber women about ten to one. Volunteers say most women feel uncomfortable going to class with men they do not know.

After that, Zimarco has more dreams. People in the camp call it “Zimarco’s school.” He wants to help make the camp a place to live, not only to survive. He wants to start a football team for migrants. He also dreams of changing the name of the camp. He hates the name “the jungle” because he says it makes it sound like the residents are not people.

He says, “We have a discotheque, a house, a mosque, a school, shops. We are not animals.”
