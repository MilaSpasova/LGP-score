Prince Harry has left Afghanistan after a four-month tour. During this time, he said he killed insurgents while flying an Apache helicopter. He also talked honestly about the stress of being a royal and wanting a life away from attention.

He said he does not like or trust some parts of the media. He also said his father often told him to act more like a royal.

Prince Harry said he fired at the Taliban during missions. These missions helped soldiers on the ground and helped rescue injured Afghan and NATO people. He said he was just doing his job. He also said the helicopter often worked as a warning to stop attacks.

While he was at Camp Bastion in Helmand Province, he gave several interviews. He said it was hard to balance different parts of his life. In the army he is called Captain Wales. He said he has “three mes”: one in the army, one in private social life, and one with his family. He said he can switch between them when needed.

He said he sometimes behaved in a childish way and “let himself down”. He said this was probably because he was “too much army and not enough prince”. But he also said he has a right to privacy.

He strongly criticized the media, especially the Sun, the Daily Mail, and the Daily Telegraph. He was angry about stories comparing his job as an Apache co-pilot gunner to Spitfire crews in the second world war. He said it was not like that.

He said his distrust of the media started when he was very small, because of how his family was treated. He said he still reads stories about himself, but they make him upset and angry. He said his father tells him not to read them, and other people say they are always rubbish.

Prince Harry was sent to Afghanistan last September to command a £45m Apache helicopter. During his tour, the Apaches flew missions to support NATO troops fighting the Taliban. They also flew with British Chinook and US Black Hawk medical helicopters during evacuations of injured people.

Four years ago, during his first tour, he had to leave Afghanistan quickly after an Australian magazine broke a media embargo by mistake. This time, the Ministry of Defence made his deployment public, but asked news organizations not to report on his daily life there, so he could do his job. Small teams from the BBC, Sky, and ITN each visited once to report. A photographer and a reporter from the Press Association were with them on all three visits.

When asked if he felt more comfortable as Captain Wales than as Prince Harry, he said yes. He said his father keeps reminding him who he is. But in the army it is easy to forget, because everyone wears the same uniform and does the same kind of work. He said he gets on well with the other soldiers and enjoys his job.

Before he went to Afghanistan, there was another media storm. Pictures appeared of him naked at a private party in Las Vegas. He said he let himself down, his family down, and other people down. But he also said he was in a private place and should have had some privacy. He again said it was an example of being “too much army and not enough prince”.

He said the newspapers knew he was going to Afghanistan, and he did not think their treatment of him was acceptable. He said some people remind him to remember who he is and not to drop his guard.

Asked why he and his brother like helicopters, he said it may be because only a limited number of people can fit in a helicopter, so no one can follow them.
