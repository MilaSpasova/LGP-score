John Lewis’s Christmas 2015 advert is about a lonely old man who lives in a crater on the moon. The store adds a charity message to this expensive campaign.

Many people see the John Lewis Christmas advert as the start of the Christmas shopping season. This year, John Lewis wants to raise hundreds of thousands of pounds for the charity Age UK. It also asks staff and customers to join their local Age UK branch to help older people who may be alone during the holiday.

John Lewis spent £7m on the campaign. It includes the TV advert, a smartphone game, and products to buy. These include glow-in-the-dark pyjamas. In 11 stores, there are also areas decorated to look like the moon.

In the story, a young girl named Lily sees the old man in a small house on the moon through her telescope. She tries to send him a letter and a note with a bow and arrow. Then she sends him a telescope tied to balloons. After that, they can finally connect.

The advert’s line says: “Show someone they’re loved this Christmas”. This is similar to Age UK’s message: “No one should have no one at Christmas”. Profits from three items, a mug, a gift tag, and a card, will go to the charity.

Rachel Swift, head of marketing at John Lewis, said the charity fits this time of year. She said the advert makes people think about an older person on their street who might not see anyone.

The campaign uses the Oasis song Half the World Away, sung in a new way by Norwegian artist Aurora.

The advert cost £1m to make. The moon scenes were filmed at Warner Bros Studios, where the Harry Potter films were made. The set was built by someone from the team behind the Star Wars film The Force Awakens.

John Lewis also used a teaser campaign on TV and social media with the hashtag #OnTheMoon. There will be a full moon on Christmas Day 2015, and Swift said this was just a coincidence.

In 2014, John Lewis also spent £7m on an advert with an animated penguin and a young boy. It used the song Real Love by John Lennon, sung by Tom Odell. By the first week of January, it had 22m views on YouTube. Sainsbury’s 2014 advert, about First World War soldiers sharing a chocolate bar, had 16.6m views.

Swift said John Lewis has used the same plan for five years. She said it is about thoughtful gifts and doing extra for someone you love at Christmas. She said they do not start by thinking the advert will be huge. They try to make something right for the brand and something customers will love.

Sarah Vizard from Marketing Week said John Lewis seemed quieter in stores this time, even though there are more competitors. She said many brands tried to compete with emotional and creative adverts, but some went back to showing what you can buy in store. She said John Lewis still does the emotional style best, and she thinks people will find this advert very cute.

Other companies also tried to get attention with Christmas adverts. Waitrose, a sister chain, put its advert online before it was shown on TV during The X Factor. It used the same agency that has made John Lewis Christmas adverts for years.

Burberry made only its second Christmas film, with Romeo Beckham and Elton John. Currys used Jeff Goldblum for its first special Christmas advert. Asda, Lidl, and Morrisons also started their campaigns at the beginning of November.

Marc Bolland, chief executive of Marks & Spencer, said that in the first week of September, the most searched word on its website was Christmas.

Boots also started its Christmas campaign in early November. It is going back to a more traditional style after its praised 2014 advert, which showed a family doing a lot to be together at Christmas.
