Women have often been kept on the side in football. This changed when a French club hired its first female professional team manager. The club was in the second division, and some people said it was only a trick to get publicity. The club was Clermont Foot 63, and it was 14th out of 20 teams in its league.

The important thing was that Helena Costa got the top job. She made history because she became the first woman to be a manager in the top two divisions of any professional European league. Véronique Soulier, the president of the club’s supporters’ group, said it made her happy. She said many supporters first felt surprised, but then agreed it was good news. She said having a woman leading a group of men can be a good thing.

Clermont Foot 63 plays at Clermont-Ferrand in the Auvergne region of south-central France. The average home crowd is about 3,800. Costa used to find talented players for Celtic in the Scottish Premiership. Costa is 36. She was born in Alhandra in Portugal, on the River Tagus. She has a master’s degree in sports science and a UEFA coaching license. She coached Benfica’s boys’ youth teams, the Qatar women’s team (and led them to their first international win in 2012), and later the Iranian women’s national team, which she left in September 2013.

Costa got a two-year contract from the club president, Claude Michy. He is known for getting attention for his club. In 2013, he said the team had signed Messi. It was not Lionel Messi from Argentina and FC Barcelona. It was Junior Messi Enguene, a 20-year-old midfielder from Cameroon.

France’s women’s minister, Najat Vallaud-Belkacem, tweeted “Bravo” to Clermont Foot. She said giving women a place is the future of professional football.

Carolina Morace, an Italian, was the only woman before this to coach a men’s professional team. She said she did not know Helena, but if a team hired her, then she must know her job. She said she hopes this will become normal one day. Morace was also a great player. She was the top scorer for 12 seasons in Italy’s Serie A and played 153 international matches for Italy. In 1999, she became coach of Viterbese in men’s Serie C1. After two games, she resigned after a fight with the club owner, Luciano Gaucci, who also owned Perugia at the time.

Morace said Gaucci wanted her to fire her assistant coach and the team’s trainer, and she refused. She said trust was gone, and she did not want to work in that uncertain situation. Today she works as a lawyer in Rome and as a football commentator on TV and in the newspaper Gazzetta dello Sport.

Morace said that even in women’s football, she sees many men working who do not have the same skills as women who cannot get jobs. Raymond Domenech, former manager of France, said women know football and can manage well, so they should also manage men’s teams. He said it already happens the other way around and nobody has a problem. He said it is natural and shows women are equal to men. He congratulated President Michy and said that if he managed a club again, he would hire a woman as his assistant, but Michy did it first.

Clermont Foot 63’s website said Costa’s hiring would bring “a new era.” On the supporters’ website, people reacted with surprise and doubt. One fan said it was only for publicity, that she would not last the season, and that players might not respect her, especially because she is the same age as the oldest players. Another fan said her CV is not bad, but asked if she will be good enough. A third fan wished her well but said it may be hard for a woman to succeed in a very macho business, and asked if the president had made a media trick.

Soulier was hopeful. She said the new manager might help the club find motivation. She said the players can be hard to manage, and maybe they will be less demanding with a woman in charge.

But Costa may be the one who demands more. She did work experience at Chelsea when José Mourinho was manager there from 2004 to 2007. Some people reportedly called her “Mourinho in a skirt.” Costa rejected the sexist comment. She said that like Mourinho she always wants to win, and she is happy to be compared with him in that way.
