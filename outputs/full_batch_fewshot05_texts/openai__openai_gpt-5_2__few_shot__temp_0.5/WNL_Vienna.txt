An international study on quality of life says Vienna is the best city in the world to live in. Baghdad is the worst. London, Paris, and New York are not in the top 35.

In the 18th Mercer Quality of Life study, many German-speaking cities rank very high. Vienna is joined in the top seven by Zurich, Munich, Dusseldorf, and Frankfurt.

Paris fell ten places to 37th. London is 39th. Mercer said this is mostly because Paris is vulnerable to terrorist attacks.

The study looked at social and economic conditions, health, education, housing, and the environment. Big companies use it to decide where to put offices and how much to pay staff.

Helena Hartlauer, 32, was born in Vienna. She said she was not surprised that Vienna is number one. She said the city government has a long tradition of investing in high-quality social housing. This makes Vienna very affordable for a major city.

She said she lives in a 100 square metre apartment in a good area, about a 20-minute walk from the city centre. Her rent is €800 (£625) per month. She said a similar apartment in London would cost more than £2,000, and even more in New York. New York is ranked 44th.

US cities do not do very well in the study, mainly because of personal safety and crime. The highest US city is San Francisco at 28th. Boston is 34th. Canadian cities rank much higher than US cities, led by Vancouver.

Hartlauer said Vienna feels very safe, especially when you travel abroad. She also said Vienna has very good public transport. The underground runs 24 hours at weekends, and a trip costs €1.

Vienna benefited a lot from the fall of the Berlin Wall. It became a gateway to Eastern European countries, many with historic ties to the former Austro-Hungarian empire.

Martin Eichtinger, the Austrian ambassador to London, lived in Vienna for 20 years. He said Vienna’s main strength is its location. He said the fall of the Berlin Wall helped make Vienna a hub for companies that want to do business in Central Europe.

The World Bank says Austria has one of the highest GDP per head in the world. It is just behind the US and ahead of Germany and Britain, but still well below Switzerland.

Mercer says Zurich has the second highest quality of life. But Hartlauer said Vienna is more fun. She said Vienna has more students than any other German-speaking city. She said it is growing fast and is young and lively. She also said she works for the city’s tourist board.

British tourists often do not choose Vienna for a weekend trip. They often go to Barcelona or Berlin, and they often think of Austria mainly for skiing, lakes, and mountains.

But Vienna is becoming more popular, after more budget flights from British cities like Manchester and Edinburgh. In 2015, Vienna had 588,000 British visitors, up 18% from the year before. Eichtinger said the flow is both ways, and London is now the number one city destination for Austrian visitors.

Mercer said Vienna has ranked first in the last seven published rankings. Mercer said Vienna scores well because it is safe and stable, has good public utilities and transport, and has good recreational facilities.

Eichtinger said the European migrant crisis has had little impact on Vienna, a city of nearly 1.8 million people. He said Austria has accommodated 90,000 refugees, but the numbers have slowed in recent months.

Mercer said London has never been in the quality-of-life top ten. Mercer said London is hurt by air pollution, traffic congestion, and climate. After London, Edinburgh is the next British city, in 46th place.

Mercer said Paris had the biggest fall in the latest rankings. Mercer said Paris was stable for several years, but this year it dropped ten places. Mercer said the drop was mainly due to the terrorist attacks in 2015. Mercer also said safety is a very important factor, so small changes can strongly affect the ranking.

Auckland in New Zealand was the highest-ranked English-speaking city, in third place. Vancouver was fifth. Australian cities also ranked very high: Sydney was 10th and Melbourne was 15th. The Economist has often ranked Melbourne as the world’s most liveable city, but people have criticized its survey as too Anglocentric.

War and political unrest are the main reasons for the worst-ranked cities. Damascus was named as the seventh worst. It ranked better than Baghdad, and also better than Bangui in Central African Republic, Sana’a in Yemen, Port-au-Prince in Haiti, Khartoum in Sudan, and N’Djamena in Chad.
