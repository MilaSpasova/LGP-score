What is it like to see the last one of something?

Sudan is the last male northern white rhino on Earth. He lives at Ol Pejeta Conservancy in Kenya with two female northern white rhinos. If he does not have a baby with one of them soon, no more northern white rhinos will be born. This is not likely, because Sudan is old. He is 42, and breeding has not worked so far. Besides these three, there are only two other northern white rhinos in the world. They are both female and live in zoos.

Sudan is guarded by armed men. This may look kind, but it is also sad. Sudan is still in danger from poachers. Poachers kill rhinos and cut off their horns to sell them in Asia as medicine. Sudan’s horn has been cut off to stop poachers, but he is still at risk.

Sudan does not know how important he is. He walks in the reserve with his guards. He is huge and strong, but he is also in danger.

People have looked at rhinos for a very long time. Sudan looks similar to a rhino shown by the artist Albrecht Dürer in 1515. In that year, a live Indian rhino was sent from the ruler of Gujarat in India to the king of Portugal. The king sent it to the Pope, but it died in a shipwreck on the way.

Humans often kill animals they admire. During the Ice Age, people painted woolly rhinos in caves in France up to 30,000 years ago. These rhinos were big and powerful, like Sudan’s relatives. But the same people may have helped kill them. When the climate changed at the end of the Ice Age, humans probably also killed them with spears.

Today many people say they love rhinos, but rhinos are still being killed in large numbers. The northern white rhino is the rarest African rhino. There are many more southern white rhinos and black rhinos. But in some countries, people want rhino horn as traditional medicine. Some believe it can cure many illnesses, from flu to cancer. This belief has increased poaching.

In South Africa, 13 rhinos were killed by poachers in 2007. In 2014, 1,215 rhinos were killed for their horns. 2015 seemed likely to be even worse.

The northern white rhino has been hunted almost to extinction, even with guards and guns. Other African rhinos are also under heavy attack. The Javan rhino is close to extinction too. India has protected the Indian rhino after British hunters almost destroyed it in colonial times, but poaching is still a danger there. Rhinos are majestic animals, and humans keep destroying them.
