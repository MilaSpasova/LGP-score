SeaWorld’s profits fell by 84% because many customers stopped going to the park. People left after claims that SeaWorld mistreated orca whales.

SeaWorld trains dolphins and killer whales to do tricks in front of large crowds. The company said it has lower attendance, lower sales, and lower profits because of ongoing problems with its brand.

SeaWorld has been in the news since the 2013 documentary Blackfish. The film said SeaWorld’s treatment of orcas led to violent behavior and helped cause the deaths of three people. After the film, attendance fell. The company lost more than half of its value on Wall Street, and its CEO was forced out.

SeaWorld started a marketing campaign to answer animal rights activists. Activists say captive orcas die younger than wild orcas.

SeaWorld cut ticket prices and spent $10m on marketing. The ads show its veterinarians caring for whales. SeaWorld CEO Joel Manby said the company is still having trouble convincing the public that it treats whales well.

Manby said the company needs time and investment to bring back attendance and improve results. He also said feedback on the campaign has been positive, but the company still has brand problems in California and wants to correct misinformation. He said SeaWorld will keep fighting with facts.

Manby became CEO in 2015 to help SeaWorld improve its image. He said he will share his plan for the future at an event on 6 November.

SeaWorld plans a new shark exhibition in Orlando. It also plans an attraction in San Antonio where customers can swim with dolphins in a “naturalistic” setting.

A financial report released on 6 August showed net income in the second quarter fell from $37.4m in 2014 to $5.8m in 2015. Revenue fell from $405m to $392m. Attendance fell by more than 100,000, from 6.58 million to 6.48 million.

Analysts will watch SeaWorld’s sales and attendance in the third quarter. This is usually the most profitable time because it includes the summer holiday season. Attendance may also fall because of a new scandal in July 2015. In that case, a SeaWorld employee was alleged to have infiltrated animal rights protest groups.

Jared Goodman from PETA said SeaWorld has a spying scandal, animals are dying in its tanks, and many people opposed its plan to build a new place to keep orcas. He said families do not want to buy tickets to see orcas in small tanks. He said profits will not recover until SeaWorld empties its tanks and builds coastal sanctuaries.

SeaWorld shares were $39 in 2013. In August 2015, they were just under $18.
