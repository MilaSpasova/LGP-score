The last series of Downton Abbey starts with a sad mood and a feeling that times are changing. The year is 1925. People already worry about economic problems later in the decade. The neighbours are selling their large house. Lord Grantham wants fewer servants because under-butlers cost too much.

But the real “Downton Abbey” is Highclere Castle. It is owned by George “Geordie” Herbert, the 8th Earl of Carnarvon. There, money has often been better than before. Lady Fiona Carnarvon says the world success of Downton Abbey has paid for ongoing building repairs. The goal is to protect Highclere for the next generation.

Lady Carnarvon said it has been an amazing experience. She said the show gave them great marketing and an international profile. She is very grateful. She said she and her husband love the house and the people there, and now millions of other people love it too.

At the moment, only the ground floor and first floor of Highclere, on the border of Hampshire, are used. But work has started on old tower rooms. Later, visitors will be able to climb the tower to see an exhibition about Sir Charles Barry. He was the architect of the Houses of Parliament, and he rebuilt the house from 1839 to 1842.

When the Downton Abbey producers first contacted Highclere in 2009, the family had a repair bill close to £12m. Urgent work cost £1.8m. By 2012, the success of Downton Abbey started to reduce the pressure. Lord Carnarvon said it was just after the banking crisis and things felt gloomy. They had done corporate events, but there were fewer after that. Then Downton Abbey came, and Highclere became a major tourist attraction.

Visitor numbers doubled to 1,200 a day. Downton Abbey, written by Julian Fellowes, became a hit in the UK in 2010 and then in the US, and it was shown around the world. It is now broadcast in 250 countries.

Highclere changed its simple ticket system into a computer booking system with advance tickets. This helps make sure foreign visitors can get in. Highclere Enterprises accounts for 2014-15 show current assets almost tripled to about £1m since 2012. Gareth Neame, the executive producer, said Downton Abbey secured Highclere’s future.

Peter Fincham, ITV’s director of television, remembers when Highclere was chosen. At first he thought it did not matter because he had never heard of Highclere Castle, and many stately homes seem similar. Later he said he was wrong, because the castle became a big character too.

Downton tourists are part of a bigger trend. VisitBritain says almost 30% of foreign visitors, almost nine million people, include castles and historic houses in their travel plans. Almost half of people who might visit Britain now say they want to do “set jetting”: visiting places they saw in films or on TV.

Each year, more than a million people take tours of historic buildings, and they spend more than £1bn. From some growing tourist markets, 51% of Brazilians, 42% of Russians and Chinese, and 35% of Indians are likely to visit an interesting site during their trips.

VisitBritain director Patricia Yates said the link between tourism and films and TV is very strong. She said period dramas have also made regions outside London more popular.

Neame is now an ambassador for the GREAT Britain campaign. Government departments and the British Council support it, and it promotes the UK around the world. Events include special Downton-themed receptions at British embassies.

Neame said they asked him because the show reaches many people. He said some people in Britain think it is just simple entertainment. In other countries, people admire British actors, writing, and production skills. He said he believes in “soft power” and thinks Britain is not proud enough of its achievements.

Neame said Downton Abbey is iconic for showing “Britishness”. He said it is really a fantasy world in a specific time in history. He said it is the first TV period drama that has jumped off the screen and become part of popular culture.

Lady Carnarvon still says Highclere’s long-term future is not fully safe. She said there is not much extra money. She said the programme helped them spend faster on the buildings and restore the follies.

Plans include a Tutankhamun centenary event in 2022. It is 100 years after the 5th Earl of Carnarvon and Howard Carter discovered the tomb, which changed our understanding of Egyptology. Another chance to keep Highclere famous is the 300th anniversary of the birth of Lancelot “Capability” Brown, who designed the grounds.

Lady Carnarvon said you should never relax and think everything is fine. She said these great houses always need investment. She said there has been a deficit since the 1930s. In the past, the estate and house supported the family’s lifestyle. Today it is the opposite: Geordie and she must find ways to support and care for Highclere.

She said she tries to show people it is fun, with special events, not only walking around an old dusty house. She said they must compete with attractions like the London Dungeon.
