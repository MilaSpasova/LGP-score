David Bowie died of cancer. Until the end of his life, he could still surprise people. His last album, Blackstar, came out on his 69th birthday, 8 January 2016. It showed he could still make strong statements and create challenging music.

In the 1970s, Bowie led new music and fashion trends. In the late 1960s, he performed mime and cabaret. Later, he became a singer-songwriter. He helped create glam-rock. Then he tried a style he called “plastic soul”. After that, he moved to Berlin and made new electronic music. He often changed his sound and his look.

Bowie was born David Robert Jones in south London. In 1953, his family moved to Kent. David was good at singing and playing the recorder. Later, he studied art, music, and design. In 1961, his mother bought him a plastic saxophone. The saxophone later became important in his music.

At 15, David formed his first band, the Kon-rads. Soon it was clear he should work alone. He chose the name Bowie so people would not confuse him with Davy Jones of the Monkees.

His first album, David Bowie, came out in June 1967. In July 1969, he released the song Space Oddity. It gave him his first big success. It came out at the time of the Apollo 11 moon landing, and it reached the UK top five.

In March 1970, Bowie married an art student, Angela Barnett. His album The Man Who Sold the World was released in the US in late 1970 and in the UK in 1971. It was darker and louder. The album talked about topics like immortality, insanity, murder, and mysticism.

In 1972, he released Hunky Dory. It was well made, but it did not sell a lot at first. Later in 1972, he released The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars. Bowie played a science-fiction glam-rock star visiting Earth. The hit song Starman brought fast success. His costumes and shows made fans very excited.

He had his first UK number 1 album with Aladdin Sane (1973). It included the hit songs The Jean Genie and Drive-in Saturday. But he was already planning new changes.

On Young Americans (1975), he showed more interest in funk and soul. The song Fame became a US number 1, and John Lennon sang as a guest.

Station to Station (1976) introduced a new character, the Thin White Duke. This was linked to his lead role as a sad space traveller in Nicolas Roeg’s film The Man Who Fell to Earth.

Bowie’s marriage broke down, and he and Angela divorced in 1980. That year he released Scary Monsters (and Super Creeps) and the number 1 single Ashes to Ashes. He also acted as John Merrick in The Elephant Man on Broadway.

In 1981, he made Under Pressure with Queen, which became a number 1 single. He also worked in film. He appeared in the German movie Christiane F (1981) and wrote music for it. He had another hit with Cat People (Putting Out Fire) from the movie Cat People (1982).

In 1983, he acted in The Hunger and in the war drama Merry Christmas, Mr Lawrence. In music, he focused on big commercial success with the album Let’s Dance and concerts. Let’s Dance and the songs Let’s Dance, China Girl, and Modern Love became huge international hits.

MTV was very popular then, and Bowie’s music videos helped him reach more people. His Serious Moonlight tour lasted six months and drew very large crowds. This was the most successful business period of his career.

In 1985, he performed at the Live Aid concert at Wembley Stadium. He was one of the most memorable performers. He also recorded Dancing in the Street with Mick Jagger, and it reached number 1.

In April 1992, soon after the Freddie Mercury tribute concert at Wembley Stadium, Bowie married the Somalian model Iman. They bought a home in New York. This new start in his private life also led him to look for new musical ideas.

On the album Black Tie White Noise (1993), he mixed soul, electronica, and hip hop. It reached number 1 on the UK album chart. It also had a UK top 10 single, Jump They Say.

New technology also affected his work. His 1999 album Hours… used music he wrote for the computer game Omikron. Bowie and Iman appeared in the game as characters. In August 2000, Bowie and Iman had a daughter, Alexandria.

In October 2001, Bowie opened the Concert for New York City. It was a benefit show six weeks after the 9/11 attacks. He performed with Paul McCartney, Jon Bon Jovi, Billy Joel, the Who, and Elton John.

He returned to the studio the next year for the album Reality. During his Reality tour in 2004, he had chest pains while performing in Germany. He had emergency surgery in Hamburg to clear a blocked artery.

After this, he slowed down. In 2006, he said he would take a year off from touring and recording. In February 2006, he received a Grammy lifetime achievement award. He had been added to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1996.

The Next Day (2013) was his first album of new songs in 10 years. It included the single Where Are We Now?, his first UK top 10 hit since 1993. The album reached number 1 in Britain and in many other places. In 2014, he won the Brit Award for Best British Male. This made him the oldest British person to win that award.

He is survived by Iman, their daughter Alexandria, his stepdaughter Zulekha, and his son Duncan from his first marriage.
