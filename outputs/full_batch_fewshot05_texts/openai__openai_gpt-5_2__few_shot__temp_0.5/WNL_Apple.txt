Apple’s brand and logo were named the most valuable in the world. It is worth nearly $119bn. This is more than the total gross domestic product of Morocco, Ecuador, or Oman.

Apple is already the world’s biggest company. Its stock market value is $591bn. Interbrand’s Best Global Brands report says Apple’s brand value grew by 21% in 12 months.

Apple is known around the world for its simple logo: an apple with a bite missing. In the 2014 report, many technology companies moved up. Older famous brands like Coca-Cola, McDonald’s, and Gillette moved down.

Google’s brand value rose by 15% to $107bn and took second place. Coca-Cola was next, up 3% to $81.5bn. Then came IBM ($72.2bn) and Microsoft ($45.5bn).

Facebook rose the most. Its brand value increased by 86% to $14.3bn. It reached 29th place, ahead of Volkswagen, Kellogg’s, and Ford.

Jez Frampton, chief executive of Interbrand (part of Omnicom), said digital and mobile technology helped brands like Apple grow stronger. He said companies like Google, Amazon, and Facebook have changed what customers expect.

Apple was founded by former Chief Executive Steve Jobs in his Los Altos garage in 1976. Apple only entered the top ten of the Interbrand study in 2011.

The logo was created by advertising executive Rob Janoff in 1977. It has a bite so people would not confuse it with a cherry. Janoff said the logo has many meanings for him, like lust, knowledge, hope, and anarchy.

Graham Hayles, Interbrand’s chief marketing officer, said it makes sense that Apple’s brand could be about one fifth of the company’s market value. He said Apple makes a lot of money because it has a very strong brand. He also said strong branding is closely linked to making profits.

Hayles said Interbrand has done this study every year since 2000. It calculates brand value by looking at a company’s financial performance, consumers’ loyalty to the brand, and an analysis of the brand’s strength.

Some technology companies also fell. Nokia dropped 41 places to 98th, with $4.1bn. Nintendo was 100th (down 33). Hayles said this shows the importance of innovation. If a company does not keep up, it is punished.

A Chinese company entered the top 100 for the first time. Huawei, a mobile-phone and broadband company, came in at 94th with $4.3bn. The US and Australian governments have partly banned Huawei because they fear its equipment could be used for cyber-espionage by the Chinese.

Most brands in the top 100 are owned by US companies. The highest non-US brands were Samsung from South Korea (6th), Toyota from Japan (8th), and Mercedes-Benz from Germany (10th). The highest British brands were HSBC (33rd), Shell (65th), and Burberry (73rd).

Other fashion brands in the top 100 include Boss, Prada, and Ralph Lauren. Louis Vuitton was the highest fashion brand, in 19th place, worth $23bn. H&M was 21st, worth $21bn.

Nike was 22nd, worth nearly $20bn. It was far ahead of Adidas, which was 59th and worth $7bn.

Frampton said that on Twitter and other social media, consumers can interact with brands and criticize them. This means companies must respond faster to protect and improve their reputations. He said customers now have more control than ever. In this world of two-way conversations, influence and engagement are very important for building a brand.

He said customers want smooth interactions, quick responses, access at any time, choices to customize, and very personal service. He said customers increasingly expect brands to know them.
