McDonald’s is not only the biggest burger chain in the world. It is also a symbol of American business. But now the golden arches look less popular. After 10 years of growth, people around the world do not seem to like it as much. McDonald’s said worldwide sales fell by 3.3% in 2013, and the results were called terrible.

Problems are happening in many places. In China, sales fell by 23% after a food scare. Local media showed workers at a supplier who seemed to say they used old beef and chicken for food going to McDonald’s and KFC. In Europe, sales fell by 4%, mostly because of unrest in Ukraine and anti-western feelings in Russia. In Russia, health inspectors are investigating about 200 of McDonald’s 450 restaurants, and 10 have been closed.

The biggest problem is in the US, where about 40% of McDonald’s restaurants are. Nearly 60 years after Ray Kroc opened his first restaurant in Des Plaines, Illinois, people are losing interest in Big Macs and fries.

In the US, McDonald’s had 12 months in a row with lower sales. Sales were down 4.1% in the latest quarter. Many younger customers are going to other places, such as Chipotle Mexican Grill. Technomic said the number of 19-to-21-year-olds who visit McDonald’s once a month fell by 13% since 2011. The number of 22-to-37-year-olds visiting did not grow.

McDonald’s burgers were also named the worst in America in a poll of more than 32,000 American diners. Many said they would rather eat at Five Guys, Smashburger, or Fuddruckers. Chick-fil-A was seen as best for chicken. Many people also think McDonald’s food is less healthy than rivals, especially Chipotle, which talks about antibiotic-free meat and “locally sourced, seasonal produce,” though “local” for Chipotle can mean 350 miles away.

In October, McDonald’s started a website in the US called Our Food Your Questions to answer customer worries. One question was about “pink slime” in burgers. This was a beef filler made from gristle and fat. It was sprayed with ammonia to make it safe for people to eat. It has been banned in Europe since the BSE crisis. McDonald’s stopped using it in 2012.

Even so, some people still worry. One person asked if McDonald’s beef contains worms. McDonald’s said no. Others asked if it uses real eggs, or if the McRib is made with the same plastic as yoga mats. Chief Executive Don Thompson said the company must deal with wrong ideas about the freshness, quality, and honesty of its ingredients.

McDonald’s is losing customers who will pay more for food that seems fresher and healthier. It is also losing its advantage in fast food: quick service and low prices. After it added $2 items to its dollar menu, it got a reputation for being more expensive, and many people say service is slower.

Mary Chapman at Technomic said the “expensive” label was not fair. She said prices did go up, but not as fast as other US fast-service chains. Technomic said McDonald’s prices rose 4.8% since 2009. This was lower than the quick service average, which rose 19.4%. Fast casual prices rose 16.9%. US consumer prices rose 11% in the same time.

But longer lines are a real issue. McDonald’s has a bigger menu than some rivals, with more complex items. A chicken McWrap takes 60 seconds to make. Chapman said she thinks it is worth waiting, but someone who wants a double cheeseburger for a dollar might not.

In the UK, McDonald’s has improved its business, and Britain is a rare bright spot. Peter Martin of CGA Peach said a strong breakfast menu, better coffee, and free wi-fi helped. CGA Peach figures show 56% of British adults visited McDonald’s at least once in the last six months.

In the US, executives say they will fix wrong ideas about the food. Thompson promised more organic food and custom-made burgers. To reduce lines, he also wants simpler menus. Analysts say it is hard to have a simpler menu and also give people more choices.

Mark Kalinowski at Janney Capital Markets said this plan sounds difficult. He said only four of McDonald’s 14,000 US restaurants had tested “build your own burger.” He questioned how it could be expanded. He said they are sceptical and want more detail.

Even with falling sales, McDonald’s is still growing worldwide. By the end of 2014, it expected to open 1,400 new restaurants. Kalinowski expects McDonald’s market share will keep shrinking, but he warned people not to ignore the company. He said McDonald’s could stay number one in total sales for many years, even decades.
