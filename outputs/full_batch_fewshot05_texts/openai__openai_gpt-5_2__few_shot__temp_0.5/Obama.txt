On Wednesday, Barack Obama flew back to Washington and went back to work in the Oval Office. Earlier that day, he gave a victory speech in Chicago. He asked the country to unite behind him.

Obama said, “You voted for action, not politics as usual.” But it did not look easy for him to get agreement. He still had to work with a Republican-led House of Representatives for at least two more years. There was also a coming fight about government spending and debt, called the “fiscal cliff.”

After his election in 2008, Obama had a short time when people were kinder to him. This time, that was unlikely.

The Republican House Speaker John Boehner and the Democratic Senate Leader Harry Reid both said they needed to work together to solve the crisis. But it could still become one of the biggest clashes between the White House and Congress during Obama’s presidency.

Obama beat his Republican opponent Mitt Romney. He won many swing states. But the election also showed that America was still very divided.

Obama’s inauguration was not until January, but he began his second term in practice on Wednesday. Many supporters were disappointed in his first term. Now he wanted to build a legacy and be seen as a great president.

He hoped the economy would keep recovering. He also wanted to work on immigration reform, investment in education, and climate change. In foreign policy, he wanted to deal with issues from Iran to Israel-Palestine.

Obama won more than the needed 270 electoral college votes. He also won a higher share of the popular vote.

Boehner sounded friendly in a statement. He talked about the need for both parties to find common ground and help the economy grow and create jobs, which he said was important for solving the debt. Reports said Obama phoned Boehner to start talks.

Reid also sounded friendly. He said he was reaching out to Republicans in the Senate and the House and asked them to come together and solve the issues.

During the campaign, Obama spoke in an interview about hope for a “grand bargain” with Republicans, which he could not get in 2011. Problems could come when they discussed details. Republicans wanted to protect military spending, while Democrats wanted cuts. Obama called for tax increases on households earning more than $250,000. Boehner rejected any tax increases.

Stock prices fell on the Dow because people expected more political gridlock. By lunchtime, all the major US markets were down over 300 points.

The new House, formed in January, would look like the current one, with a large Republican majority. The Senate stayed almost the same. Democrats kept a slim majority, gaining three seats and losing one.

In the presidential race, Romney won only one swing state, North Carolina. Obama held New Hampshire, Virginia, Ohio, Wisconsin, Nevada, Iowa, and Colorado.

In his victory speech, Obama talked about long lines to vote. He said there was a need for electoral reform.

He used the strong and inspiring style of speech he was known for in 2008, but used less in 2012. His campaign team thought it was not right because many people were unhappy with his presidency and the economy was in poor condition.

After winning, he used famous lines from earlier speeches again, including his 2008 slogan about “hope.”

Obama walked up to the lectern while Stevie Wonder’s “Signed, Sealed, Delivered, I’m Yours” played. He told the very happy crowd that the road had been hard and the journey long, but Americans had picked themselves up and fought back. He said he believed the best was still to come for the United States of America.

The speech lasted more than 25 minutes. He gave an emotional tribute to his wife Michelle, his daughters Malia and Sasha, and his Vice-President Joe Biden. He returned to the message that first made him known across the country.

He said the country was not as divided as politics suggests. He said people were not as cynical as commentators believe. He said Americans were more than red states and blue states, and that they were the United States of America.

Obama said he had plans for his second term. He mentioned changes to the tax code, immigration reform, and an America that is not threatened by the destructive power of a warming planet.

Before that, Romney phoned Obama to concede. In a concession speech in Boston, Romney told supporters the nation was at a critical point. He said leaders should not fight in a partisan way, and should work together to do the people’s work. He also said he prayed Obama would be successful in guiding the nation.

For most of the campaign, the election was a judgment on Obama. Many people were unhappy with the slow economic recovery and high unemployment. But Americans chose to keep him.

In history, many African Americans and many white liberals would have been disappointed if the first black presidency had ended in failure and stopped early.
