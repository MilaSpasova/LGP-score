The Duchess of Cambridge had a baby boy on Monday, 22 July. The baby is third in line to the throne. He is expected to be the 43rd monarch since William the Conqueror took the English crown in 1066.

Kensington Palace said at 8.30pm that the baby was born at 4.24pm. He was born in the Lindo Wing at St Mary’s Hospital in Paddington, West London. The Duke of Cambridge said, "We could not be happier."

Kensington Palace said the Duchess was safe and the Duke was there for the birth. The baby weighed 8lbs 6oz. The palace said the Duchess was in labour for at least ten and a half hours, and it "progressed as normal".

The Queen, the Duke of Edinburgh, the Prince of Wales, the Duchess of Cornwall, Prince Harry, and both families were told the news. They were happy that the Duchess and the baby were doing well.

The couple did not announce the birth right away. They wanted some private time with their newborn. William called his family and spoke to the Queen, his father Charles, and his brother Prince Harry.

Because of this birth, the monarchy has three generations of heirs to the throne. This has not happened since 1894.

The baby is the first Prince of Cambridge to be born in more than 190 years. The last one was Prince George of Cambridge, a grandson of George III and the only son of Prince Adolphus Frederick, the 1st Duke of Cambridge.

Prince Charles said he and his wife were "overjoyed" to have their first grandchild. He said it was a special moment for William and Catherine. He said he was proud to be a grandfather for the first time and they wanted to see the baby soon.

The new royal will be called HRH Prince George of Cambridge. When Kate was three months pregnant, the Queen issued a decree. It said all children of the eldest son of the Prince of Wales should have the title Royal Highness and be called Prince or Princess.

A formal notice was put on a decorated easel at Buckingham Palace before 9pm. It was the same easel used for Prince William’s birth in 1982. The crowd outside the palace grew from hundreds to thousands.

A woman from New York, Sharon Surloff, was happy with the photo she took of the notice. She said police told people, "OK, 20 seconds and then the next person". She said she arrived at nine in the morning and was glad to be there.

The palace also announced the birth in a press release. People outside cheered, "It’s a boy". The formal medical bulletin was taken from the hospital to a car by Ed Perkins, Prince William’s press secretary. He gave it to a soldier. The soldier took it to Buckingham Palace with police escort. The bulletin was signed by Marcus Setchell, the Queen’s gynaecologist.

Prime minister David Cameron congratulated the couple outside 10 Downing Street. He said people across the country and the Commonwealth would celebrate and wish them well. He said it was an important moment for the nation, and also a happy moment for a loving couple with a new baby boy.

Barack Obama and his wife also sent congratulations from the White House. Obama said he and Michelle were pleased for the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge on the birth of their first child. He wished them happiness and blessings.

The Archbishop of Canterbury, Justin Welby, tweeted that he was delighted for them and asked for love, health, and happiness for them.

Labour leader Ed Miliband congratulated the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge. He wished them and their son happiness and good health.

The campaign group Republic said the royal birth raised questions about Britain and democratic values. Chief Executive Graham Smith said the baby should grow up without "constant interference and intrusion". He said the baby’s career, religion, and personal relationships had already been planned, and he asked if this "circus" was the best way to run things.
