Music subscription services like Spotify and Deezer have passed $1bn in sales worldwide. More people are choosing to pay for music online.

In 2013, streaming and subscription income rose by more than 50% to $1.1bn. Figures published in March 2014 say this helped recorded music sales in Europe grow for the first time in 12 years.

There are about 450 music-rental services in the world. Many people still listen for free, but more people want more choice and are willing to pay. In three years, the number of paying subscribers rose from 8 million to 28 million, says the 2014 digital music report from the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI).

These services are easy to use on smartphones and tablets. People like them because they can try new music without buying a download or a CD. People also say they like a cheap, easy, and legal choice instead of pirated downloads. IFPI Chief Executive Frances Moore said streaming and subscription is now a main model for the music business.

The IFPI also said One Direction were the biggest-selling artists of 2013. They sold 4m physical and digital copies of their album Midnight Memories. Katy Perry’s Prism was the best-selling album by a female artist. It was in sixth place, behind Eminem, Justin Timberlake, Bruno Mars, and Daft Punk.

Technology companies are trying to join the streaming trend. Apple launched iTunes Radio, and Google promoted its Play Store. Smaller services like Beats Music, made by the team behind the Beats headphones brand, also joined.

In Britain and America, streaming may soon make more money for the music industry than downloads from online stores like Apple’s iTunes. Subscription services now make up one third of all digital sales worldwide. Downloads make up the rest, but the two are growing at different speeds.

In the US, the share of people who said they use subscription and streaming rose from 19% in 2012 to 23%. The share who download fell from 28% to 27%. In Britain, the number of downloaders stayed the same, at exactly one third, but subscribers grew from 19% to 22%. In Sweden, France, and Italy, streaming is already more popular than downloading.

Digital formats are now 39% of all music sales. That is nearly £5.9bn out of £15bn. CD and vinyl sales fell a lot in 2013, but they still bring in just over half of the industry’s income. Music sales in Japan fell recently. Japan is one fifth of music industry sales, and physical formats stayed popular there longer. Because of this, global sales across all formats fell 3.9%.

However, vinyl is becoming popular again in some places. Vinyl sales rose by 32% in America and by 101% in the UK in 2013.
