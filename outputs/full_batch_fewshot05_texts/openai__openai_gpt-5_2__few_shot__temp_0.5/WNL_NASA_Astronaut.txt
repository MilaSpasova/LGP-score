Astronaut Scott Kelly told reporters about his 340 days in space after he landed back on Earth from a record mission. He said the long mission felt like it lasted forever. He said that sometimes you can feel a little crazy.

Kelly and a Russian astronaut, Mikhail Kornienko, spent almost a year on the International Space Station (ISS). NASA did this to study how weightlessness, radiation, and small living spaces affect the human body. NASA says this research is needed for a future mission to Mars.

Kelly said the mission length was the hardest part. He said he felt much more sore when he returned to Earth’s gravity than after shorter missions. Kelly and his twin brother, Mark, a retired astronaut, have been taking physical and mental tests for a year. The tests will continue so NASA can learn how the body handles the stress of space travel.

Kelly said he had aches and his skin was very sensitive. He said his balance has been mostly okay so far. But he said the first thing he tried to throw onto a table, he missed, because you can forget how strong gravity is.

He said coming back to gravity was uncomfortable. In space, he became 1.5 inches taller, but on Earth he went back to normal height. Still, he said he felt amazed after landing. When the Russian capsule opened in Kazakhstan, he said he smelled cool air and a smell like a plant blooming. He also smelled the burned, slightly sweet smell of the spacecraft after it came back through the atmosphere.

When he moved away from the capsule, he thought about the size of the mission: 340 days on a 15-year-old space station. He said the ISS is a million pounds, about the size of a football field, and has an inside space some people compare to a six-bedroom house. He said the ISS uses power from the sun, was built by an international team, and was paid for by millions of taxpayers.

Kelly said people will learn new things from the research on the space station, even things they do not know yet. He compared the work of more than 450 missions there to the work of NASA computer scientists in the 1960s and 1970s. He also said the view is great.

He shared many photos online of Earth’s cities, land, oceans, and atmosphere. He said Earth is beautiful. He talked about the colors of the water near the Bahamas and the colorful lakes in the northern Himalayas. He said he would like to visit that area, but first he would need to learn what country owns the lakes. He said the atmosphere looks very thin and fragile. He said that, together with large areas of pollution, it is alarming.

He said he could see large pollution systems from space. He saw smoke clouds from wildfires over parts of the US. He also saw parts of Asia with visible pollution almost all year. He said the message “we need to save the planet” misses the point a little. He said the planet will get better, but humans might not be here if they destroy the environment. He said seeing Earth’s thin atmosphere for so long can make a person more of an environmentalist.

He said people must take care of the air they breathe and the water they drink. He said humans affect these things, and humans can change them if they decide to.

Kelly’s social media was very active, and he said it was a team effort with his girlfriend. He gained many followers, but he said he did not know about the size of his audience. Instead, he was watching the 2016 presidential election news. One of the first questions he asked after landing was, “How did Super Tuesday go?”

But he did not share his opinions about Donald Trump or Bernie Sanders. He said that as a government employee, he must follow the Hatch Act, a law that limits some political activity by federal employees.

He said work helped him stay sane in space. He set near goals, like the next crew arrival, the next spacewalk, the next robotics work, and the next science experiment. He said being back on Earth with everyone else still did not feel fully real. He said he once felt shocked to see a crowd after a past mission. He said he will probably feel that kind of culture shock soon.

A NASA scientist said Kelly showed the qualities needed for a mission to Mars. Kelly said he thinks he will not fly again with NASA. But he said he would not say he is 100% done. He mentioned private spaceflight companies like SpaceX. He said they might need someone like him someday. He also said that maybe in the next 20 years, people will be able to buy a cheap ticket and go for a short visit to space.
