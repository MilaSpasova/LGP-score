It is well known that many millennials use technology a lot. More than 8 in 10 say they sleep with a mobile phone next to their bed. Almost 2 in 3 say they text while driving. One in 5 has posted a video of themselves online. Three quarters have made a profile on a social networking site.

A 2010 report says millennials use social media more than other generations. It says 75% of millennials have made at least one social media account. Only 50% of Generation X, 30% of baby boomers, and 6% of people age 65 and older use social media. But some millennials do not use social media at all.

Celan Beausoleil, 31, Oakland, California
Celan Beausoleil is a social worker. She used Facebook on and off, mostly off. She last turned off her account in December 2015. She said it was hard to handle how much personal information other people shared, on top of her work.

She said her job is listening to people’s lives every day. Then, on social media, she saw many details of many people’s lives, even people she was not close to. This felt too much, like “too much closeness.” She said she already carries a lot from her work, and social media felt too heavy in her personal life too.

But she liked that social media can connect the world in a special way. She said this was one of the only reasons she stayed on Facebook so long. She said she could click from one friend to another, and finally reach someone on the other side of the world.

Jason Mathias, 26, Baltimore, Maryland
Jason Mathias said one day he noticed how much time he spent on social media. He thought about how small seconds add up. He wondered what he could do if he spent those seconds on other things.

Mathias works for the Baltimore City government. He had Facebook and Twitter for years. He deleted both in November 2012. After that, he quickly forgot about Facebook.

He still sees some benefits of social media. For example, when he met his girlfriend’s friends for the first time, it helped to later connect names, faces, hometowns, and jobs with photos. Social media can also make it easy to plan big events online. Now he depends on friends for party invites, and he believes he sometimes misses things.

But he enjoys having more time. During Lyft rides and free moments at work, he reads news articles and books instead of scrolling through a newsfeed. Also, because he cannot follow friends online, he has to call them. He says he has come to enjoy that.

Lauren Raskauskas, 22, Naples, Florida
Lauren Raskauskas says she is a private person. She does not like that social media can make people judge and study you. She also worries about giving away her data. She is looking for a job.

She deleted Twitter recently. She turned off Facebook two years ago because she did not like everyone knowing what she was doing. She joined Facebook later than others because her parents were strict about technology.

She can still see good sides of social media. One friend she had lost touch with moved to Naples for a month, but Raskauskas did not know until after the friend left. She said that was disappointing.

In the end, her worries about privacy were stronger than the benefits. She also saw a clear good side after a break-up. In a past break-up, when she was on Facebook, she worried about changing her profile photo and status. This time, she did not have to think about that.

Hariharan Rajagopalan, 18, Boston, Massachusetts
Hariharan Rajagopalan is a student at Boston College. He says not using social media has no downsides for him. He says he has not seen any effect so far. He said that even if classmates post about parties and events on Facebook, they also text him.

He said he was always a step behind with those kinds of things, so it never mattered much to him.

He only used social media when he had to. Before his first year of college, he made his first and only social media account. He joined Facebook to contact his future roommate and talk about plans.

Months later, he still has the account, but he says he does not use it and does not check it. The only activity is when his two sisters tag him in family photos.

He has avoided other social media accounts, but he has felt interested in Twitter. As a sports fan, he said it is where most news breaks. But he did not make an account. He said he does not need an account to read tweets.
