Emmanuel Limal dated many women, but he was tired of meeting women who did not want to start a family soon. Limal is a 43-year-old actor from France. He lived in Copenhagen for 20 years and looked for love because he hoped to have children. He started using dating websites, but he did not like what he saw.

Limal said many people tried to look very busy and interesting. They talked about travel and hobbies, but not about children. Some websites let people click “I’d like kids someday,” but their profiles made them seem too busy. Limal said that if someone goes to the gym eight times a week and travels every month, they are not putting family first.

Limal has a six-year-old daughter from an earlier relationship. He comes from a big family, because his father is one of 11 children. Limal has always wanted more children. He said it was hard to know when to say he wanted kids when meeting someone new. He called it a big dating taboo. Then he read a profile of a 38-year-old woman who said she wanted children, but felt it was “really bad to admit.” Limal thought she should not be ashamed.

Limal remortgaged his apartment to pay for a new website called Babyklar.nu, which means “baby-ready now” in English. It works like a normal dating site, but it asks people to be honest about wanting to start a family soon. People are also asked if they are OK dating someone who already has children and also wants another baby. The site does not ask how many children people want.

Limal said the response was very strong. When the site launched in June, it got 50 sign-ups an hour. He said couples have already met through the site and are now together. He expects the first Babyklar.nu baby by next summer. More men than women signed up: 53% men and 47% women. Some users said it felt good to say what they wanted, and to be honest.

The site came at a good time for Denmark, a country of five million people. A report from the Copenhagen hospital Rigshospitalet says Danes are not having enough babies. The rate is now 1.7 children per family, and that is not enough to keep Denmark’s population the same. People blame women having children “too late,” and couples living together and waiting to start families.

Limal hopes the site will give men and women another choice if they want a family but have not met the right person. He says it is not only about making babies. He wants it to be about children and love. His goal is to match people who want a family and a partner, and who will stay together. He says he is a romantic.

There are plans to bring the site to France and the UK later in 2013. For now, Danes get the benefits. Limal said Danes do not mind having children before marriage, so things can move fast. He also said Denmark is small, so someone from Jutland can date someone from Copenhagen without too much travel. Limal also said he has found love. He met a nice woman, and she wants a baby too, so they will see what happens.
