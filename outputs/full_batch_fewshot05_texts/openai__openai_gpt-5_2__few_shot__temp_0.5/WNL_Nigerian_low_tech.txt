With climate change, we often focus on the first part of the story: the problem. We forget the second part: many solutions are ready now. These solutions include faster recycling, lower emissions, and sustainable options instead of plastic, air conditioning, smartphones, and fast fashion.

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change recently met in Copenhagen to present its latest report about the effects and speed of climate change. Climate change is now measured on all continents. Our efforts to lower emissions must be stronger so it does not grow out of control. The meeting in Copenhagen also focused on solutions, not only on risks and problems.

To focus on what can be done, the Sustainia Award, chaired by Arnold Schwarzenegger, celebrated ten leading sustainability solutions used in 84 countries. The awards covered many areas, like food, fashion, energy, transportation, education, and health. They showed real alternatives to the dark future stories we often hear. They made sustainability feel real for innovators, investors, consumers, and policy makers.

From California, we saw that we can now make plastics from greenhouse gases. These plastics can compete with normal oil-based plastics in price and quality. From Switzerland, we learned how to recycle and reuse old clothes and shoes better, in a recycling system used in over 60 countries. From Canada, we learned how smartphones can make bike-sharing easier.

The ten projects each had different solutions, but the Nigerian project, Wecyclers, won Arnold Schwarzenegger's and the rest of the jury's vote. It won the Sustainia Award 2014.

Wecyclers helps low-income communities earn money from waste that piles up in their streets. It uses a fleet of cargo bicycles to collect and recycle unmanaged waste in Lagos. Families can trade garbage for consumer goods using an SMS-based point system.

Recycling companies buy Wecyclers' sorted waste and process it into products. They turn it into mattresses, pillows, and trash bags. Wecyclers answers local waste problems. It is estimated that only 40% of the city's rubbish is collected. According to the World Bank, only 46% of municipal solid waste in Africa is collected. More than 5,000 households have signed up so far, and there are plans to expand to other cities in Nigeria.

People often think climate solutions must be hi-tech and focused on cutting emissions, building infrastructure, or improving efficiency. But to solve many different problems, we also need many different kinds of solutions. Sustainability is not only about lowering emissions. It is also about using natural resources in a smarter way and creating healthier lives. Some projects may be low-tech, but they can still create big, lasting change for whole communities.

Because there are many challenges and many solutions, we should focus more on the part of the story that builds excitement, creates momentum, and pushes people to act for needed change.
