Harvey Goldsmith, a famous British rock promoter and manager, says the time of the big British summer music festival, including Glastonbury, is ending.

Goldsmith has worked with many famous stars, like the Who, the Rolling Stones, Queen, Madonna, Bob Dylan, and Luciano Pavarotti. He says the biggest problem is that there are not enough big new bands to replace the old ones.

He said the festival circuit has already reached its top. He said it peaked about two years ago. He said there are too many festivals, but not enough big acts to be the main headline acts.

Goldsmith said there were about 900 music festival events in the UK between May and September 2014. He said they cannot all continue. He thinks festivals will change. He thinks there will be more events that are not only music, but also things like poetry, books, or magic shows. He thinks there will be many small festivals with a mix of things.

Goldsmith also said the way people listen to music has changed. He said people do not want to listen to a full album as much now. He said many rock bands became famous by making several albums and improving over time. But he said young people often listen to one track, then quickly move to the next. He said pop music is everywhere, and he thinks this could change again, but it will take time.

Goldsmith, who is 69, also said he is working with Robin de Levita. De Levita is a Dutch producer of the Who’s 1970s rock musical Tommy. They are working at a new 1,100-seat theatre in Wembley. It is due to be finished in time for the first stage adaptation of The Hunger Games in June 2016.

Goldsmith said de Levita will bring his SceneAround idea to London. In this theatre, the audience seats are on a turntable that turns around different scenes. There are also projections on panel screens. This idea started in an aircraft hangar outside Amsterdam and was very successful.

Goldsmith said he is also planning to bring a show about Anne Frank to the new venue. He said it is a completely different way to produce shows.

At the Hay Festival of Literature and Arts in Powys, Wales, Goldsmith also shared stories from his long career. He talked about Keith Moon putting dynamite into a Sydney hotel room toilet to try to unblock it. He also talked about John Lennon having very strong stage fright before a show at Madison Square Garden in 1974. He said Lennon was dragged out of his dressing room, vomiting, and pushed on stage. Goldsmith said fear is common for artists, but when the music starts, they are fine.

Goldsmith also explained why Elvis Presley never performed outside North America. He said Presley’s manager, Colonel Tom Parker, told him the real reason was Parker’s uncertain immigration status. Goldsmith said Parker explained he was an illegal Dutch immigrant and did not want to risk leaving the US. Goldsmith said it was Parker, not Elvis.

Goldsmith said Freddie Mercury was the most powerful stage performer, and the best live performer they have ever had. He said at Live Aid, Mercury saw the audience and quickly took control of it.

Goldsmith said a new Queen is not close to appearing. He said they are not producing a new generation of this kind of act. He said Coldplay was probably the last one, and that was ten years ago. He said there are not many artists that look like the next generation of heritage artists.

He said that without big acts, festivals cannot have big shows. He said Glastonbury cannot find any more big acts, even though it is the top festival. He said the big festivals are really over.
