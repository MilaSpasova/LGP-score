A few months before he died, Carl Sagan recorded a hopeful message for people who want to explore Mars. He said: “Whatever the reason you’re on Mars is, I’m glad you’re there. And I wish I was with you.”

Seventeen years after Sagan wrote about his hopes for the future in 1996, a company from the Netherlands wants to make this idea real. The company is called Mars One. It plans to send four astronauts to Mars in 2023 to start a human colony. But there are serious problems.

First, on Mars the astronauts’ bodies must live with weaker gravity. Mars gravity is 38% of Earth’s gravity. People think this could change their bones, muscles, and blood flow so much that they might not be able to live on Earth again. Second, they must leave their family and friends, because there is no return ticket.

The Mars One website says a return “cannot be anticipated nor expected”. To return, they would need a rocket ready to fly from Mars, life support for up to seven months, and a way to reach a space station near Earth or land safely on Earth. The website says none of this is easy, and it needs a lot of technology, weight, and money.

Still, the company’s Medical Director, Norbert Kraft, says 10,000 people have already applied. He told The Guardian that the applicants are from age 18 to at least 62. Some are women, but most are men.

Kraft said people have different reasons for wanting to go. A 32-year-old American woman named Cynthia said she dreamed about going to Mars when she was a child. She remembered going with her mother in the early 1990s to a lecture at the University of Wisconsin.

She said the speaker was Sagan. She asked him if humans would land on Mars in her lifetime. Cynthia said he asked her if she wanted to be stuck in a “tin can spacecraft” for two years to get there. She said “yes”. He smiled and told her he truly believed humans would reach Mars in her lifetime.

Cynthia told Mars One that when she first heard about the project, she thought it was her chance. She said her childhood dream could become real. She said she could help build the first settlement on Mars and show people on Earth that there are still unknown places humans can try to reach.

Kraft said Mars One is looking for people with these qualities: resilience, adaptability, curiosity, trust, and resourcefulness. They also must be over 18.

Mars One started in 2010. Its founder is Bas Lansdorp, an engineer. Mars One says it has a realistic plan and a financing plan using existing technology, and that the mission can be done. The website says Mars already has basic things needed for life. It says water can be taken from ice in the soil. It also says Mars has sources of nitrogen, an important part of the air we breathe. The colony will use special solar panels for power.

Mars One says it signed a contract with an American company, Paragon Space Development Corporation. This is to begin work on the life support system and spacesuits for the mission.

The project is reported to cost $6bn. Lansdorp said he hopes to pay for part of it by selling broadcasting rights. He told ABC News that the broadcasting money from the London Olympics was almost enough to pay for a mission to Mars.

Another ambassador for the project is Paul Römer, who helped create Big Brother. On the website, Römer said this Mars mission could be the biggest media event in the world. He said it could be like reality TV and a talent show, with no ending, and the whole world watching.

The website says the goal is a permanent human colony. The first team would land on Mars in 2023 and start building. After that, a new team of four astronauts would come every two years.

However, some people are sceptical. They worry about how astronauts would get to Mars and build a colony with all the life support and other things they need.

Professor Gerard ‘t Hooft is an ambassador for the project. He won the Nobel Prize for theoretical physics in 1999. He says there are unknown health risks. He said the radiation is very different from anything tested on Earth.

Mars One says the mission wants to inspire people, like the Apollo Moon landings did. It wants people to believe that many things are possible.

Mars One also says a permanent settlement on Mars is not only possible, but necessary. It says this could help us learn faster about how the solar system formed, how life began, and our place in the universe.
