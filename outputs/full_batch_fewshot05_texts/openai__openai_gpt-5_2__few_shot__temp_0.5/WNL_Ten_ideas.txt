From glow-in-the-dark trees to bike sheds under the ground and bins that use solar power, here are some unusual ideas to make cities better places to live.

Living in a city has good points, but it is harder to live in a sustainable way. People deal with pollution, traffic, and fewer green spaces. Inventors have ideas to help. Here are ten of the strangest ideas.

Pop-up parks
Some cities seem built more for cars than for people. A pop-up park is a simple idea. You take an empty car park, a little money, and one or two plants, and you make a small park. The PARK(ing) project started as an art experiment in San Francisco. It has now spread around the world. People also make temporary city farms and ecology demonstrations. The movement has a day of action every September.

Underground storage
Not everyone in cities uses a car. Bikes are getting more popular. But where can you keep a bike safe? An engineering company in Tokyo called Giken made an idea called an “eco-cycle, anti-seismic underground bicycle park”. It is a round storage place that is only seven metres wide. It goes deep into the ground and can hold 204 bikes. The owner can get the bike back by pressing a button. The system brings it up in about 13 seconds.

Glow-in-the-dark trees
Many people think of Christmas lights when they think about things that glow at night. But Daan Roosegaarde thinks about glowing plants. This Dutch designer and artist created an experimental “bioluminescent” plant. The technology mixes DNA from glowing sea bacteria with part of a plant’s genes. This makes a glow like a jellyfish. Trials are happening to make an industrial-scale version. Roosegaarde hopes it could one day replace normal street lights.

Footstep power
Every day, many commuters and shoppers in West Ham in east London cross a raised walkway near the underground station. Most people do not notice that the ground feels a little springy. Even fewer know this helps power the streetlights above. The floor has smart tiles. The tiles catch energy from people’s footsteps and turn it into electricity. The UK company Pavegen made this system. It has also put a similar system in London’s Heathrow Airport and in other places around the world.

Supertrees
People have made man-made trees. Singapore’s Gardens by the Bay has a small group of them. These steel “supertrees” can be up to 50 metres tall. They have flowers and ferns growing on them. Their metal tops also help take in and spread heat. They can collect rainwater and help bring air to two “climate-controlled biomes” (large conservatories) below. Eleven of the 18 trees also have solar panels on their “branches”.
