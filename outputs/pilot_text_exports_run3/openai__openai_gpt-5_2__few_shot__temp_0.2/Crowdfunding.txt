This new light could be a great invention, like Trevor Baylis’s wind-up radio in the 1990s before the internet. It is a cheap light that uses free power from gravity. It could stop people in Africa and India from using dangerous kerosene lamps.

British designer Patrick Hunt tried to get money from banks or venture capital to start selling his invention. But it was hard. He said getting funding was slow and complex, and people did not want to take a risk.

So he used crowdfunding on a US website called Indiegogo, which had just opened in the UK. In five days, he reached his goal and raised £36,200. By the end of 2012, in 40 days, he had raised £400,000.

The LED light uses a dynamo. A 10kg bag of rocks pulls down as it falls. The bag is tied to the light and lifted about 2m high. When it falls slowly, it makes enough power for 30 minutes of light.

Hunt is getting ready to make the light in China. He will test the market again by sending 1,000 lights to Africa before full mass production. He hopes to make millions of units.

Hunt is part of a new group of entrepreneurs who use crowdfunding to get money. Another new site is InvestingZone. It connects rich people with start-up entrepreneurs.

Indiegogo does not give shares. Instead, people who donate can get “perks” at different donation levels. People who helped fund Hunt’s light could feel good about helping poorer people, and they could also get their own light.

Danae Ringelmann is a co-founder of Indiegogo. She says the “gravity light” shows how crowdfunding can work. She says it can quickly test an entrepreneur’s idea. She says it is not about knowing people in a bank or a venture capital firm. It is about showing value to customers and fans, and getting them to support and fund the idea.

She also says even ideas that do not get funding are still worth testing. Then you can learn faster and not waste time.

Ringelmann lives in the US. She started her career as a Wall Street analyst. In 2008, she quit to help friends in the arts raise money.

At first, the site focused on films. It launched at the Sundance Film Festival that year.

Five years later, the site raises about $2m each week for new businesses that are starting or growing. In December 2012, it added euro and sterling services to grow in Europe. It says Britain is its third biggest market. International activity has risen 41% since December.

There are many competitors, like Kickstarter, Seedrs, and Funding Circle. But Ringelmann says Indiegogo is different because anyone can start a campaign. No project is too strange.

Indiegogo takes a 4% fee from successful campaigns. If a campaign does not reach its target, users can choose to refund all the money with no charge, or keep the money but pay a 9% fee.

A British woman, Lauren Pears, raised £100,000 on the site to open a “cat café” in London. It is called Lady Dinah’s Cat Emporium. It has not opened yet. It is described as a place where people can sit in a comfortable chair with hot tea, a book, and a cat.

Ringelmann says venture capitalists sometimes reject ideas. They may say the market might not want the product. She says entrepreneurs can run an Indiegogo campaign, even before launching the product. The campaign can show there is a market.

She says the site lets people test the market, price, and features. It can also help find new ways to make money and get important feedback.

Ringelmann says she has helped 100,000 businesses and services raise money. She gives advice to new entrepreneurs. She says there are many ideas, but what matters is doing the work well. She says if you are afraid someone will steal your idea and do it better, then you are not the right person to do it. She says it is about confidence, moving fast, and learning.

For Ringelmann, growing in Europe and making a deal with a web payment provider are part of a dream to make finance open to more people. This will allow payments through local card services like Maestro in the UK and Carte Bleue in France, and also PayPal. More than 7,000 finance campaigns are live on the site.

Crowdfunding has grown as another choice besides banks. But it is not very attractive to big investors, because they usually want a stake in a business. This could start to change in the UK with the launch of InvestingZone.
