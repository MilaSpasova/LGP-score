The lottery balls have fallen, and all six numbers match. Now the winner can buy an Audi, book a holiday in the US, and call an estate agent. An analysis says many lottery millionaires do this.

The UK National Lottery started in 1994. Since then, it has made 3,000 millionaires. Together they have won more than £8.5bn in total. On average, each winner got £2.8m. The study authors, Oxford Economics, say the money also helped create 3,780 more millionaires among winners’ children, family, and friends.

Most winners (59%) stop working right away. But 19% keep their jobs, and 31% do unpaid volunteer work. The study says 98% of winners’ spending stayed in the UK. Because of spending on homes, cars, and holidays, each winner is estimated to support six full-time jobs for one year.

Winners added almost £750m to GDP and produced more than £500m in taxes for the Exchequer. Most money was spent on property. Winners spent £2.72bn on their main homes, and £170m to pay off debts and mortgages.

Many winners wanted to keep a steady income, so they spent £2.125bn on investments. They gave £1.17bn as gifts to family and friends. They spent £680m on cars and holidays.

Camelot, the operator of the UK National Lottery, paid for the study to mark 3,000 winners. The study used research from 100 winners who won £1m or more. It found that all 3,000 winners bought 7,958 houses or flats in the UK, about 2.7 each, and spent £3.3bn. Most winners (82%) changed their main home and spent an average of £900,000.

Many new homes had extra features. Almost a third (29%) wanted a hot tub. A walk-in wardrobe was important for 28%. Almost a quarter (24%) chose a home behind electric gates. And 22% had a games room, with 7% putting in a snooker table.

Bigger homes also need workers. About 30% hired a cleaner and 24% hired a gardener. A small number (5%) hired a beautician.

Audi was the most popular car brand for 16% of winners. Range Rover and BMW were also popular (11% each). Mercedes was 10%, and Land Rover was 5%. Winners spent £463m on 17,190 cars. The average price of their favourite car was £46,116.

Holidays were also important. Most winners (68%) chose five-star hotels abroad. The US was the top place to visit for 27%, followed by the Caribbean (9%). In the UK, caravan sales also benefited. Over the past 18 years, 10% of millionaires bought a caravan, creating sales of about £7.4m.

Some winners (15%) started their own businesses. Another 9% helped other people start businesses. And 6% invested in or bought other people’s businesses. Businesses started or supported by lottery winners employ 3,195 people.

Andy Logan, who wrote the report, said a win affects more people than they expected. It changes the lives of friends and family. He also said each win has a clear effect on the UK economy, because so much money is spent in the UK. He said each win creates a ripple effect for this generation and often the next one too.
