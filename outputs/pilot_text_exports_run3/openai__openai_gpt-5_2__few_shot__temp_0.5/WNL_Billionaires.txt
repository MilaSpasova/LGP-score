A thick layer of bird droppings covers a gold-colored railing at one of Britain’s most expensive homes. There are pigeon bones, broken mirrors, and water running through broken parts of the ceiling. This house is called The Towers. It is a £30m mansion on “Billionaires’ Row” in north London. People did not know how badly damaged it was until now.

The Towers is one of many big houses on The Bishops Avenue. It is believed that about ten mansions there have been almost empty since they were bought about 25 years ago, for members of the Saudi Arabian royal family. The columns are breaking. Swimming pools with mosaic tiles are full of rubble. Nature has taken over, and owls now live there.

This scene happens along the avenue, which Lloyds Bank says is the second most expensive street in Britain. At the same time, more people struggle to buy a home in London because prices rise by 11.2% each year. But 16 mansions on the most expensive part of The Bishops Avenue are empty. Many are behind locked gates. Their windows have steel bars, the gardens are overgrown, and guard dogs patrol the grounds.

Across the street there is another empty mansion worth £18m, with broken windows and special paint to stop people climbing. Another house has metal bars on the windows, and it was sold for £20m.

Even so, prices keep rising. Dryades, a mansion that was owned until recently by a Pakistani politician, sold for £12m in 2007 and is believed to be worth about £30m today. Heath Lodge is worth £13m today. It was the place where fashion tycoon Aristos Constantinou was murdered in 1984 by a silver bullet. The house was sold in the late 1970s for £400,000.

Seeing these empty houses can be very painful for people who cannot afford housing in London. One security guard said it was very frustrating to watch so much space fall apart. The guard said there was enough space to house dozens of people.

Long ago, rich royals with oil money from Nigeria and Saudi Arabia were some of the first to come to this road near Hampstead Heath. Iranians came after the fall of the shah. Now Chinese buyers are coming too, after Russians and Kazakhs who have spent millions to get an address that estate agents say is as famous as the Champs Elysées and Rodeo Drive. Recently, two mansions were for sale for £65m and £38m. They promised lots of Italian marble, lifts with leather padding, and luxury panic rooms.

But around these empty houses, fountains are falling apart and lawns have turned into wet boggy ground. Inside one house, water drips through a big crystal chandelier onto a thick carpet that is rotting under parts of a fallen ceiling. Moss grows through broken bricks. Broken mirror tiles lie across a bathroom. The swimming pool has about one foot of dirty salty water, and flowers grow through the tiles. Wooden parts of the sauna bend outward.

The most dramatic ruin is The Towers. It is a grand house among many acres of hornbeams, oak trees, and lime trees. Pigeons fill its large halls. Water runs through three floors, and green algae covers the walls. The water drops into a huge empty basement swimming pool. There are unopened wooden boxes marked “bullet-proof glass,” which shows the previous owners were worried about security.

Today, very few people live on The Bishops Avenue all the time. A guard outside one mansion said the owners were not there. A guard outside Royal Mansion would not say if anyone was home. Staff at another mansion only warned the Guardian about the guard dogs. Magdy Adib Ishak-Hannah, an Egyptian-born private healthcare businessman with personal wealth of £45m, said he is one of the few people who live there permanently.

He said it is not a friendly place where you can talk to neighbors. He said he has never seen what his neighbors look like. He said a Saudi princess next door spent £35m on a new house, and he has never seen her. He said about three houses are lived in 24/7. He said half the homes are used only three to six months a year, and the rest may not be used at all.

These broken mansions show a culture where the world’s richest people buy British property as investments. One Hyde Park, an apartment building in Knightsbridge, is another example. More than half the flats there are registered with the council as empty or second homes.

Still, people on the avenue talk about building £5m apartments instead of £50m mansions, to try to bring people back.

Anil Varma, a local property developer, plans to rebuild one valuable site as 20 apartments. They will have a concierge, maid service, a 25-metre pool, a spa, and a cinema.

He said that if you build a big house and try to sell it for £30m to £40m, it will not sell. He said local people will not buy, so you must find buyers from overseas.

But using these empty homes to help solve the housing crisis still seems far away. Andrew Harper, a local Conservative councillor, laughed when asked if some of the empty houses could become affordable homes. He said the land price would be too high.

He said very wealthy people own property there, and sometimes they live in it and sometimes they do not.
