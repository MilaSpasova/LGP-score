Some cities are trying unusual ideas to make city life better. These ideas include glow-in-the-dark trees, underground bike storage, and solar-powered bins. City living can be good, but it can also be hard to live in a green way. People in cities face pollution, traffic, and fewer green spaces. Inventors are trying new ways to help.

Pop-up parks
Some cities seem built more for cars than for people. A pop-up park is a simple idea. You use an empty parking space, a little money, and a few plants to make a small park. The PARK(ing) project started as an art experiment in San Francisco. Now it has spread around the world. People also make temporary city farms and ecology demonstrations. The movement has a day of action every September.

Subterranean storage
Not everyone in cities uses a car. Bicycles are becoming more popular. But people need a safe place to keep a bike. A Tokyo engineering company called Giken designed an “eco-cycle, anti-seismic underground bicycle park.” It is a round storage system that is only seven metres wide. It goes deep underground and can hold 204 bikes. Owners can get their bike by pressing a button. The system brings the bike back up in about 13 seconds.

Glow-in-the-dark trees
Some people think of Christmas decorations when they imagine glowing trees. But Dutch designer-artist Daan Roosegaarde created a “bioluminescent” plant. This experimental technology combines DNA from glowing marine bacteria with part of a plant’s genes. This makes a glow like a jellyfish. Tests are happening to try to make a large-scale version. Roosegaarde hopes it could one day replace normal street lights.

Footfall harvesting
In West Ham in east London, many commuters and shoppers walk across a raised walkway near the underground station every day. Most people do not notice that the ground feels a little springy. The rubber surface bends about five millimetres. This movement helps power the streetlights above. The walkway uses smart tiles that collect energy from footsteps and turn it into electricity. A UK company called Pavegen made this system. It also has a similar system at London’s Heathrow Airport and in other places.

Supertrees
Singapore’s Gardens by the Bay has man-made trees called “supertrees.” They are up to 50 metres tall and have steel frames. Flowers and ferns grow on them. Their metal tops help take in and spread out heat. They can also collect rainwater. They help ventilate two “climate-controlled biomes” (large conservatories) below. Eleven of the 18 trees also have solar panels on their “branches.”
