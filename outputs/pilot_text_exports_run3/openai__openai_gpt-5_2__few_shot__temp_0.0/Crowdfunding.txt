This invention could be as useful as Trevor Baylis’s wind-up radio in the 1990s. It is a cheap light that uses free power from gravity. It could help people stop using dangerous kerosene lamps in Africa and India.

British designer Patrick Hunt first tried to get money from banks or venture capital. But it was hard. He said the process was slow and complicated, and people did not want to take a risk because the idea was not proven.

So he tried crowdfunding on a US website called Indiegogo, which had just opened in the UK. In five days, he reached his goal and raised £36,200. By the end of 2012, in 40 days, he had raised £400,000.

The LED light uses a dynamo. A 10kg bag of rocks is attached to the light. You lift the weight to about 2m. When the weight slowly falls, it makes enough power for 30 minutes of light.

Hunt is getting ready to make the product in China. He will test the market by sending 1,000 lights to Africa. After that, he hopes to make millions.

He is part of a new group of entrepreneurs who use crowdfunding to raise money. Another new website is InvestingZone. It connects rich people with start-up entrepreneurs.

Indiegogo does not give shares. Instead, it lets people offer “perks” for different donation amounts. People who gave money to Hunt’s project could feel good about helping poorer people, and they also got their own light.

Danae Ringelmann, a co-founder of Indiegogo, says the “gravity light” shows how crowdfunding can work. She says it can test an entrepreneur’s idea. She says it is fast and efficient, and it is not about knowing people in a bank or a venture capital firm. It is about showing customers and fans that the idea is good, so they support it.

She also says it is good to test ideas even if they might fail. If the idea is not good, you find out sooner and learn faster.

Ringelmann is based in the US. She started as a Wall Street analyst. In 2008, she quit and used her skills to help friends in the arts raise money.

At first, the site focused on films. It launched at the Sundance Film Festival that year.

Five years later, it raises about $2m each week for new businesses. In December 2012, it started services in euros and pounds to grow in Europe. It says Britain is its third biggest market. International activity is up 41% since December.

Indiegogo has many competitors, like Kickstarter, Seedrs, and Funding Circle. But Ringelmann says Indiegogo is different because anyone can start a campaign. No project is seen as too strange.

Indiegogo takes a 4% fee from successful campaigns. If a campaign does not reach its goal, users can refund all money with no charge. Or they can keep the money raised, but pay a 9% fee.

A British woman, Lauren Pears, raised £100,000 on the site to open a “cat café” in London. It is called Lady Dinah’s Cat Emporium. It has not opened yet. It is described as a place where people can sit in a comfortable chair with hot tea, a book, and a cat.

Ringelmann says some entrepreneurs are rejected by venture capitalists. The investors may say it is a great idea, but they do not know if people really want it. Then the entrepreneurs run an Indiegogo campaign. Even the campaign alone can show there is a market.

She says crowdfunding lets you test the market, the price, and the features. It can also help you find new ways to earn money and get important feedback.

Ringelmann says ideas are common. What matters is doing the work well. She says if you are afraid someone will steal your idea and do it better and faster, then you may not be the right person to do it. She says you need confidence to move fast and learn.

Ringelmann says Indiegogo’s growth in Europe and a deal with a web payment provider support her goal to make finance more open to everyone. The site has more than 7,000 live fundraising campaigns. Payments will work with local card services like Maestro in the UK and Carte Bleue in France, as well as PayPal.

Crowdfunding has grown as another choice besides banks. But it is less attractive to very rich investors, who often want a stake in a business. This may start to change in the UK with the launch of InvestingZone.
