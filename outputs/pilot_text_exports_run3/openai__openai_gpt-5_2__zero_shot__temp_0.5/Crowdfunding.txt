This new idea could be as important as Trevor Baylis’s wind-up radio in the 1990s, before the internet. It is a cheap light that gets power from gravity. It could stop people in Africa and India from using dangerous kerosene lamps.

A British designer named Patrick Hunt wanted money to start selling his invention. He tried banks and venture capital, but it was hard. He said getting funding was slow and complicated, and people did not want to take a risk.

So he tried crowdfunding on a US website called Indiegogo, which had just opened in the UK. In five days, he reached his goal and raised £36,200. By the end of 2012, after 40 days, he had raised £400,000.

The LED light uses a dynamo. The dynamo is powered when a 10kg bag of rocks slowly falls. The bag is attached to the light and lifted about 2m high. As it falls, it makes enough power for 30 minutes of light.

Hunt is getting ready to make the light in China. He will test the market by sending 1,000 lights to Africa before making many more. He hopes to make millions of units.

Hunt is part of a new group of entrepreneurs who use crowdfunding to get money. Another new site is InvestingZone. It connects rich people with start-up entrepreneurs.

Indiegogo does not give people shares. Instead, it lets creators offer “perks” for different donation amounts. People who donated to Hunt’s light felt good about helping poorer people, and they also got their own light.

Danae Ringelmann, a co-founder of Indiegogo, said the “gravity light” shows how crowdfunding can work fairly. She said it is fast and efficient because it is not about knowing the right people at a bank or a venture capital company. It is about showing customers and fans that your idea is good, and getting them to support it with money.

She also said that even if an idea does not get funding, it is still useful to test it. Then you learn sooner if it is not a good idea.

Ringelmann lives in the US. She started her career as a Wall Street analyst. In 2008, she quit and used her skills to help friends in the arts raise money.

At first, the site focused on films. It launched at the Sundance Film Festival that year.

Five years later, the site was raising about $2m a week for new businesses that were starting or growing. In December 2012, it added euro and sterling services and said Britain was its third biggest market. International activity rose 41% since December.

There are many competitors, like Kickstarter, Seedrs, and Funding Circle. But Ringelmann said Indiegogo is different because anyone can start a campaign. No project is seen as too strange.

Indiegogo takes a 4% fee from campaigns that succeed. If a campaign does not reach its goal, users can choose to return all the money for free, or keep the money but pay a 9% fee.

A British woman named Lauren Pears raised £100,000 on the site to open a “cat café” in London. It is called Lady Dinah’s Cat Emporium. It has not opened yet, but it is described as a place where people can sit in a comfortable wingback chair, have hot tea, read a book, and spend time with a cat.

Ringelmann said venture capitalists sometimes reject ideas because they do not know if people want them. She said entrepreneurs can run an Indiegogo campaign, even before launching the project, and the campaign can show that there is a market.

She said crowdfunding lets you test what people want, test prices, test features, find new ways to earn money, and get important feedback.

Ringelmann said ideas are common, and what matters is doing the work well. She said if you worry your idea will be stolen by someone who can do it better and faster, then you are not the right person to do it. She said you need confidence to move fast and learn.

She said Indiegogo’s growth in Europe and a deal with a web payments company are part of a dream to make finance more open to everyone. Payments will work through local card services like Maestro in the UK and Carte Bleue in France, as well as PayPal. More than 7,000 finance campaigns are live on the site.

Crowdfunding has grown as another choice instead of banks. But it is not as attractive to very rich investors, who want a stake in a business. That may start to change in the UK with the launch of InvestingZone.
