It was a nice summer evening. I decided to go swimming near Doolin Pier in County Clare, Ireland. I moved there in 2012. A woman was in the water with Dusty, a dolphin. Dusty often swims with a group of people.

Dusty came to Doolin around 2008. Since then, hundreds of people have swum with her. This made people think she was very tame. She was even in an Irish tourist advertisement, where a girl said she wanted to touch a dolphin.

That evening, the woman was tickling Dusty’s belly. It looked safe and fun. About 20 tourists and local people stood on the pier and watched. Soon after I got into the water, Dusty left the woman and suddenly became very wild. Later I learned she is very territorial when she is with someone. Her tail hit the water hard. At first I thought she was just showing off, but then I realized she might be angry.

I knew I had to get out. I swam toward the pier. But very quickly, Dusty hit me with her nose. It was very strong and very painful. I was pushed forward fast.

People on the pier stared at me. Dusty stayed next to me, still hitting the water with her tail. That was the most scary thing. I thought, if she hits me with her tail, I could go under the water and die.

I reached the pier, but I could not climb out because I was hurt. I felt deep fear. I shouted for help. A man reached in and pulled me onto the steps. Then another man came. He said he was an orthopaedic surgeon and he specialized in marine trauma. He had been driving into Doolin and saw what was happening.

I was very cold and very worried. I did not know how bad my injuries were. My biggest fear was bleeding inside my body. The surgeon checked me and helped me feel calmer. He said he could not feel signs of internal bleeding, but I probably had broken bones. Later I learned I had six fractures in my spine, three broken ribs, and a damaged lung.

I stayed in the hospital for five days. I wore a back brace for several weeks. I could not work for five months. I had trouble moving, and I had stiffness and pain. Later, doctors said I had post-traumatic stress. After my near-death experience, I became anxious and reacted too strongly. I felt people were looking at me the wrong way. Loud noises were hard for me. I also had memory loss.

Three months before the accident, I opened a health-food shop. But I had to give it up because I could not work. It was the hardest year of my life, but now it is in the past. I had craniosacral therapy, osteopathy, and massage. Now I am building my own osteopathy practice. I understand patients better because I have been a patient too.

I am thankful that I am healthy. I want to stop other people from getting hurt. People often think dolphins are always kind and safe. Who thinks a dolphin will attack a person? If you see a dangerous animal coming at you with its teeth showing, you feel scared. But dolphins have a wide smile, so they look friendly.

I am not angry with Dusty. I respect her. But I was in her area, and she is a wild animal. She can be unpredictable. People need to know this. Many people come to swim with her, and they do not understand how dangerous it can be. People said my injuries were among several injuries that summer.

After the man pulled me out, Dusty swam away. Then she came back. She floated upright next to me and looked at me. We looked into each other’s eyes. I felt she was sorry. She seemed like a different dolphin, and the anger was gone. The people on the pier were amazed. After that moment, I was not so scared. I felt at peace with her.
