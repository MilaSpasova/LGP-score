The lottery balls have fallen, and all six numbers match. Many lottery millionaires then buy an Audi, book a holiday in the US, and call an estate agent. This is based on a study of how jackpot winners spend and invest.

Since it started in 1994, the lottery has made 3,000 millionaires. Together they have won more than £8.5bn, about £2.8m each on average. The study says their money also helped make 3,780 more millionaires among their children, family, and friends. The study was by Oxford Economics.

Most winners (59%) stop working straight away. But 19% keep their normal job, and 31% do unpaid voluntary work. 98% of winners’ spending stayed in the UK. Because they spend on homes, cars, and holidays, the study estimates that each winner supports six full-time jobs for one year.

The winners added almost £750m to UK GDP, and made more than £500m in tax for the Exchequer. Most of the money went to property. Winners spent £2.72bn on their main homes, and £170m to pay off debts and mortgages.

Winners also wanted to keep a steady income, so they spent £2.125bn on investments. They gave £1.17bn as gifts to family and friends. They spent £680m on cars and holidays.

Camelot, the company that runs the UK National Lottery, paid for the study to mark the 3,000th winner. The research used 100 winners who won £1m or more. It found that the 3,000 winners bought 7,958 houses or flats in the UK, about 2.7 each, spending £3.3bn. Most winners (82%) moved to a new main home, spending about £900,000 on average.

Many new homes had extra features. Almost a third (29%) wanted a hot tub. 28% wanted a walk-in wardrobe. 24% chose a home behind electric gates. 22% had a games room, and 7% installed a snooker table.

Bigger homes need care. 30% hired a cleaner and 24% hired a gardener. 5% hired a beautician.

Audi was the favourite car for 16% of winners. Range Rover and BMW were also popular (11% each), then Mercedes (10%) and Land Rover (5%). Winners spent £463m on 17,190 cars. The average price of their favourite car was £46,116.

Holidays were important too. Most winners (68%) chose five-star hotels abroad. The US was the top place to go for 27%, followed by the Caribbean (9%). Some winners also bought caravans in the UK. Over 18 years, 10% of millionaires bought a caravan, creating about £7.4m in sales.

Some winners (15%) started their own business. 9% helped other people start a business, and 6% invested in or bought other people’s businesses. Businesses started or supported by winners employ 3,195 people.

Andy Logan, the report’s author, said a win spreads further than expected. It changes the lives of friends and family, and it also affects the UK economy because so much money is spent in the UK. He said each win creates a ripple effect in this generation and often the next.
