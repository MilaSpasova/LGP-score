James Hamblin is a senior editor at the American magazine The Atlantic. In his series “If Our Bodies Could Talk,” he tried a no-showering challenge. He took fewer showers. When he did shower, he stopped using shampoo and soap.

He found something many other people have also found. When we try very hard to clean our skin with soaps and body washes, the skin tries harder to go back to balance. Showering can remove the skin’s natural oil and helpful bacteria. This can happen even more with hot water and cleaning products.

After a shower, your skin may feel tight and dry. This is because much of the skin’s natural moisture is gone. Our skin also has millions of helpful bacteria, like our gut does. Showering and scrubbing can kill these bacteria. When the bacteria grow back, the new microbes may cause more bad smell. So showering too often may make you smell more. If you stop showering and stop using soap, your skin may go through an early adjustment period that feels unpleasant. After that, the skin often returns to balance, makes less oil, and healthy bacteria grow again.

In the end, Hamblin felt that the human body can be nice when it works on its own. Dermatologists also say that using less soap can help acne and eczema.

Taking fewer showers can also help the environment. An average shower lasts seven minutes and uses 65 litres of water. That is 65 litres of clean drinking water that goes down the drain with soap, every day, sometimes more than once.

Clean water is becoming harder to ignore, as California faces another summer of drought. Clean water is one of the world’s most valuable resources, and it may soon be in short supply. There is also waste from many body wash bottles. These are strong reasons to let your body be more natural.

If this idea makes you feel uncomfortable because you remember people who smell, relax. Many people who wash less still use deodorant if they need it. Everyone’s natural smell is different, and it can change because of diet, hydration, and exercise. Also, washing hands with soap is still recommended to stop infectious diseases from spreading.

You do not need to stop showering completely, like Hamblin did. But you can reduce. Skip a few showers, use less soap, and let the skin’s bacteria grow a little.
