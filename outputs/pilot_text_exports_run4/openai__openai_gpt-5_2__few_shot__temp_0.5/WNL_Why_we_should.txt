James Hamblin is a senior editor at the American magazine The Atlantic. In his series “If Our Bodies Could Talk”, he tried a challenge: he stopped showering for a while. He also took fewer showers, and when he did shower, he did not use shampoo or soap. He wanted to see what happens when people clean their bodies too much.

He found that many people have the same experience. When we often wash with soap, body wash, and scrubbers, the skin tries harder to get back to normal. A shower can remove the skin’s natural oil and bacteria. Hot water and cleaning products can remove too much.

After a shower, your skin can feel tight and dry. This is because the skin’s natural moisture is washed away. Skin also has millions of helpful bacteria, like the gut does. Frequent rubbing and scrubbing can kill these bacteria. Later, the bacteria come back, but the new microbes may cause more body odour. So, showering too often may make you smell more.

When you stop showering and stop using soap, your skin may first go through an adjustment period. After that, the skin often becomes balanced again, oil production slows, and healthy bacteria grow.

After the experiment, Hamblin felt that the human body can be nice when it works on its own. Dermatologists also say using less soap can improve acne and eczema.

Showering less can also help the environment. The average shower lasts seven minutes and uses 65 litres of water. This is 65 litres of clean, drinkable water mixed with soap and sent down the drain every day, sometimes more than once.

Clean water is becoming more important, especially as California has another summer of drought. It is becoming clear that clean water is one of the world’s most valuable things, and it may soon be in short supply. Also, body wash bottles create more waste. These are good reasons to be more natural.

If this idea makes you feel uncomfortable, relax. Many people who wash less still use deodorant if they need it. People’s natural smell is different, and it can change because of diet, how much water they drink, and exercise. Also, washing hands with soap is still recommended to help stop infectious diseases from spreading.

You do not need to stop all showers at once like Hamblin did. But between cleaning many times a day and never showering, there is a simple idea: reduce. Skip some showers, use less soap, and let the skin’s bacteria grow.
