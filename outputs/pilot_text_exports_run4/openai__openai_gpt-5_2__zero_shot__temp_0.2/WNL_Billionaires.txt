A thick layer of bird droppings covers a gold-colored railing at one of Britain’s most expensive homes. There are pigeon skeletons, broken mirrors, and water running through broken parts of the ceiling. This house is called The Towers. It is a palace in “Billionaires’ Row” in north London. It is worth £30m. Its poor condition has been kept secret until now.

The Towers is one of ten big houses on The Bishops Avenue. People believe these houses were bought about 25 years ago for members of the Saudi Arabian royal family. Most of the houses have been almost empty since then. Their Greek-style columns are breaking. Their swimming pools with mosaic tiles are filled with broken pieces of stone and other rubbish. Nature has taken over so much that owls have moved in.

This empty and broken look can be seen along the whole street. Lloyds Bank says The Bishops Avenue is the second most expensive street in Britain. At the same time, more people in London are struggling to buy a home because house prices rise by 11.2% each year. On the most expensive part of the street, 16 mansions are empty. Many are behind locked gates. Their windows have metal bars, and the gardens are overgrown. Guard dogs patrol some areas.

Across the street is another empty mansion worth £18m, with broken windows and anti-climb paint on the walls. Another house has metal bars on the windows and has been sold for £20m.

Even so, prices keep rising. Dryades, a mansion once owned by a Pakistani politician, sold for £12m in 2007 and is believed to be worth about £30m today. Heath Lodge is worth £13m today. It was sold in the late 1970s for £400,000. Heath Lodge was the place where fashion tycoon Aristos Constantinou was murdered in 1984 with a silver bullet.

For people who struggle to afford housing, these empty houses can feel very upsetting. A security guard on the street said it was frustrating to see so much space falling apart. He said the homes are huge, with tens of thousands of square feet, enough to house dozens of people.

Rich royals from Nigeria and Saudi Arabia were some of the first people to come to this curved road near Hampstead Heath. Iranians moved here after the fall of the shah. Now, Chinese buyers are coming after Russians and Kazakhs. They spend millions to get an address that estate agents say is as famous as the Champs Elysées and Rodeo Drive. Recently, two mansions have been for sale for £65m and £38m. They promise Italian marble, lifts with leather padding, and luxury panic rooms.

But the grounds of the empty mansions are falling apart. Stone fountains are breaking, and lawns have turned into wet, muddy areas. Inside one house, water drips through a large crystal chandelier. A thick carpet is rotting under parts of a collapsed ceiling. Moss grows through broken bricks, and pieces of mirrored tile are scattered in a bathroom. A swimming pool has about a foot of dirty, salty water, and flowers grow through the tiles. Wooden slats push out from the sauna.

The worst damage is at The Towers. It is a large mansion among acres of hornbeams, oaks, and lime trees. Pigeons live in the tall halls. Water pours through three floors. The walls are bright green with algae. The water drops into a large, empty basement swimming pool. There are also unopened wooden crates marked “bullet-proof glass,” showing that the previous owners worried about security.

Today, very few people live on The Bishops Avenue all the time. A security guard outside one mansion said the owners were not there. Another guard outside Royal Mansion would not say if anyone was home. A worker at another mansion warned the Guardian about the guard dogs. Magdy Adib Ishak-Hannah, an Egyptian-born private healthcare mogul with personal wealth of £45m, said he is one of the few people who lives there permanently.

He said, “It’s not a neighbourly place, where you can chat over the fence. To be honest, I have never seen what my neighbours look like. Next door, a Saudi princess spent £35m on a new house and I’ve never seen her. There are about three houses that are lived in 24/7 and half of the properties are occupied three to six months a year. The other half, who knows if they come or not?”

These broken, very expensive houses show a culture where the world’s richest people buy British property as investments. One Hyde Park, a block of apartments in Knightsbridge, is another example. There, more than half the flats are registered with the council as empty or second homes.

Still, people on the avenue talk about a change. They talk about building £5m apartments instead of £50m mansions, to try to get more people to live there.

Anil Varma, a local property developer, plans to rebuild one of the most valuable sites on the street. He wants to make 20 apartments with a concierge, maid service, a 25-metre pool, a spa, and a cinema.

He said, “If you build a big house and try and sell for £30m to £40m, it won’t sell. Locals won’t buy and so you have to bring in overseas buyers.”

But using these empty homes to help solve the housing crisis still seems far away. Andrew Harper, a local Conservative councillor, laughed when asked if some of the empty houses could become affordable homes. He said the land price would be too high.

He said, “Very wealthy people own property there. Sometimes they live in them and sometimes they don’t.”
