James Hamblin is a senior editor at The Atlantic magazine. In his series “If Our Bodies Could Talk,” he tried not showering to see what happens when we clean our bodies too much. He took fewer showers. When he did shower, he did not use shampoo or soap.

He found something many people have noticed. When we wash our skin a lot with soaps and body washes, the skin tries hard to get back to normal. Showering can wash away the skin’s natural oils and bacteria. Hot water and cleaning products can make this effect stronger.

After a shower, your skin may feel tight and dry. This happens because natural moisture gets washed away. Our skin also has millions of helpful bacteria, like our gut does. Frequent rubbing and scrubbing can kill many of these bacteria. When the bacteria grow back, the new mix can sometimes cause more body smell. If you stop using soap and shower less, there can be an early adjustment period. After that, the skin often returns to balance, oil production slows, and healthy bacteria can grow again.

After his experiment, Hamblin felt that the human body can work well on its own. It is not only about smell or looks. Dermatologists say using soap less can help acne and eczema. Showering less can also help the environment. The average shower lasts seven minutes and uses 65 litres of water. That is 65 litres of clean, drinkable water, mixed with soap and sent down the drain every day, sometimes more than once.

Clean water is becoming more important, especially as California has another summer of drought. Clean water is one of the world’s most valuable resources, and it may soon be in short supply. There is also waste from body wash bottles. These are reasons to live more “au naturel.”

If this idea makes you feel uncomfortable, relax. Many people who shower less still use deodorant if needed. Everyone’s natural smell is different and can change with diet, hydration, and exercise. Also, washing hands with soap is still recommended to help stop infectious diseases from spreading.

You do not have to stop showering all at once like Hamblin did. Between showering many times a day and never showering, there is a middle choice. Reduce. Skip a few showers, use less soap, and let the helpful bacteria grow.
