This new light could be as important as Trevor Baylis’s wind-up radio was in the 1990s, before the internet. It is a cheap light that uses gravity to make power. It could help stop people in Africa and India from using dangerous kerosene lamps.

A British designer named Patrick Hunt wanted money to start selling his invention. He tried banks and venture capital companies, but it did not work. He said getting funding was slow and complicated, and people did not want to take a risk.

So Hunt tried crowdfunding on a US website called Indiegogo, which had just opened in the UK. In five days, he reached his goal and raised £36,200. By the end of 2012, after 40 days, he had raised £400,000.

The LED light uses a dynamo. The dynamo is powered when a 10kg bag of rocks slowly falls. The bag is attached to the light and lifted about 2m high. As it falls to the ground, it makes enough power for 30 minutes of light.

Hunt is getting ready to make the light in China. He will test the market again by sending 1,000 lights to Africa before making many more. He hopes to make millions of units.

Hunt is part of a new group of entrepreneurs who use crowdfunding to get money. Another new site is InvestingZone. It connects rich people with start-up entrepreneurs.

Indiegogo does not give people shares in a company. Instead, people can get “perks” for donating different amounts. People who helped fund Hunt’s light felt good about helping others and also received their own light.

Danae Ringelmann, a co-founder of Indiegogo, says the “gravity light” shows how crowdfunding can work well. She says it is fast and efficient because it is not about knowing the right people at a bank or venture capital firm. It is about showing your idea to customers and fans, getting them to support it, and getting them to pay for it.

She also says it can be useful to test ideas that do not get funding. Then you learn sooner that the idea was not good.

Ringelmann lives in the US and started as a Wall Street analyst. In 2008, she quit and tried to help friends in the arts raise money. Indiegogo first focused on films and launched at the Sundance Film Festival that year.

Five years later, the site was raising about $2m each week for new businesses. In December 2012, it started services in euros and pounds to grow in Europe. The company says Britain is its third biggest market. International activity has risen 41% since December.

Indiegogo has many competitors, like Kickstarter, Seedrs, and Funding Circle. But Ringelmann says Indiegogo is different because anyone can start a campaign. No idea is too strange.

Indiegogo charges a 4% fee for successful campaigns. If a campaign does not reach its target, users can choose to give all the money back with no charge. Or they can keep the money they raised, but then they pay a 9% fee.

A British woman named Lauren Pears raised £100,000 on Indiegogo to open a “cat café” in London. It is called Lady Dinah’s Cat Emporium. It has not opened yet, but it is described as a place where people can sit in a comfortable chair, have hot tea, read a book, and spend time with a cat.

Ringelmann says some ideas are rejected by venture capitalists because they do not know if people really want the product. But an Indiegogo campaign can show proof that there is interest. Even if the project is not launched, the campaign can show there is a market.

She says crowdfunding lets you test your market, pricing, and features. It also helps you get important feedback.

Ringelmann says ideas are common, but success depends on doing the work well. She says if you are afraid someone will steal your idea and do it faster, then you may not be the right person to carry it out. She says you need confidence to move fast and learn.

Ringelmann says Indiegogo’s growth in Europe and a deal with a web payment company are part of a dream to make finance more open to everyone. Payments will be possible through local card services like Maestro in the UK and Carte Bleue in France, and also through PayPal. More than 7,000 finance campaigns are live on the site.

Crowdfunding as another choice instead of banks has grown. But it is not as interesting to big investors, who want a stake in a business. This may start to change in the UK with the launch of InvestingZone.
